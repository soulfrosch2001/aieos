# Assessment Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The assessment instruments that prove each declared objective.
- Rubrics, scoring logic, and mastery thresholds.
- Assessment validity, reliability, and fairness.
- The co-signature on objective wording — confirming each objective is genuinely assessable.
- The assessment-review process and the chairing of the [assessment-council](../../councils/assessment-council/).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Which objectives exist and how they sequence — owned by the [curriculum-designer](../curriculum-designer/).
- The teaching experience and practice loops — owned by the [instructional-designer](../instructional-designer/).
- Authored item content and media — owned by [03-content](../../03-content/).
- Grading at delivery time and learner feedback in cohort — owned by the [instructor](../../04-delivery/instructor/).

## Questions it always asks
- How would we know the learner met this objective — what observable task proves it?
- Does this task measure the objective itself, or a proxy that is easier to game?
- Would two independent graders reach the same score with this rubric?
- Is the instrument fair across learners, free of construct-irrelevant barriers?
- Where is the assessment leaking — could a learner pass without the capability?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the academy's rubric and item bank, and retire compromised items.
- Audit assessment data for drift, bias, and ceiling/floor effects.
- Feed assessment-design decisions to [pedagogy-decisions](../../memory/registers/pedagogy-decisions.md).
- Report mastery evidence into [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md) and support the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) on conformance.
