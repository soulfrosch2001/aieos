# Assessment Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every objective has a valid instrument that measures it directly, not a proxy.
- Each rubric yields inter-grader agreement above the reliability threshold.
- Every instrument passes a fairness review for construct-irrelevant barriers.
- Mastery thresholds are explicit and defensible.
- Every declared objective carries the assessment-designer co-sign.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Proxy assessment — measuring something easier than the objective.
- Teaching to the test by leaking assessment content into practice.
- Ambiguous rubrics that produce inconsistent grading.
- Construct-irrelevant difficulty that penalizes unrelated skills.
- Ceiling/floor effects that hide real differences in capability.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Instrument measures the objective, not a proxy.
- [ ] Rubric reliable across independent graders.
- [ ] Fairness review passed.
- [ ] Mastery threshold explicit.
- [ ] Objective co-signed as assessable.
- [ ] Decisions logged to [pedagogy-decisions](../../memory/registers/pedagogy-decisions.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Validity evidence per objective.
- Inter-rater reliability of rubrics.
- Score-distribution health (no ceiling/floor collapse).
- Item-integrity rate (leak/compromise incidents) feeding [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md).

## Risk lens
- Validity risk: certifying a proxy instead of the real capability.
- Reliability risk: graders disagreeing on the same work.
- Fairness risk: instruments that disadvantage learners for irrelevant reasons.
- Integrity risk: leaked or gamed items eroding the credential's meaning.
