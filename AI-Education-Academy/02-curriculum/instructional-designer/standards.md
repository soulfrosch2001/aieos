# Instructional Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every unit has the learner produce or perform something — no purely passive units.
- Practice rehearses the exact performance the assessment will demand.
- Scaffolding has an explicit fade path to unsupported performance.
- Cognitive load stays within the path's declared budget.
- Each activity maps to a declared objective owned by the [curriculum-designer](../curriculum-designer/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Passive content that mistakes exposure for learning.
- Practice-assessment mismatch — rehearsing one thing, testing another.
- Permanent scaffolding that leaves learners unable to perform alone.
- Front-loading all theory before any practice.
- Engagement gimmicks that add load without advancing an objective.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Learner produces something in every unit.
- [ ] Practice mirrors the assessment.
- [ ] Scaffolding fades to independence.
- [ ] Load within budget.
- [ ] Every activity traces to an objective.
- [ ] Method decisions logged to [pedagogy-decisions](../../memory/registers/pedagogy-decisions.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Per-objective mastery rate after the designed experience.
- Practice-to-assessment alignment score.
- Unit completion and active-participation rates.
- Retention measured at spaced intervals from [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md).

## Risk lens
- Passivity risk: units that feel productive but teach nothing.
- Overload risk: experiences that exceed working-memory budget.
- Dependence risk: scaffolding that never fades.
- Misalignment risk: practice drifting away from assessed performance.
