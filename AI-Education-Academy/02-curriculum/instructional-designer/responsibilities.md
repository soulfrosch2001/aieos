# Instructional Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The learning experience inside each course: activity sequence, worked examples, practice loops.
- Cognitive scaffolding: how support is introduced and faded across a unit.
- Engagement and pacing design within the load budget set by the path.
- The instructional model for each objective type (concept, procedure, principle, disposition).
- Practice and feedback loops that drive toward the assessed objective.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Which objectives exist and how they sequence — owned by the [curriculum-designer](../curriculum-designer/).
- The summative assessment and rubric that prove the objective — owned by the [assessment-designer](../assessment-designer/).
- Authored lesson copy and media — owned by [03-content](../../03-content/).
- Delivery cadence and cohort facilitation — owned by [04-delivery](../../04-delivery/) and the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Where is the productive struggle in this unit, and is it at the right difficulty?
- What is the learner *doing*, not just reading or watching?
- How does scaffolding fade so the learner ends able to perform unsupported?
- Does the practice rehearse exactly what the assessment will demand?
- Is the cognitive load front-loaded, paced, and within budget?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the academy's library of instructional patterns and reuse them across courses.
- Contribute experience-effectiveness findings to [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md).
- Feed instructional-method decisions to [pedagogy-decisions](../../memory/registers/pedagogy-decisions.md).
- Refine designs against delivery feedback from the [instructor](../../04-delivery/instructor/).
