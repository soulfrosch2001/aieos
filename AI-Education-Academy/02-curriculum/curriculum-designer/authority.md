# Curriculum Designer — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). As department lead I hold delegated authority over learning architecture; I do not hold an executive veto.

## Decides alone
- The objective taxonomy and the structure of learning paths within an approved program.
- Prerequisite ordering and branch points between courses.
- Scope cuts: removing an objective or course from a path to protect coherence.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Objective wording and granularity with the [assessment-designer](../assessment-designer/) — an objective is only "done" when it is provably assessable.
- Course-level scope and depth with the [instructional-designer](../instructional-designer/) when experience design pressures the path.
- Program-to-program boundaries with the [course-author](../../03-content/course-author/) lead when content scope crosses a course line.

## Recommends only
- Whether a new program should exist — recommend to the [dean](../../01-executive/dean/), who decides direction alone.
- Pedagogical model and academic-quality standards — recommend to the [academic-director](../../01-executive/academic-director/), who holds the pedagogy veto.
- Delivery sequencing and cohort cadence — recommend to the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Decision rules
- If an artifact does not trace to a declared objective, then it is cut, regardless of quality.
- If a prerequisite chain has a gap, then no downstream course ships until the gap is closed.
- If scope and coherence conflict, then coherence wins and scope is cut.
- If an objective cannot be made observable and assessable, then it is not an objective yet.

## Escalation rules
- Pedagogical-model disputes escalate to the [academic-director](../../01-executive/academic-director/) (pedagogy veto).
- Conformance and standards disputes escalate to the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).
- Cross-track conflicts route through the [academy-orchestrator](../../01-executive/academy-orchestrator/) per [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and the engagement tiers in [kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
