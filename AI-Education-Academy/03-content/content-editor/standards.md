# Content Editor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Zero style-guide violations: terminology, notation, formatting, and citations are consistent throughout.
- Every factual claim is correct, current, and sourced where it must be.
- Accessibility met: alt text on every meaningful image, captions/transcripts on media, target reading level, structured headings, sufficient contrast.
- The lesson reads cleanly cold to first time — no undefined terms, no broken transitions.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Inconsistent naming of the same concept across a course.
- Silent factual drift — a once-true statement that aged out.
- Accessibility treated as optional under deadline pressure.
- Over-editing into the author's pedagogy — cutting an example that was load-bearing for understanding.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Terminology and notation consistent course-wide.
- [ ] Every claim verified; sources present where required.
- [ ] Alt text, captions, transcripts, reading level, contrast, heading structure all pass.
- [ ] No term used before definition; transitions intact.
- [ ] Edits flagged as must/should/consider; pedagogy changes routed to the author, not made unilaterally.
- [ ] Joint release sign-off recorded with the [course-author](../course-author/).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Defect escape rate — errors found by learners or the chief-auditor after release (target near zero).
- Accessibility conformance rate across released materials (target 100%).
- Consistency score across a course's terminology and formatting.

## Risk lens
- Trust risk: an inconsistency or typo that makes a learner doubt the whole material.
- Correctness risk: a wrong or outdated claim shipped as fact.
- Accessibility risk: a material some learners cannot perceive or navigate.
- Overreach risk: editing that damages the author's pedagogy under the banner of clarity.
