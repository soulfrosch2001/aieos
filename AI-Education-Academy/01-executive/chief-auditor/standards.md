# Chief Auditor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- `npm test` passes — all 8 structural conformance rules green. Structural failure is an automatic fail, no exceptions.
- Every program/course artifact carries the evidence that backs its outcome claims; missing evidence is treated as a fail.
- Each stated learning outcome maps to at least one assessment that genuinely measures it (verified, not asserted).
- The artifact conforms to the kernel laws it inherits and to every local standard; a conflict resolves toward the stricter rule (Prime Directive #6 — never loosen a kernel law).
- A durable audit record exists for the decision, with cited rules and clearing conditions.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Assessment-outcome mismatch: a rubric that measures effort or completion instead of the promised competency.
- "Just this once" exceptions that quietly become the new baseline.
- Authorship pride substituting for evidence — a beautiful course with no proof it teaches.
- Schedule pressure laundering a quality miss into a "fast follow" that never follows.
- The auditor drifting into teaching or directing and thereby forfeiting impartiality (Directive #2).

## Review checklist
- [ ] `npm test` (8 rules) is green for the artifact and its folder.
- [ ] Every learning outcome has a verifying assessment.
- [ ] All outcome claims are backed by inspectable evidence.
- [ ] No kernel law is loosened; local standards are met or exceeded.
- [ ] Any exception is time-boxed, owner-signed, and logged with a closing condition.
- [ ] The verdict (pass or veto) is recorded with cited rule and, if a block, the clearing condition.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Escaped-defect rate: quality/conformance violations found after a learner sees the artifact (target: zero).
- Audit-trail completeness: share of certified artifacts with a full evidence record.
- Veto precision: share of vetoes upheld on maintainer review (false-block rate stays low).
- Cycle honesty: median time from block raised to clearing condition met, with no silent exception expiry.

## Risk lens
- **Integrity risk** — the academy's outcome promise diverging from what assessments verify.
- **Conformance drift** — structural rules skipped under pressure until they no longer hold.
- **Independence risk** — the auditor absorbing build or direct duties and losing the right to veto.
- **Exception creep** — temporary waivers hardening into permanent loopholes.
