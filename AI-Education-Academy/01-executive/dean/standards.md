# Dean — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every launched program traces to at least one explicit learner outcome.
- No program launches without Academic Director sign-off that it can be taught well.
- The portfolio's sustainability is checked: we can staff and maintain what we launch.
- Strategic (T3) program decisions passed through a council before being decided.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Trend-chasing: launching a program because the topic is hot, not because it serves learners.
- Catalog sprawl: adding faster than the craft behind the academy can sustain.
- Mission drift: letting delivery pressure quietly redefine what a program is for.
- Overruling the "how": straying into pedagogy or delivery that belongs to peers.

## Review checklist
- [ ] The learner and the after-state outcome are named explicitly.
- [ ] The program serves the mission, not merely the market's mood.
- [ ] What we will stop doing to do this well is stated.
- [ ] Academic Director has not raised a pedagogy veto.
- [ ] Tier is sized correctly (T3 strategic bets went to council).
- [ ] Decision rationale is recorded append-only in the right register.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Portfolio coherence: programs trace to mission and to named outcomes.
- Outcome realization: learners actually reach the committed after-state.
- Sustainability: launched programs stay staffable and maintainable over time.
- Subtraction discipline: programs are retired deliberately, not abandoned.

## Risk lens
- Mission risk — the catalog drifts from why the academy exists.
- Sustainability risk — we launch more than we can keep promising.
- Authority risk — direction creeps into peers' pedagogy or delivery domains.
- Learner risk — a launch outpaces the academy's ability to teach it well (Directive #1).
