# Academic Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Prerequisites for every lesson are explicit and guaranteed by sequencing.
- Each assessment demonstrably measures the outcome it claims to measure.
- Cognitive load per lesson is bounded — no stacking of multiple hard new ideas.
- The lesson coheres with what precedes and follows it in the learning path.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Coverage thinking: optimizing for topics listed rather than outcomes reached.
- Prerequisite gaps: assuming knowledge the learner was never given.
- Assessment-outcome mismatch: testing recall while claiming to test capability.
- Local excellence, global incoherence: great lessons in a broken sequence.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Prerequisites named and guaranteed by the sequence.
- [ ] Assessment maps one-to-one to the stated learner outcome.
- [ ] Cognitive load is survivable for the intended learner.
- [ ] Lesson coheres with neighbors in the path.
- [ ] Significant work was discussed in council before building (Directive #3).
- [ ] Any veto issued carries its reason and a path to clear it.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Pedagogical coherence: the catalog's prerequisite graph is sound and acyclic.
- Assessment validity: assessments measure stated outcomes, not proxies.
- Veto precision: vetoes are rare, justified, and cleared cleanly.
- Outcome integrity: committed outcomes remain honestly teachable over time.

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk — accumulated small drifts break learning paths.
- Validity risk — assessments stop measuring what they claim.
- Overload risk — lessons exceed survivable cognitive load.
- Authority-collision risk — pedagogy veto and audit veto get conflated; they stay distinct.
