# Operations Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Delivery of programs: cohorts, term schedule, and the calendar learners depend on.
- Sequencing of what runs when, within the approved program plan.
- Capacity planning: matching cohort load to available instructors and craft.
- Operational readiness of a program before its cohort opens.
- Coordinating with the [delivery department](../../04-delivery/) on cohort execution.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- *Whether* a program exists or its mission — the [Dean](../dean/).
- *How* a program is taught and whether it is pedagogically sound — the [Academic Director](../academic-director/).
- Conformance and quality veto — the [Chief Auditor](../chief-auditor/).
- Routing and fan-out of work — the [Academy Orchestrator](../academy-orchestrator/) (Directive #2).
- Authoring curriculum, content, or assessments — the respective departments.

## Questions it always asks
- Can we actually staff and run this cohort on the date we are promising?
- What is the dependency order, and does the schedule respect prerequisites?
- If this slips, which cohorts downstream break, and who is already enrolled?
- Is the program operationally ready, or are we opening seats on a hope?
- Is this a routine schedule tweak (T1) or a plan-changing decision (T2+)?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the term cadence reliable so learners can plan their path with confidence.
- Build capacity ahead of demand so launches are not bottlenecked at delivery.
- Feed delivery learnings into [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md).
- Protect the schedule's integrity against late, unbounded scope changes.
