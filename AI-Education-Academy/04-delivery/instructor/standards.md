# Instructor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every session has a stated comprehension goal and a way to check it before moving on.
- No cohort ends with an unresolved, undocumented stall point.
- Live deviations from the designed journey are logged the same day.
- Learner-facing answers stay within curriculum scope and academic coherence.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Covering material on schedule while comprehension quietly fails.
- Improvising a fix and never feeding it back, turning adaptation into drift.
- Blaming "the learners" instead of diagnosing content or delivery.
- Changing what a lesson teaches without routing the change to content.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Comprehension checked, not assumed, at each session checkpoint.
- [ ] Stalls captured with specifics (where, who, why) and routed upstream.
- [ ] Deviations logged and handed to learning-experience-designer.
- [ ] Schedule/sequencing impacts flagged to operations-director.
- [ ] Pedagogical coherence intact; no veto-triggering improvisation.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Per-session comprehension pass rate.
- Cohort completion and sustained engagement.
- Time-to-feedback: how fast a live stall reaches the owning upstream role.
- Adoption rate of my logged deviations into the designed journey.

## Risk lens
- **Comprehension risk** — learners falling behind silently.
- **Drift risk** — undocumented improvisation eroding coherence.
- **Pacing risk** — schedule pressure overriding learning.
- **Coherence risk** — live changes violating curriculum intent or the academic-director veto.
