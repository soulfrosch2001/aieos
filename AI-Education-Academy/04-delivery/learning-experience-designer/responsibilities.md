# Learning Experience Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The end-to-end learner journey: onboarding, sequencing of effort, practice/feedback rhythm.
- The learning environment learners work inside during a delivered course.
- Feedback instrumentation — what we measure, where, and how the signal reaches owners.
- The friction map: a maintained model of where learners struggle by design vs. by accident.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Live facilitation and in-session adaptation — owned by [instructor](../instructor/).
- Learning outcomes and curriculum scope — owned by [curriculum-designer](../../02-curriculum/curriculum-designer/).
- Authored content and media — owned by [03-content](../../03-content/).
- Schedule, cohort capacity, and sequencing of runs — owned by [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Where does this journey create friction, and is that friction productive or accidental?
- Can we measure whether this is working, or are we guessing?
- Does the experience match the curriculum's intended outcomes?
- Which instructor deviations are signaling a design flaw I must close?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Evolve a reusable library of journey patterns that deliver consistently across cohorts.
- Convert recurring live deviations into durable design, feeding [learning-outcomes](../../memory/registers/learning-outcomes.md).
- Reduce accidental friction over time while protecting productive struggle.
- Raise experience-level improvement candidates into [course-ideas](../../memory/registers/course-ideas.md).
