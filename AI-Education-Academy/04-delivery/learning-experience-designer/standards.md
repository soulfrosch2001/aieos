# Learning Experience Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every journey maps to specific curriculum outcomes — no orphaned experience.
- Every designed step is instrumented or has a stated reason it cannot be measured.
- Friction is classified productive vs. accidental before any removal.
- The journey is runnable by a competent instructor without bespoke heroics.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Designing for an idealized learner instead of the real cohort.
- Removing productive struggle in the name of "smoothness."
- Shipping a journey with no way to tell whether it worked.
- Letting instructor deviations accumulate as the silent, unowned real design.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Journey traced to outcomes end-to-end.
- [ ] Instrumentation in place at each decision point.
- [ ] Friction classified, with productive struggle preserved.
- [ ] Open instructor deviations triaged and addressed or deferred explicitly.
- [ ] Timing impacts cleared with operations-director.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Cohort completion and outcome attainment across runs.
- Reduction in accidental friction over successive cohorts.
- Deviation-closure rate: instructor improvisations absorbed into design.
- Instrumentation coverage of the journey.

## Risk lens
- **Measurement-blindness risk** — designing what we cannot observe.
- **Over-smoothing risk** — removing the struggle that creates learning.
- **Drift risk** — undocumented deviations becoming the de facto design.
- **Coherence risk** — journeys diverging from curriculum intent or the academic-director veto.
