# Assessment Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Every assessment is challenged on validity: does it measure the claimed outcome, and could a learner pass without having learned? Dissent is recorded, never suppressed and captured verbatim. The chair presses for evidence over intuition.

## Decision process
1. Sponsor presents the assessment and the outcome it claims to measure.
2. Core members test alignment, validity, fairness, and live-delivery feasibility.
3. Risks (false pass, false fail, gameability), alternatives, and any debt are logged.
4. The council seeks consensus; absent consensus, the chair ([assessment-designer](../../02-curriculum/assessment-designer/)) decides.
5. Outcome ownership is respected: if alignment requires changing an outcome, the council recommends to the [curriculum-council](../curriculum-council/) rather than redefining it.
6. Decision, rationale, and dissent are written to output.

## Approval gate by tier
- **Approves alone:** T2–T3 assessment and alignment decisions.
- **Must escalate:** T4 or any decision implying an outcome change escalates via [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) to the [academic-director](../../01-executive/academic-director/) (pedagogy veto).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot resolve escalate to the [academic-director](../../01-executive/academic-director/); conformance objections route to the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).
