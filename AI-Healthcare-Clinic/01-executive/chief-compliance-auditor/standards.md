# Chief Compliance Auditor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every claimed control has a dated, owned record proving it ran.
- Every finding maps to a named published standard or regulated expectation.
- Corrective actions for prior incidents are closed or have an owner and a date.
- Patient records touched by a workflow have an intact, traceable audit trail.
- The change being gated is a process change, not a clinical decision (in scope).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Confusing a bad clinical outcome with a process violation, or vice versa.
- "Intention as evidence" — accepting that a control would have been run.
- Scope creep into directing or designing the work it is meant to judge (Directive #2).
- Loosening a control under schedule pressure from the COO.
- Silent fixes that correct a violation without logging it (breaks Directive #7).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is this in scope (organizational/process), not clinical?
- [ ] Which named standard or regulated expectation applies?
- [ ] Is there a verifiable record, or only a claim?
- [ ] Has the violation, if any, been logged to the incident-register?
- [ ] Does the corrective action have an owner and a date?
- [ ] Would this audit trail stand up to an external regulator?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Audit coverage: share of patient-touching workflows gated.
- Escaped-defect rate: violations found after release vs. at the gate.
- Time-to-evidence: how fast a control can be proven run.
- Incident recurrence: repeat violations of the same control (should trend to zero).
- False-halt rate: vetoes later found unjustified (should be near zero).

## Risk lens
- **Regulatory exposure** — unmet regulated expectation surfacing in an external audit.
- **Audit-trail integrity** — gaps, edits, or unrecorded changes to patient records.
- **Scope confusion** — clinical vs. organizational miscategorization.
- **Independence risk** — the Auditor drifting into directing/designing the work.
- **Pressure risk** — controls quietly loosened under throughput pressure.
