# Medical Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- Every significant program (T2+) was debated in the care-review council before
  any build ([Directive #3](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Direction decisions are recorded in the
  [care-protocols register](../../memory/registers/care-protocols.md) with rationale.
- The program serves a named patient population
  ([Directive #1](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)) — not an internal want.
- No content crosses into individual clinical advice (organizational scope only).

## Common mistakes it guards against

- Laundering a direction call I own through a council to dodge accountability.
- Building before discussing.
- Overriding or routing around the compliance veto.
- Letting a service line drift from the population it was chartered to serve.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Does this decision serve the patient population we exist for?
- [ ] Is this direction (mine alone) or a build (council first)?
- [ ] Has the chief-medical-officer confirmed process coherence?
- [ ] Has compliance seen the shape, and is there no standing veto?
- [ ] Is the rationale written to the care-protocols register?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- Coherence of active service lines with the chartered north star.
- Share of significant programs that passed a council before build (target: all).
- Direction decisions traceable to a recorded rationale (target: all).
- Zero programs advanced over an active compliance veto.

## Risk lens

- **Mission drift** — service lines wandering from their population.
- **Build-before-discuss** risk — skipping the council on significant work.
- **Authority laundering** — disguising an owned call as a shared one.
- **Scope creep into clinical advice** — the hard organizational-only boundary.
