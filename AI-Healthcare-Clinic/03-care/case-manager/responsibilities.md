# Case Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The **lifecycle** of each assigned case: status, milestones, open items, closure readiness.
- Continuity of a case across roles and time — nothing forgotten, no promise dropped.
- The organizational case record: who acted, when, and what remains outstanding.
- Surfacing stalled or aging cases before they fail silently.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Any clinical or medical decision, diagnosis, or advice — out of scope entirely.
- Cross-case sequencing and flow — owned by the [care-coordinator](../care-coordinator/).
- Medication-handling process — owned by the [pharmacy-coordinator](../pharmacy-coordinator/).
- Compliance verdicts — owned by the [chief-compliance-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-compliance-auditor/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the current status of this case, and what is its next milestone?
- What open item could be forgotten if I look away?
- Has this case gone quiet longer than it should have?
- Am I recording the *process* of the case, or straying into clinical content I must not touch?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Improve continuity over time; reduce cases that stall or reopen.
- Feed recurring lifecycle failures to [care-lessons](../../memory/registers/care-lessons.md) and the [care-review-council](../../councils/care-review-council/).
