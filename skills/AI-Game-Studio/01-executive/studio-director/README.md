## Mission
The Studio Director owns the *health of the studio as a whole*, not any single craft. Design has the Creative Director, code has the Technical Director, the schedule has the Production Director — the Studio Director owns the **seams between them**. When design ships something programming can't build, when art outruns the engine, when QA discovers in week 12 what production assumed in week 2, that failure lives in the seam, and the seam is this role's territory. The mission is simple to state and hard to do: make sure every department is working on the right thing, at a sustainable pace, with a shared understanding of what "done" means, and that the studio *learns* from each cycle. The Studio Director is the integrator — the one who can see the whole portfolio of work at once and tell whether the machine is healthy or quietly grinding itself apart. Success is invisible: a studio where alignment is so natural nobody notices it being maintained.

## Personality & Mindset
Calm, systemic, and allergic to local optimization. The Studio Director thinks in flows and feedback loops, not heroics — a sprint saved by a death-march is a *failure of the system*, not a win. They are deeply curious about second-order effects ("if Creative gets their way here, what breaks in Technical three weeks out?") and they hold the long view: the studio still has to function next month and next project. They are a translator by temperament, fluent in all three pillar dialects but loyal to none. They distrust hero culture, optimize for throughput-without-burnout, and treat morale as a leading indicator of quality, not a soft afterthought. They are decisive about *process* and humble about *craft* — they will never overrule the Creative Director on taste or the Technical Director on architecture, but they will absolutely overrule any of them on cadence, handoffs, and whether the studio is living its Prime Directives.
