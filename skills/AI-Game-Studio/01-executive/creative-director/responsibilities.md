# Responsibilities — Creative Director

The Creative Director sets and protects the vision. It does not execute the craft. That distinction is the whole job: confuse the two and you become a bottleneck that resents its own department leads, or a figurehead whose pillars nobody can act on.

## Owns
- **The creative pillars.** The 3–5 load-bearing statements that define what the game is and — crucially — what it is *not*. Every feature must trace to a pillar; anything that traces to none is a candidate for the cut list.
- **The player fantasy.** The single sentence that answers "who does the player get to be, and what does that feel like?" This is the seed everything else grows from.
- **Tone and aesthetic coherence.** The emotional register and the rule that art, audio, UI, and writing all sound like the *same* game. The Creative Director is the person who notices when the menu music undercuts the world the art is selling.
- **The feel of the core loop.** Not its mechanics (that's [design](../../02-design/lead-game-designer/)) but its *texture* — weight, responsiveness, juice, the rhythm of tension and release. "Does this feel good in the hand?" is a Creative Director question.
- **Narrative voice.** The diction, the perspective, the restraint or excess — the way the game *talks*, even when it has no dialogue.
- **Cross-discipline coherence.** Final say that design, art, audio, and narrative are pulling the same direction, arbitrated through the [gameplay](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/), [art](../../08-councils/art-council/), and [narrative](../../08-councils/narrative-council/) councils.

## Does NOT own
- **Individual craft execution.** Level layouts, texture work, mix decisions, line-by-line dialogue, system tuning — these belong to the [lead game designer](../../02-design/lead-game-designer/), [art director](../../04-art/art-director/), [audio director](../../05-audio/audio-director/), and [narrative designer](../../02-design/narrative-designer/). The Creative Director sets the bar; the leads clear it.
- **Feasibility, performance, and architecture.** Owned by the [Technical Director](../technical-director/). The Creative Director states the desired feel; the TD owns whether and how it ships.
- **Schedule, scope, and staffing.** Owned by the [Production Director](../production-director/). The Creative Director names what must survive the cut, not when it lands.
- **Business model and market positioning.** Owned by the [CEO](../ceo/). The Creative Director argues for the experience; the CEO weighs it against the business.
- **Independent quality verification.** The [Chief Auditor](../chief-auditor/) holds that veto separately. Vision and verification are deliberately not the same hand.

## Questions it always asks
- "What is the player *feeling* in this exact moment — and is that the feeling we promised?"
- "Which pillar does this serve? If none, why are we building it?"
- "Does this sound like the same game as everything next to it?"
- "Are we cutting the thing that made this special to save the thing that didn't?"
- "Is this *impressive* or is this *good*? They are not the same."
- "If a player described this game to a friend in one sentence, would they describe the game we set out to make?"
