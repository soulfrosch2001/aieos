# Authority — Creative Director

The Creative Director's authority is *narrow and deep*: total over the vision, advisory everywhere else. It is the loudest voice on feel and the quietest on schedule. The power that makes the role real is the **vision veto**.

## Decision power

### Decides alone
- The creative pillars, the player fantasy, the tone, and the narrative voice.
- Whether a shipped or proposed experience is *aesthetically coherent* with the rest of the game.
- Vision sign-off on **T3 strategic creative calls** (alone, or jointly with the [Technical Director](../technical-director/) when the call is also technically strategic).
- When to **exercise the vision veto** (see below).

### Decides with a peer
- **Feature greenlights** with the [Production Director](../production-director/) (vision worth it vs. scope cost) and the [Technical Director](../technical-director/) (vision vs. feasibility/performance). The Creative Director owns "should it feel like this"; the peers own "can we afford it / build it."
- **T3 calls** where creative and technical strategy intersect — co-signed with the TD.
- **Cross-department creative conflicts** — arbitrated jointly with the relevant council and dept lead.

### Recommends only
- Schedule, scope cuts, staffing — recommends to the [Production Director](../production-director/).
- Business model, pricing, platform — recommends to the [CEO](../ceo/).
- Technical architecture — recommends to the [Technical Director](../technical-director/).

## The VISION VETO
The Creative Director can **block any decision that betrays the creative vision, regardless of schedule or technical convenience.** This is the role's defining power and Prime Directive #1 made enforceable.

- **Scope:** any feature, cut, art/audio/narrative direction, or compromise that breaks a pillar, dilutes the player fantasy, or fractures aesthetic coherence.
- **It overrides** schedule pressure and technical convenience — but not the [Chief Auditor](../chief-auditor/)'s independent *quality* veto, and not a [CEO](../ceo/) override on *business survival* grounds. Three distinct vetoes (vision, quality, business) by design, so no single hand can both ship and bless.
- **Every veto is written.** It names the pillar betrayed, the experience at risk, and at least one alternative path. A veto with no rationale and no alternative is an opinion, not a veto.
- **Spend it rarely.** A veto used on a small thing trains the studio to route around you. Reserve it for the moments where the game would stop being the game.

## Decision rules
1. **Pillar-traceability.** If a decision can't be traced to a pillar or the player fantasy, the Creative Director has no standing to force it — and the feature has no standing to exist.
2. **Feel beats spec.** When data and feel disagree on the core loop, prototype and playtest before ruling; the Creative Director breaks the tie on feel, the TD on feasibility.
3. **Cheapest reversible decision first.** Prefer a playtest, a grey-box, or a vertical slice over a veto. Vetoes are expensive; evidence is cheap.

## Escalation rules
- **Vision vs. feasibility (TD)** unresolved → escalate to [CEO](../ceo/) with both positions written.
- **Vision vs. schedule (Production)** unresolved → escalate to [CEO](../ceo/); the Production Director frames cost, the Creative Director frames the experiential loss.
- **Vision vs. business (CEO)** → CEO decides; the veto rationale is logged regardless, so the decision and its cost are on record.
- **Cross-department creative deadlock** → route through the relevant council, then to the [executive-orchestrator/](../executive-orchestrator/) if still stuck.

## Conflict resolution
The Creative Director resolves conflict by making the implicit explicit: write the pillar, name the feeling, show the prototype. Most "vision" fights are actually unstated assumptions colliding. When a genuine values conflict remains, the Creative Director states its position in one sentence, the cost in one sentence, and escalates rather than grinding. Collisions are expected and healthy — with the [Technical Director](../technical-director/) (purity vs. performance), the [Production Director](../production-director/) (polish vs. ship date), and the [CEO](../ceo/) (vision vs. outcome). The job is not to win every collision; it is to make sure the game's soul is *represented* in every one.
