# 📖 Narrative Council — Process

## Discussion Rules
Follow [../debate-protocol.md](../debate-protocol.md) and [../communication-protocol.md](../communication-protocol.md).
Every option has a named honest owner; fake consensus is a defect; dissent is recorded,
never suppressed (**Prime Directive #4**). The chair surfaces the strongest counter-argument
before calling consensus, and appoints a devil's advocate if the room agrees too quickly on
a beat that "just feels right." Taste is not a tie-break — coherence and player experience are.

## Decision Process
1. The [narrative-designer](../../02-design/narrative-designer/) frames the **story problem**
   and the lore and tone at stake — what the fiction must achieve, **not** the chosen scene.
2. Each core member states a position with reasoning. The
   [quest-designer](../../02-design/quest-designer/) speaks for **playability**: where does
   this beat collapse if the player branches, refuses, or arrives out of order?
3. **Agency check** — the council's signature test: name where the story is *authored* and
   where it is *systemic emergence*. If a beat assumes the player did something they were
   never required to do, the fiction is lying. Resolve story-vs-agency tension explicitly.
4. **Lore-consistency pass** — does this contradict established canon, character voice, or
   timeline? The chair owns the lore bible; a contradiction is a defect, not a flavor choice.
5. **Mechanic-feel deference** — if a story beat needs a mechanic to *feel* a certain way,
   the call belongs to the [gameplay-council](../gameplay-council/); the narrative council
   states the intent, not the implementation.
6. Reversibility check: a script page is cheap to redraft; recorded VO is not. Prefer the
   cheapest path to a real verdict, and gate expensive commitments behind a written decision.
7. No consensus at the round limit → the **chair decides** and records every dissent.

## Approval Gate by Tier
- **T2 — new quest line / character / narrative system:** the council **+**
  [Executive Orchestrator](../../01-executive/executive-orchestrator/) sign off.
- **T3 — story pillar, major arc, or tonal shift:** requires
  [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) sign-off (vision); the
  [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) may veto on quality grounds.
- A verdict is provisional until the beat is read in context — a table-read or in-engine
  pass — before any VO is committed with the [voice-director](../../05-audio/voice-director/).

## Escalation
- Craft deadlock (story craft inside the council) →
  [narrative-designer](../../02-design/narrative-designer/) as chair decides.
- Story vs. mechanic feel → [gameplay-council](../gameplay-council/); unresolved →
  [Executive Orchestrator](../../01-executive/executive-orchestrator/).
- Vision or tonal conflict → [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/),
  the vision tie-break on all fiction.
- Quality objection → [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/), who may block
  regardless of tier. See [../../00-company/decision-authority.md](../../00-company/decision-authority.md).
