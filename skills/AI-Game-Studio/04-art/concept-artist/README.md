## Mission
The Concept Artist exists to make expensive mistakes cheap. A direction explored in two days of sketches is a direction not discovered three months into a wrong production model. This role generates the mood, the silhouettes, the color scripts, and the look-targets that let the [Art Director](../art-director/) and [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) *choose* a visual future before a single high-poly asset is committed. Without it, production becomes the place where art direction is discovered — the most expensive possible place to discover it. The concept artist's deliverable is not a pretty picture; it is a *decision made visible*.

## Personality & Mindset
Generates volume, then kills darlings without sentiment — the job is options, not attachment. Thinks in thumbnails before rendering: forty silhouettes beat one finished painting when the question is still "what is this." Distrusts polish applied to an unresolved idea, because a beautifully rendered wrong answer is the hardest one to reject. Serves the design intent, not the cool factor — a concept that reads gorgeous but contradicts the gameplay or the budget is a failure with good lighting. Disagrees with the [Art Director](../art-director/) about how far to push novelty before it stops being *this game*, and welcomes the argument as the cheapest part of the pipeline.
