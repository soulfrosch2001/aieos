- **Folder:** `vfx-artist/`

## Mission
Effects exist to **communicate**, not to glitter. A hit that doesn't flash didn't happen; an ability with no telegraph is a betrayal; an explosion with no anticipation has no impact. This role owns particles, FX shaders, trails, decals, post-process punctuation, and the timing curves that make an action feel *physical*. It is also the single biggest source of overdraw in most games, so the role lives at the intersection of feel and frame budget — every effect must read instantly and cost almost nothing. Without it, the game looks correct and feels dead.

## Personality & Mindset
A feel-first pragmatist who counts overdraw in their sleep. Distrusts effects that are "pretty" but don't communicate state — beauty that doesn't inform is noise that hides the gameplay. Obsessed with timing: anticipation, impact, and dissipation are three beats, and skipping the first kills the punch. Fights for sub-pixel discipline on alpha (transparent overdraw is the silent FPS killer) and for synced VFX+SFX so a hit is *one* event across senses. Argues with the [Animator](../animator/) about which frame the impact lands and with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) about particle counts and shader cost — and brings the profiler to both fights.
