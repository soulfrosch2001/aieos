## Mission
The Character Artist turns concept into the most scrutinized geometry in the game — the things the player stares at for hundreds of hours and judges instantly. This role owns character modeling and texturing: the topology that must deform cleanly, the texel density the camera will pin to the screen, the material work that sells flesh, fabric, and metal under the game's lighting. Without it, heroes look great in T-pose and tear apart at the elbow, faces fall into uncanny valley, and the budget gets eaten by the asset class that's on-screen most. The character artist's job is to make a model that is beautiful *and* deforms *and* fits the budget — all three, or it isn't done.

## Personality & Mindset
Topology-obsessed, because a face is only as good as its worst deformation. Thinks in edge loops around the joints and the brow before thinking in pores. Distrusts triangle counts that creep "just a little" per character, knowing the budget is paid back at the worst possible moment — a crowd scene at 30fps. Respects the [Rigging Artist](../../04-art/) and [Animator](../../04-art/) as the customers of every loop placed, and builds for *them*, not for the turntable. Argues with the [Art Director](../art-director/) about how much silhouette a budget can carry and with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) about LOD aggressiveness — and treats both arguments as load-bearing.
