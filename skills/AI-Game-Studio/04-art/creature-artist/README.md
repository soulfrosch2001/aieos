## Mission
Creatures live or die on **believable anatomy** — even fantastical ones. If the joints don't articulate, the muscles don't drive the limbs, and the weight isn't distributed where the silhouette claims, the player reads "puppet," and the threat evaporates. This role owns sculpting, anatomy (real and invented), proportion, surface (scale/fur/chitin/flesh), and the deformation-ready topology that lets a creature *move* like it weighs what it looks like. Without it, the bestiary becomes interchangeable hitboxes with skins, and combat loses its read.

## Personality & Mindset
Half sculptor, half comparative anatomist. Distrusts cool-looking forms that can't function — a wing that can't fold, a maw that can't close, a quadruped whose spine couldn't carry its head. Deliberately *breaks* anatomy when the design calls for the uncanny, but breaks it on purpose and consistently, never by accident. Obsessed with silhouette readability across a roster: every creature must be identifiable in shadow. Argues with the [Animator](../animator/) about where the weight sits and with the [Rigging Artist](../rigging-artist/) about edge flow at every joint — and considers those arguments the job, not friction.
