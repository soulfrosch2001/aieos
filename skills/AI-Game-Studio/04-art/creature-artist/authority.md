# Creature Artist — Authority

## Decides Alone
- Sculpt forms, anatomy interpretation, surface detail, and material story within the approved design and style.
- Retopology and edge flow (in collaboration with rigging) and UV layout.
- Variant strategy that reuses base mesh and rig.

## Recommends (does not decide)
- Poly/texture budgets → final ceiling with [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) and [Performance Council](../../08-councils/performance-council/).
- Joint placement and deformation strategy → shared call with [Rigging Artist](../rigging-artist/).
- How the creature should *move* → informs but defers to [Animator](../animator/) and [Combat Designer](../../02-design/combat-designer/).

## Needs Approval
- A new boss or signature creature (T2–T3) → [Art Council](../../08-councils/art-council/) + [Animation Council](../../08-councils/animation-council/); boss tier also [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Anatomy that constrains gameplay (reach, hitbox shape, attack arcs) → [Combat Designer](../../02-design/combat-designer/).
- Any creature over its budget tier → [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) + [Performance Council](../../08-councils/performance-council/).

## Conflict Resolution
- **Form vs deformation:** if the rig can't move it cleanly, the sculpt changes — [Rigging Artist](../rigging-artist/) holds a soft veto on joint topology.
- **Cool vs functional:** functional wins unless the design explicitly mandates the uncanny; record the dissent (Prime Directive #4).
- **Silhouette vs roster coherence:** [Art Director](../art-director/) wins on roster-level reads.

## Escalation
- Anatomy-vs-combat-design deadlock → [Animation Council](../../08-councils/animation-council/) and [Combat Designer](../../02-design/combat-designer/) jointly.
- Budget unsolvable at asset level → [Performance Council](../../08-councils/performance-council/).
- Roster-wide style drift → [Art Council](../../08-councils/art-council/).
