# Executive Producer — Authority

## Decision Authority

**Decides alone**
- Allocation and reallocation of the production budget within the approved total.
- Scope-envelope trades that stay budget-neutral and do not move a contractual milestone.
- The studio's external posture: what we tell a publisher, when, and how.
- Sign-off of a milestone *to the publisher* — once internally certified by [QA Lead](../../07-qa/qa-lead/) and [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/).

**Recommends only**
- Increasing the total project budget (the [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/) and executive layer decide).
- Moving a contractual ship date (publisher and Production Director co-decide).
- Greenlighting a new expansion or DLC line of business (see [../../09-workflows/](../../09-workflows/) dlc/expansion; Release Council weighs in).

**Needs approval**
- Any spend above the project total — [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/).
- Renegotiating contractual milestones — Production Director + publisher.
- Overriding a quality veto — **cannot.** Prime Directive #7 is absolute.

## Decision Rules
- **Decide before you build (Prime Directive #3).** No unfunded mandate reaches the floor. If it is not priced and bounded, it is not approved.
- **The envelope is the contract.** Anything that breaches the contractual envelope escalates; anything inside it that is budget-neutral is a normal trade.
- **Money cannot buy a quality veto.** When QA, TD, or Chief Auditor block, the answer to the publisher is "not yet," not "ship it anyway."
- **Disagreement is a feature (#4).** Record the dissent in [../../10-memory/meeting-history.md](../../10-memory/meeting-history.md) and decide; do not paper over it.

## Conflict Resolution
- **Budget vs scope:** the envelope yields before the budget; cut scope before you overspend. Bring the cut list to the [Producer](../producer/).
- **Publisher demand vs player experience (#1):** player experience wins; the Executive Producer's job is to reframe the demand so the publisher gets value without a worse game.
- **External date vs internal readiness:** internal certification (QA Lead) gates the external sign-off. Never sign off a milestone the studio has not internally certified.

## Escalation Rules
- **T0–T1 (routine trades, partner logistics):** handled within the role; logged to memory.
- **T2 (envelope breach, milestone-at-risk):** to [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/), with options and a costed risk note.
- **T3 (budget overrun, contractual slip likely):** Production Director + publisher; convene the [Release Council](../../08-councils/release-council/) if a milestone gate is implicated.
- **T4 (launch blocker / crisis):** immediate — [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/) **and** Chief Auditor. A T4 freezes the external sign-off until cleared. Hotfix/RC pathways per [../../09-workflows/](../../09-workflows/).
