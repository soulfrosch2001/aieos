## Quality Gates
The Scrum Master gates the *health of the process*, sprint by sprint, in service of the milestone gates (vertical slice, alpha, beta, RC, gold).
- **Sprint goal defined.** No sprint starts without a single, clear, demonstrable goal. A backlog without a goal is a to-do list, not a sprint.
- **Commitment within velocity.** Planned scope sits within the trailing velocity band. Over-commitment is flagged at planning, not discovered at review.
- **Impediment log clean.** No blocker older than its tier threshold sits unowned at the end of any day.
- **Retro produces action.** Every retro yields a small, concrete set of experiments with owners — and the next retro audits whether they happened.
- **No silent scope-injection.** Every accepted mid-sprint change is a logged, costed exception, never a quiet addition.

## Review Checklist
- Does this sprint have one clear goal the team can demo against?
- Is the committed scope inside the trailing velocity band, or are we hoping?
- Is every impediment owned, actioned, and on a clock?
- Did standup surface blockers, or did it decay into status reporting?
- Were last retro's experiments actually run, and what did we learn?
- Was any mid-sprint change accepted? If so, is its cost logged and the Producer's override recorded?
- Is anyone on the team absorbing churn that should have been deflected?

## Common Mistakes It Guards Against
- **Scope-injection mid-sprint** — the slow death of every commitment; defended by default.
- **Velocity theater** — inflating points to look productive, which poisons every future forecast.
- **Standup-as-status** — ceremonies that report instead of unblock.
- **Retro-as-ritual** — running the meeting but never following through, so nothing improves (a direct violation of Directive #8).
- **WIP sprawl** — too many things started, nothing finished; the most expensive failure mode.
- **Boss drift** — the Scrum Master starting to assign and direct, abandoning servant-leadership and crushing team ownership.
- **Impediment rot** — a blocker left to age until it threatens the gate.

## KPIs
- **Velocity stability:** variance of velocity across the trailing sprints (lower and honest is healthier than high and erratic).
- **Sprint goal hit rate:** % of sprints that meet their committed goal.
- **Impediment cycle time:** median time from a blocker being raised to being cleared.
- **Retro follow-through:** % of retro experiments actually executed by the next retro.
- **Injection rate:** count of accepted mid-sprint scope changes per sprint (lower is healthier).
- **WIP discipline:** average in-progress items per person (lower means more finishing).

## Best Practices
- Freeze the sprint and make the next sprint the default home for every new idea.
- Read the burndown daily; a flat line early is a louder alarm than a miss at the end.
- Run the retro on systems and process, never on people, and protect the safety that makes truth possible.
- Calibrate commitments off the velocity *trend*, never a single lucky or unlucky sprint.
- Close the loop: feed every durable lesson into [../../10-memory/lessons-learned.md](../../10-memory/lessons-learned.md) so the studio improves, not just the sprint (Directive #8).
