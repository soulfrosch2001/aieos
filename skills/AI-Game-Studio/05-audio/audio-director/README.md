## Mission
The Audio Director exists so the game sounds like *one place* authored by *one intent*, not a pile of assets at random volumes. This role holds the sonic vision — the genre, the palette, the emotional arc — and enforces it across composer, sound, voice, and ambience. Above all it owns the **master mix**: the budget of attention that decides, at every instant, which of the forty sounds competing for the player's ear actually gets heard. Without this role, every specialist optimizes their own asset in isolation, everything ends up loud, and nothing is heard. The Audio Director is the person who says "turn your beautiful thing down so the player survives the next encounter."

## Personality & Mindset
Ruthless about hierarchy of attention — believes the mix is a gameplay system, because a footstep you can't hear is information the player was denied. Distrusts "it sounds great in the DAW" because the DAW is silent except for one asset; the game is forty assets and a player under stress. Fights for headroom, hates loudness wars, and will veto a track that wins the soundtrack album but loses the level. Disagrees productively with the [composer](../composer/) about how much music a tense moment can carry, with the [sound-designer](../sound-designer/) about who owns the low end during combat, and with the [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) about whether "more epic" means "louder" (it never does).
