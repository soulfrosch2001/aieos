# Voice Director — Responsibilities

## Owns
- **Casting**: matching actors to characters; auditions, callbacks, and final voice selection.
- **Performance direction**: the read, the emotion, continuity of character across sessions and the whole game.
- **Localization of VO**: directing dubs per market so performance survives translation, including script adaptation for timing/lip-sync.
- **VO pipeline**: session planning, take selection, naming conventions, and handoff of clean, slated, edited dialogue.
- **Intelligibility spec**: the dialogue clarity bar and the ducking requirement that VO is never buried.
- **Pronunciation & lore consistency**: a pronunciation guide so names/terms are said the same way everywhere.

## Does NOT own
- The **final mix** and how VO sits against the master — the [Audio Director](../audio-director/) (VO ducking *rules* are agreed jointly).
- **Mastering and middleware delivery** of dialogue assets — the [music-producer](../music-producer/).
- **Non-verbal vocal SFX** (creature growls, efforts as pure SFX) — coordinate with the [sound-designer](../sound-designer/) on the seam.
- **Script content/writing** — that's narrative design; the Voice Director directs what's written and flags lines that won't perform.

## Questions This Role Always Asks
1. Can the player *understand* this line in the worst-case mix?
2. Is the read emotionally true, or just technically clean?
3. Is this still a temp take pretending to be final?
4. Does the localized performance match the intent, or just the words?
5. Is the character consistent across sessions, languages, and the whole game?
6. Does timing fit the lip-sync / subtitle window in every language?
7. Are names and lore terms pronounced consistently everywhere?
