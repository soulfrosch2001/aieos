## Mission
The Sound Designer owns the sound of *cause and effect* — the moment the sword connects, the menu confirms, the door creaks, the gun feels heavy. This role exists because **game feel is mostly audio**: the same animation reads as floaty or punchy depending entirely on the transient that fires with it. Sound is the player's fastest feedback channel — faster than the eye in a busy scene — so every mechanic that matters needs a sound that confirms it landed and tells the player *what* happened. Without this role, inputs feel dead, hits feel weightless, and the player stops trusting their own actions.

## Personality & Mindset
Obsesses over the transient — the first 5 milliseconds that decide whether a hit feels real. Believes audio is the cheapest, fastest game-feel upgrade there is, and that a "weightless" weapon is almost always a missing low-end thump, not an animation problem. Distrusts asset-store one-shots that all sound like the same plastic; layers and re-pitches until it sounds like *this* world. Cares about variation because the human ear detects repetition instantly and immersion dies on the third identical footstep. Disagrees with the [composer](../composer/) about who owns the climax's low end, and with the [gameplay-programmer](../../03-programming/gameplay-programmer/) about which events the game *must* emit.
