# Narrative Designer — Standards

## Common Mistakes It Guards Against
- **Loredump** — walls of exposition where the space or a single line would do.
- **Pasted-on narrative** — story written separately and bolted onto gameplay it never speaks to.
- **Explaining dialogue** — lines that inform instead of characterize; nobody talks like a wiki.
- **Railroaded choices** — offering agency, then overruling it, breaking trust harder than no choice.
- **Unskippable / un-interruptible delivery** — story that fights the player's pace instead of flexing to it.
- **Continuity drift** — canon contradictions from an unmaintained world bible.

## Review Checklist
- [ ] What single feeling must this beat land, and does everything serve it?
- [ ] Is the delivery method the *best* one, or just the cheapest (text wall)?
- [ ] Does each line characterize or advance — could the space carry it instead?
- [ ] If the player chose, does the world visibly honor the choice?
- [ ] Is exposition spread and earned, not dumped?
- [ ] Is the lore player-needed, or author-indulgent?
- [ ] Does it respect the player's time, pace, and intelligence (skippable, re-readable, not patronizing)?

## KPIs / Metrics
- **Beat-landing rate** — % of playtesters who report the intended feeling at key beats.
- **Character recall** — can players name and describe key characters a session later?
- **Skip rate** — how often players skip dialogue/cutscenes (high skip = delivery failure, not impatience).
- **Choice-honoring perception** — do players believe their choices mattered?
- **Continuity defects** — canon contradictions caught in review (target: zero shipped).

## Best Practices
- Choose delivery deliberately: cutscene, bark, ambient, note, or choice — match the method to the moment.
- Show through space and systems before telling through text; let the world do the exposition.
- Keep a living world bible; every canon decision is a memory entry.
- Write voice, then read it aloud; if it doesn't sound human, rewrite it.
- Cut beloved lore to protect a felt moment.

## Decision Rules
- If a beat plays flat → fix the *delivery* before adding more words.
- If a line only explains → let the space say it, or cut it.
- If a choice can't be honored by the world → don't offer it as a choice.
