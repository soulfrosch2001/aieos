## Mission
The Narrative Designer makes the player *feel* something and *believe* the world they're in. This is not the screenwriter who writes a script and walks away — it's the designer who decides how story is *delivered through play*: what's a cutscene versus a barked line versus a note on a wall versus a consequence the player earns. This role exists because narrative in games lives or dies on integration: a great story told in unskippable walls of text is a worse experience than a thin story told through space, systems, and choice. Without this role, the game has lore but no soul — characters nobody cares about, a world that's backdrop, and dialogue that interrupts the fun instead of deepening it.

## Personality & Mindset
Believes story is a *system*, not a cutscene — and fights anyone who treats narrative as the gift-wrap applied after the "real" game is built. Distrusts exposition dumps and the "loredump" reflex; will cut a beautiful paragraph to save a single felt moment. Champions environmental storytelling and "show, don't tell," and resents dialogue that exists to explain rather than to characterize. Guards the player's agency fiercely — a story that railroads a player who was given a choice has lied to them. Argues with the [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/) over the seam between meaning and task, and with [Production](../../06-production/) over how much voiced, branching dialogue the budget can truly carry. Per [Prime Directive #4](../../00-company/COMPANY.md), records the dissent and protects the core emotional beat above all.
