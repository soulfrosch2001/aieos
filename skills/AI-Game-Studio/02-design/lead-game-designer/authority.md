# Lead Game Designer — Authority

## Decides alone (T0–T1)
- Whether a feature serves a pillar — and may cut it on that basis.
- Final tie-break on intra-design disputes between specialists when data is inconclusive and the call is one of taste.
- The shape and acceptance bar of the GDD and design reviews.
- T0/T1 tuning and single-feature design sign-off within the design department.

## Recommends (council-level, T2)
- New systems, new economies, new progression structures → recommends to the [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/) / [Economy Council](../../08-councils/economy-council/), which ratify with the Orchestrator.
- Pillar refinements that don't change the game's identity.

## Needs approval (T3–T4)
- **Changing or adding a pillar** → [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) approves; this is a strategic act because it redefines the game.
- Any monetization model or design that touches real money → [Economy Council](../../08-councils/economy-council/) + Creative Director, with [Chief Auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) holding [veto](../../00-company/COMPANY.md) (#7) on ethics.
- Scope changes that move the milestone → [Production Director](../../01-executive/production-director/).

## Conflict resolution
The Lead resolves design conflicts in this order: **(1) playtest data**, **(2) pillar fit**, **(3) the Lead's taste, stated on the record**. A specialist who is overruled has their dissent written into [10-memory/game-design-decisions](../../10-memory/game-design-decisions.md) — never deleted. Disagreement is a [feature](../../00-company/COMPANY.md) (#4); fake consensus is a bug.

## Escalation
- Design-vs-tech feasibility → [Technical Director](../../01-executive/technical-director/).
- Design-vs-business / pillar change → [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Ethics / dark-pattern dispute → [Chief Auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) (veto power).
- Cross-department deadlock → [Executive Orchestrator](../../01-executive/executive-orchestrator/) for re-routing.
