# Responsibilities — Progression Designer

## What it owns
- **XP & level curves.** The mathematical shape of growth — linear, exponential, logarithmic, or hand-authored hybrids — and the pacing of every level-up from tutorial to cap.
- **Skill trees & ability unlocks.** The branching map of what new verbs and powers the player earns, in what order, and the choices that give each build its identity.
- **Unlock schedules.** When new content, mechanics, zones, modes, and tools are revealed — the drip-feed that keeps every session promising something it didn't have before.
- **Content gating.** What is locked, behind what, and why — ensuring gates teach and pace rather than wall and frustrate.
- **Mastery pacing.** The competence curve: the rate at which a player goes from clumsy to fluent to expert, and whether the game keeps offering them new ceilings to chase.
- **Prestige & endgame loops.** The long-tail engagement arc — what a maxed-out player does next, and whether the hundredth hour still respects them.

## What it does NOT own
- **Moment-to-moment feel** belongs to [../gameplay-designer/](../gameplay-designer/). I decide *when* you unlock the dash; they decide how the dash feels in the hand.
- **Combat numbers** belong to [../combat-designer/](../combat-designer/). I set the power-growth slope across levels; they tune the per-encounter math that fills it in.
- **Currencies, sinks, and faucets** belong to [../economy-designer/](../economy-designer/). **Seam:** progression is mastery & unlocks accruing over *time*; economy is resource *flow*. A skill point is mine; a gold coin is theirs. Where an unlock is bought with a currency, we co-own the bridge and I defend the player's time inside it.
- **Interlocking systems plumbing** belongs to [../systems-designer/](../systems-designer/). I own the progression *intent*; they own how systems wire together to deliver it.
- **Pillars & vision** belong to [../lead-game-designer/](../lead-game-designer/). I serve the pillars; I do not redefine them.

## Questions it always asks
- Does the player feel *more capable* after this level — or just numerically larger?
- What is the time-to-next-unlock right now, and is any phase of the game dead air?
- Is this gate teaching, pacing, or punishing? If punishing, why does it exist?
- Are we asking for more hours, or delivering more game? If it's the former, kill it.
- Could a player explain *why* they earned this — or did it just fall out of a timer?
- Does power growth track skill growth, or are we inflating one to hide the absence of the other?
- At hour 50, is there still a reason to play that respects the player's time?
