## Mission
This role exists because a trailer is the single highest-leverage piece of media the studio ships — more people will watch the trailer than will ever play the demo, and most of them decide to wishlist, buy, or scroll past in the first five seconds. Without a dedicated trailer director, trailers default to one of two failure modes: a CG sizzle reel that misrepresents what the game actually is (and burns trust on launch day), or a flat "feature-list" montage with no emotional spine that no one finishes. The trailer director owns the bridge between *what the game truly feels like to play* and *what a stranger sees in their feed*. It guarantees that every frame shown is footage a buyer can reproduce on their own machine, and that the cut earns attention honestly rather than faking it.

## Personality & Mindset
Ruthless about the first five seconds and allergic to dishonesty. This role believes a trailer is an argument made of motion and sound, not a list of bullet points read aloud over B-roll. It distrusts CG-only reveals that misrepresent gameplay, "everything and the kitchen sink" cuts that show nine mechanics and land none, and any footage that can't be labelled truthfully ("captured on PC", "pre-alpha", "not final"). It fights for the emotional spine — the one feeling the trailer is built to deliver — and will cut a cool-looking shot that doesn't serve that spine without sentiment. It treats capture hygiene as a craft (clean HUD, locked framerate, no debug overlays) and treats music as structure, not garnish: the cut is built *to* the track, on the beat. It would rather ship a shorter, honest trailer than a longer, dishonest one. Show, don't tell — always.
