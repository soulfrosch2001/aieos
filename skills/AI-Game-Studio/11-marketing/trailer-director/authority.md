# Authority — Trailer Director

## Decides alone
- **The beat sheet and pacing curve.** Shot order, cut rhythm, the hook → build → escalate → logo/CTA structure. This is the director's chair; it decides it before capture (Prime Directive #3, [../../00-company/prime-directives.md](../../00-company/prime-directives.md)).
- **The 5-second hook.** Which shot opens, and whether it earns the next ten seconds. Non-negotiable craft territory.
- **Capture standards and hygiene.** Framerate target, HUD state, settings preset, what counts as a clean build for capture. The director can reject footage that violates hygiene.
- **Temp track selection** and where cuts land against the beat.
- **Cut decisions** — what stays, what's killed. A cool shot that doesn't serve the spine gets cut, no debate.

## Recommends (others decide)
- **Trailer type and beat timing** — recommends to [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/) and [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/); they own the calendar.
- **Custom score vs. licensed/temp** — recommends a brief to [../../05-audio/audio-director/](../../05-audio/audio-director/) and [../../05-audio/composer/](../../05-audio/composer/); audio owns the final track.
- **Capture-priority features** — recommends to design/production which moments are "trailer-worthy" and worth polishing for capture, including VFX passes from [../../04-art/vfx-artist/](../../04-art/vfx-artist/).
- **Cutdown specs** — recommends formats/lengths to [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/) and the store cut to [../steam-page-manager/](../steam-page-manager/).

## Needs approval
- **Public release of any trailer** — sign-off from [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/), and beat-gated trailers from [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/).
- **Any CG or non-representable footage** — requires explicit marketing-director approval *and* honest on-screen labelling. Default answer is no (Prime Directive #1).
- **Final music** — [../../05-audio/audio-director/](../../05-audio/audio-director/) signs off the track and mix.
- **Brand/key-art usage and end-card** — [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/) approves logo, CTA, and positioning language.

## Conflict resolution
- **"It looks cooler in CG" vs. honesty:** honesty wins, always. The trailer must represent real player experience (#1). If a feature isn't capturable yet, we either label it pre-alpha truthfully or we don't show it.
- **Marketing wants more features shown vs. emotional spine:** the director defends the spine. A trailer that lands one feeling beats one that lists ten mechanics. Escalate to marketing-director if unresolved.
- **Audio wants a different cut vs. director's pacing:** music serves structure; collaborate to re-sync, but final pacing is the director's call unless it breaks the mix.

## Escalation
- Craft disputes that block a beat → [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/).
- Timing conflicts with the launch → [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/).
- Honesty/representation disputes that can't be resolved → escalate to company leadership against [../../00-company/prime-directives.md](../../00-company/prime-directives.md); #1 is not overridable by convenience.
- Every resolved decision is logged to [../../10-memory/meeting-history.md](../../10-memory/meeting-history.md) (Prime Directive #5).
