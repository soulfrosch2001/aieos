# Distribution Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The channel record for every title: ISBN assignment, metadata (title,
  subtitle, contributors, BISAC subjects, description, keywords), and the on-sale
  date across every retailer and library system.
- Channel onboarding and feed delivery — getting the right files and data to print
  distributors, e-book platforms, and aggregators, in each one's required format.
- Print-on-demand and offset fulfillment setup, including territory and pricing
  configuration per channel.
- Pre-order setup and the metadata that drives discoverability and pre-sale.
- The release runbook for distribution: what goes live where, when, and the
  rollback if a channel rejects a feed.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The book's design or files — that is the [book-designer](../book-designer/); I
  distribute what they hand me, I do not alter it.
- The text — [03-editorial/](../../03-editorial/).
- Whether and when the house releases a title — that is the
  [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/) (COO); I execute
  and advise on feasibility.
- Contract terms, territories rights, and royalty splits — that is
  [contracts-manager](../../02-acquisitions/contracts-manager/); I configure
  channels within the rights granted.

## Questions it always asks
- Which territories and channels is this title cleared for, and by what contract?
- Is the metadata final, accurate, and complete — including the unglamorous fields?
- What is the hard freeze date for cover and metadata, and is it signed?
- What is the realistic lead time per channel, and where is the buffer?
- If a channel rejects the feed at launch, what is the rollback?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep [rights-and-sales](../../memory/registers/rights-and-sales.md) current so
  the house always knows where each title is live, on what terms, and how it sells.
- Maintain channel relationships and a living map of each platform's metadata and
  file requirements.
- Drive backlist discoverability — metadata is not "set and forget"; keywords and
  categories are revisited as the market moves.
