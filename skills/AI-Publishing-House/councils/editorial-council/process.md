# Editorial Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel discussion norms inherited via the stdlib
[architecture-council](../../../councils/architecture-council/). Dissent is
recorded, never suppressed (Directive
[#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). A standard is argued on the reader
and the work, not on seniority; the [editorial-director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/)
chairs but does not pre-empt the debate. Every ruling must name what it trades
away — there is no free editorial standard.

## Decision process
1. **Frame** — the question is stated as a choice between at least two viable
   directions, with the work or standard at stake.
2. **Reads** — developmental, copy, and proof perspectives are heard; each names
   where the decision helps and where it hurts.
3. **Blast radius** — the council establishes whether this binds one title or the
   whole list.
4. **Trade-off** — the chosen direction's cost is stated explicitly.
5. **Decide** — the [editorial-director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/)
   rules, breaking deadlock if needed, and may exercise the editorial veto on work
   below standard.
6. **Record** — minutes, ruling, and dissent are filed (see [output.md](output.md)).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: [T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) single-title
  editorial direction.
- Must escalate: [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) standard-defining
  rulings need council **plus** executive sign-off; see
  [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
Deadlock escalates one level up per
[decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) and
[escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). A
[chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto stops the
work; only a human maintainer overrides it. A standards dispute that turns
commercial escalates to the [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/).
