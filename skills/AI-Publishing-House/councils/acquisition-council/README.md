# Acquisition Council

Status: stable
Type: council
Owner: ceo
Extends: feature-council

## Purpose
The room where the house decides **whether to acquire a manuscript** — to add it
to the list, commit advance and production capacity, and stake the house's name on
it. It inherits the stdlib [feature-council](../../../councils/feature-council/):
a manuscript is the house's "feature proposal", and the question is the same
shape — *is this the right thing to commit to, and what are we trading away to do
it?* It does **not** edit the book (that is
[editorial-council](../editorial-council/)), set the release date, or design the
object.

## Participants
- **Chair** (breaks deadlocks): [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/) — owns the list and
  decides what the house publishes (Directive
  [#1](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- **Core** (must attend): [acquisitions-editor](../../02-acquisitions/acquisitions-editor/),
  [literary-scout](../../02-acquisitions/literary-scout/),
  [contracts-manager](../../02-acquisitions/contracts-manager/), and the
  [editorial-director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/) (does the book meet
  the bar we can hit?).
- **Advisory** (as needed): [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/)
  for cost/schedule feasibility; [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/)
  (quality/process veto, never directs).

## When convened
At [T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) for a standard acquisition; a
list-defining or lead-title acquisition is [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md)
and adds executive sign-off. Not standing — one manuscript, one council. Inherits,
never forks (Directive [#6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Inputs
- The manuscript (or proposal) and the scout/editor read on it.
- The market and comparable-titles case — who buys this and why.
- Draft commercial terms from [contracts-manager](../../02-acquisitions/contracts-manager/)
  (advance, rights, territories).
- An editorial-feasibility read: how much work to reach house standard.
- At least one honest alternative — pass, or acquire a different title with the
  same capacity.
