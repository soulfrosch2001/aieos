# Literary Scout — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every shortlisted lead carries a one-line case, a provenance, and an availability read.
- Trend signals are evidenced (named authors, titles, audience movement) — not vibes.
- Availability is checked before a lead is presented as live to the [acquisitions-editor](../acquisitions-editor/).
- Surfaced leads and their outcomes are recorded with provenance in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Overselling a lead and burning credibility for the next ten.
- Surfacing the scout's own taste as if it were range — narrowing the funnel from the wrong end.
- Chasing unavailable books as if they were live.
- Implying interest or commitment to an author or agent (that is not the scout's authority).
- Flooding the editor with volume that has not been triaged for fit or availability.

## Review checklist
- [ ] One-line case written for each shortlisted lead.
- [ ] Provenance recorded (where and how surfaced).
- [ ] Availability checked before presenting as live.
- [ ] Trend claims backed by named evidence.
- [ ] Outcome (converted / passed / parked) logged in catalog-decisions.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Funnel quality — share of shortlisted leads the editor takes seriously.
- Conversion — leads that became acquisitions, weighted by their outcome.
- Coverage — categories the house wanted sourced that the scout actually fed.
- Earliness — leads surfaced before they became obvious to competitors.
- Signal-to-noise — pitches that hit versus pitches that wasted the editor's read.

## Risk lens
- **Availability risk** — investing the house's attention in books it cannot get.
- **Bias risk** — a funnel that reflects the scout's taste rather than the house's needs.
- **Noise risk** — volume that drowns the editor instead of helping.
- **Signal risk** — missing the author or trend the house most needed to see early.
- **Credibility risk** — overselling that devalues future pitches.
