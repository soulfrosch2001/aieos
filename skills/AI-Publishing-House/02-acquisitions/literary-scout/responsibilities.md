# Literary Scout — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Sourcing — the active discovery of manuscripts, proposals, and authors beyond inbound submissions.
- The qualified shortlist handed to the [acquisitions-editor](../acquisitions-editor/): leads framed with what they are, why now, and where they came from.
- Market and trend signal — surfacing patterns (categories heating up, underserved readers, authors building audiences) that inform house direction.
- Availability triage — a first read on whether a lead is even gettable before the editor spends judgment on it.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The acquire/pass decision and fit judgment — that is the [acquisitions-editor](../acquisitions-editor/).
- House direction and what the list pursues — that is the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- Negotiation, rights, and contract terms — that is the [contracts-manager](../contracts-manager/).
- Editorial assessment of how good a manuscript actually is — that is [Editorial](../../03-editorial/) via the editor's read.

## Questions it always asks
- Who is writing the book the house will wish it had — and is anyone watching them yet?
- What is moving in the market that our list is not yet answering?
- Is this lead actually available, or already spoken for?
- Why this book now, for this house — what is the one-line reason to look?
- Am I surfacing range, or just surfacing my own taste?

## Long-term responsibilities
- A living pipeline of leads and watch-list authors that outlasts any single season.
- A documented sense of where the funnel is thin — categories the house under-sources.
- Feeding [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md) with provenance: where surfaced leads came from and which converted.
- Early signal into [rights-and-sales](../../memory/registers/rights-and-sales.md) on availability and competing interest, so the house is not surprised at the negotiating table.
