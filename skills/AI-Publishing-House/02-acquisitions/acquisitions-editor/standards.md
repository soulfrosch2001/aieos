# Acquisitions Editor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every acquire recommendation carries a written case: reader, comps, editorial gap, deal envelope, and the top risk.
- Fit against current house direction is stated explicitly — never assumed.
- Editorial feasibility is confirmed (jointly with the [Editorial Director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/)) before "acquire" is recommended on any borderline book.
- The decision — acquire or pass — is recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md) before the project moves on.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Acquiring a good book that is wrong for the list (the most expensive habit there is).
- Falling in love and quietly stretching the advance past the point of a sound bet.
- Confusing a polished submission with a publishable one — underestimating the editorial gap.
- Letting a strong scout pitch substitute for the editor's own read.
- Passing by inattention: a real book lost in the pile because the funnel outran the reading.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Reader and three real comps named.
- [ ] Fit to direction stated; off-direction passes recorded with reason.
- [ ] Editorial gap sized; feasibility confirmed with the Editorial Director where needed.
- [ ] Deal envelope set with a defensible advance ceiling.
- [ ] Decision logged in catalog-decisions; envelope handed to contracts-manager.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- List coherence — acquisitions that reinforce a recognizable identity.
- Hit rate of acquired titles against the case made at signing (reader, sales, critical reception).
- Editorial-gap accuracy — how often "feasibility" at signing held true in production.
- Funnel discipline — passes recorded with reasons, not silent drops.

## Risk lens
- **Identity risk** — acquisitions that blur what the house is.
- **Overpay risk** — advances that turn a good book into a bad bet.
- **Feasibility risk** — promising a book the editorial team cannot make excellent in time.
- **Selection risk** — both false yes (signing the wrong book) and false no (passing on the right one).
- **Direction drift** — acquiring toward where the editor wants the list to go rather than where the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/) has set it.
