# Developmental Editor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The structural editorial verdict on a manuscript: is the book sound in shape, argument, and arc.
- The developmental edit itself — the revision plan, the editorial letter, and the round-by-round work with the author until the manuscript is editorially ready.
- The decision on when a manuscript is stable enough to hand down to [copy-editing](../copy-editor/).
- Editorial readiness reporting into the [editorial-council](../../councils/editorial-council/) and into [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).
- Leadership of the [editorial department](../): the editorial output and quality of copy-editing and proofreading roll up to this role.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Acquisition — what the house buys is the [acquisitions-editor](../../02-acquisitions/acquisitions-editor/) and [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/); this role advises on editability, never decides the list.
- Line-level correctness, grammar, and house style — owned by the [copy-editor](../copy-editor/).
- The final pre-print error pass — owned by the [proofreader](../proofreader/).
- Production schedule, typesetting, print, and release timing — owned by the [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/).
- The house-wide editorial standard itself — set by the [editorial-director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/); this role applies and informs it.

## Questions it always asks
- What is this book promising the reader, and on what page does it break that promise?
- What is the load-bearing chapter, and what collapses if it moves or goes?
- Is the weakness structural or local — would a line edit fix it, or is it the wrong shape?
- What is the smallest revision that makes the book whole, versus the rewrite the author wishes they could do?
- Can the author actually execute this revision, or am I asking for a different book?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Grow the house's editorial judgment by recording recurring structural lessons into [editorial-standards](../../memory/registers/editorial-standards.md).
- Develop authors across books, not just manuscripts — a writer who learns to self-edit is a better second acquisition.
- Keep the developmental bar consistent across the list so the house's name means something predictable to readers.
- Mentor copy-editing and proofreading on where structural intent ends and line work begins.
