# Copy Editor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- A complete style sheet exists and every consistency decision is recorded on it.
- No unresolved inconsistency in names, timeline, terminology, or capitalization remains in the manuscript.
- Every voice- or meaning-touching change has an author query and resolution, not a silent overwrite.
- Structure is confirmed frozen before the pass began (joint with the [developmental-editor](../developmental-editor/)).
- The required tier gates are met (Directive #9) before the manuscript advances to proofreading.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Silent overwrites of an author's deliberate voice in the name of a grammar rule.
- Beginning the line pass before structure is frozen, then wasting it on cut text.
- Inconsistent calls — fixing "grey" to "gray" in one chapter and not another because the style sheet was incomplete.
- Treating a fact-claim as correct because it reads smoothly.
- Drifting into structural editing that belongs to the developmental stage.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Style sheet complete and applied end to end.
- [ ] Names, dates, timeline, and terminology consistent throughout.
- [ ] Every meaning-touching change carries an author query and a resolution.
- [ ] Checkable, load-bearing fact-claims are flagged.
- [ ] Structure-freeze confirmed before the pass; no structural reopen pending.
- [ ] Style-sheet and query log handed to the proofreader.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Error escape rate to proofreading — line errors the copy edit should have caught (lower is better).
- Consistency-defect count found downstream or post-print.
- Query precision — share of queries that surface a real issue rather than noise.
- Style-sheet completeness at hand-off, measured by inconsistencies introduced or missed at later stages.

## Risk lens
- Consistency risk — a name, date, or term that varies and breaks reader trust.
- Voice-erosion risk — over-correction that flattens the author's style.
- Fact risk — an uncorrected wrong claim reaching print under the house's name.
- Boundary risk — gaps or double-work where the copy and proof stages meet.
