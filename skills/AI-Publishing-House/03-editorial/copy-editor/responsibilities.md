# Copy Editor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Line-level correctness of the manuscript: grammar, syntax, usage, punctuation, and clarity.
- House style application and the per-book style sheet — the canonical record of every consistency decision.
- Continuity and internal consistency: names, timelines, terminology, cross-references, and capitalization.
- Author queries — flagging ambiguities, apparent errors, and fact-checkable claims for the author to resolve.
- The copy-editing verdict: whether the manuscript is line-clean enough to advance to [proofreading](../proofreader/).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Structure, argument, and arc — owned by the [developmental-editor](../developmental-editor/); I do not re-shape the book.
- What the house publishes — owned by [acquisitions](../../02-acquisitions/acquisitions-editor/) and the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- The final pre-print error pass on typeset pages — owned by the [proofreader](../proofreader/).
- The house style standard itself — set by the [editorial-director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/); I apply and feed it.
- Typesetting, design, and production timing — owned by the [book-designer](../../04-production/book-designer/) and [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is this an error, or the author's deliberate choice — and if unsure, have I queried rather than overwritten?
- Is this consistent with every other instance of the same thing in the book, and with the style sheet?
- Does this claim of fact need verifying, and have I flagged it?
- Has the structure actually been frozen, or am I editing lines that may still be cut?
- Does my fix preserve the author's voice, or impose mine?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain and evolve the house style guidance via [editorial-standards](../../memory/registers/editorial-standards.md), so rulings made once are not re-litigated each book.
- Build a reliable style-sheet practice that the proofreader and future editors can inherit cleanly.
- Track recurring error classes across the list so the house learns where its manuscripts tend to fail.
- Keep the boundary with proofreading clear and documented, reducing both gaps and double-work.
