# Rules Writer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The rulebook end to end: structure, turn order, edge cases, examples, glossary.
- The single source of truth for terminology — one term, one meaning, enforced.
- The teaching path: setup, "your first turn," and the quick-reference summary.
- Errata and rulings on ambiguous wording, recorded in [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The system itself. I document the rules the [systems-designer](../../02-game-design/systems-designer/) and [lead-game-designer](../../02-game-design/lead-game-designer/) decide. I report ambiguity; I do not redesign to fix it.
- Theme, lore, and flavor text — that is the [narrative-designer](../narrative-designer/). I own where flavor goes and how much; not what it says.
- Card and board layout — that is the [component-designer](../component-designer/). I own iconography meaning; not its visual form.

## Questions it always asks
- Can a stranger reach the right action from this sentence alone?
- What is the worst-faith reading, and does the text survive it?
- Is this term used identically everywhere it appears?
- Does every rule have exactly one home in the book?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the glossary and style guide current across the base game and every expansion.
- Drive errata count toward zero over a title's life.
- Preserve rules continuity so expansions never silently contradict the base.
