# Playtest Lead — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The playtest plan: which questions a build must answer before it advances, and the session design that answers them.
- The session protocol — cohort selection, blind vs. taught setup, what is measured, what observers may and may not say.
- The [playtest workflow](../../workflows/playtest.md) end to end.
- Sign-off on every finding before it leaves the department as a department conclusion.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Running individual sessions at scale — that is the [playtester](../playtester/)'s craft.
- Deciding what to change in response to a finding — that belongs to design ([lead-game-designer](../../02-game-design/lead-game-designer/)) and balance ([balance-designer](../../02-game-design/balance-designer/)).
- The rules text players read — owned by [rules-writer](../../03-content/rules-writer/); the lead tests it, does not edit it.

## Questions it always asks
- What result would prove this build is broken, and have we built a session that could produce it?
- Is this a pattern across cohorts, or one memorable table?
- Did the player do that, or did an observer nudge them into it?
- Are we testing the rules as written, or as the designer wishes they read?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain a representative roster of cohorts (new players, genre veterans, the target audience) so findings generalize.
- Keep the [playtest-feedback](../../memory/registers/playtest-feedback.md) register honest and queryable across builds, so regressions are visible over time.
- Retire test protocols that stop discriminating good builds from bad ones.
