# Playtester — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The session was run to the lead's protocol, or the departure is logged explicitly.
- Observations are recorded during play, not reconstructed afterward.
- Facts and interpretations are visibly separated in the report.
- No coaching, leading questions, or rule rescues occurred (or the breach is flagged for voiding).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Helping a stuck table and erasing the very failure under test.
- Paraphrasing players into the conclusion the report "should" reach.
- Recording from memory after the session, losing the timestamps that make it usable.
- Reusing a player who already knows the build, contaminating a blind cohort.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Session matched protocol, or deviations are logged.
- [ ] Each observation has a moment (time/turn) attached.
- [ ] Interpretations marked as hunches, not findings.
- [ ] Anomalies flagged for the lead.
- [ ] Entry appended to playtest-feedback before the session is considered done.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Observation fidelity: share of logged moments the lead can act on without follow-up questions.
- Protocol adherence: sessions run clean vs. voided for contamination.
- Anomaly value: flagged anomalies that became confirmed findings.
- Turnaround from session end to logged report.

## Risk lens
- Observer effect — my presence or help changing player behavior.
- Recall decay — anything not logged in the moment.
- Narrative drift — sliding from what happened to what it means.
- Cohort contamination — repeat or unrepresentative players.
