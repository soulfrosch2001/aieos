# Design Council — Process
Status: draft
Type: council
Owner: design-director
Extends: feature-council ([../../../councils/feature-council/process.md](../../../councils/feature-council/process.md))

## Discussion rules
Design is argued, not announced. Every candidate gets a fair playtest in
imagination before any is cut; the chair forbids "we already pitched it" as a
substitute for a reason. The lead-game-designer and the systems-designer are
*expected* to clash on the core loop, and that clash is recorded in their names,
never smoothed over (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). A
council that was unanimous on a hard design usually skipped the debate.

## Decision process
1. **Frame** — the chair restates the target player experience at stake. If the
   feeling the game should produce is unclear, the council stops here.
2. **Lay out options** — at least two designs, each with its component cost, table
   footprint, and the tension it creates.
3. **Debate the tradeoffs** — explicitly against the studio quality gates and the
   age/time band; flag any fairness cost to
   [balance-council](../balance-council/README.md).
4. **Decide** — consensus preferred; the chair breaks a true deadlock and may
   exercise the **design veto** to protect coherence.
5. **Emit a plan, not components** — the council hands a build plan to the
   game-design and content departments (Directive
   [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)); the plan names the parallel
   tracks, up to 15 (Directive [#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: [T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) — the council is
  the sign-off.
- Must escalate: [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) and above —
  council plus executive, per
  [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
A deadlock the chair will not break with the veto, or a design that grows past T2
mid-debate, escalates rather than being silently downgraded
([engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md)).
