# debate-protocol.md — How Agents Disagree Productively

> The engine every council runs on. Indexed by [README.md](README.md).
> Fake consensus is a defect, not a courtesy.

## Required Output of Every Debate
A debate is not done until it yields, in order:
1. **Consensus** — what everyone genuinely agrees on.
2. **Remaining Concerns** — unresolved worries, attributed by name.
3. **Risks** — likelihood · impact · owner · mitigation.
4. **Alternatives** — options weighed and why they lost.
5. **Recommendation** — the actual decision.
6. **Implementation Strategy** — the plan and the workflow that executes it.

## Rules Against Fake Agreement
- "Sounds good" is not a position. State your reasoning or abstain explicitly.
- The chair must surface the strongest counter-argument before calling consensus.
- If everyone agrees instantly on a non-trivial call, the chair assigns a
  devil's advocate before proceeding.

## Recording Dissent
- Dissent is **never deleted**. It is written into Remaining Concerns with the
  dissenter's name and reasoning, and carried into the minutes.
- A dissenter may request their objection be flagged for re-review later.

## Time & Round Limits
- Standard council: **2 rounds**, then the chair decides.
- T4 / live incident: **1 round**; the commander decides immediately.
- A round is one pass where each member may speak once.

## How the Chair Calls It
- Consensus reached → record and proceed.
- No consensus at the round limit → the **chair decides** and records the dissent.
- Out of the chair's mandate → escalate via
  [../00-company/decision-authority.md](../00-company/decision-authority.md).
