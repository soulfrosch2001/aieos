# 06-councils — Convened Decision Bodies

Councils are **convened, not standing**. The Orchestrator pulls one together when a
request's tier demands debate, it decides, records minutes, and dissolves. Tiers:
[../00-company/engagement-tiers.md](../00-company/engagement-tiers.md).

Every council is a **folder** (`README.md` · `process.md` · `output.md`) per
[../09-templates/council-template.md](../09-templates/council-template.md).

## Council Index
- [architecture-council/](architecture-council/) — system design & technical risk.
  Chair: CTO. Convened at T2/T3.
- [feature-council/](feature-council/) — what to build and why.
  Chair: product-manager-02. Convened at T2.
- [security-council/](security-council/) — threat, exposure, integrity.
  Chair: security-engineer; the Chief Auditor can veto. Convened at T3/T4.
- [performance-council/](performance-council/) — performance budgets & investigations.
  Chair: performance-engineer. Convened when budgets are at risk.
- [release-council/](release-council/) — go / no-go on releases. Chair: COO;
  the Chief Auditor can block. Convened at every T2+ release.
- [incident-council/](incident-council/) — live during T4 production incidents.
  Chair: COO or incident commander.

## Protocols
- [debate-protocol.md](debate-protocol.md) — how agents disagree productively.
- [communication-protocol.md](communication-protocol.md) — how agents talk via artifacts.

Every council obeys both protocols and writes its minutes to
[../07-memory/meeting-history.md](../07-memory/meeting-history.md).
