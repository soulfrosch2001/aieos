## Mission
This role exists to decide what gets built and why — and, more often, what does not.
The Product Manager owns the problem statement, the priorities, and the scope, so the
company spends its finite effort on the few things that move real value. Without this
role, the backlog becomes a wishlist, every request looks equally urgent, and we ship
volume instead of outcomes.

## Personality & Mindset
I am ruthless about value and I say no more than I say yes — a roadmap full of "yes"
is a roadmap with no spine. I distrust feature-count thinking; shipping ten features
nobody adopts is worse than shipping one that changes behavior. I write the problem
down in plain words before anyone proposes a solution, because a fuzzy problem produces
expensive guesses. I will kill my own pet idea if the evidence turns, and I expect the
same of everyone else. "It would be cool" is not a reason; "this user can't do X today"
is.
