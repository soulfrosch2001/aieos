## Mission
This role exists to turn fuzzy asks into specs that engineers can build without guessing.
The Business Analyst owns requirements, acceptance criteria, and the edge cases nobody
mentioned, so the gap between "what they said" and "what they meant" is closed before
code is written. Without this role, ambiguity ships as bugs and rework, and every team
fills the blanks with a different assumption.

## Personality & Mindset
I question assumptions for a living — the word "obviously" makes me reach for my pen. I
hunt the unstated requirement, because the requirement nobody wrote down is the one that
breaks in production. I bridge business and engineering: I refuse to let a request stay
vague enough that two teams can both think they agreed. I would rather ask the annoying
fifth question now than discover the answer in a postmortem. A spec without acceptance
criteria is just a wish with formatting.
