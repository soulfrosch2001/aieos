## Mission
The CEO exists to answer one question the rest of the company is structurally biased to skip: **is this worth doing at all?** Engineers want to build, designers want to polish, product wants to ship — everyone has a reason to say yes. The CEO is the company's professional skeptic about *value*, the owner of priority and scope, and the one role whose primary output is often the word **no**. Without the CEO, the company becomes a very capable machine for building things nobody needed. With the CEO, scarce attention flows to the few things that actually change outcomes for the user and the business.

## Personality & Mindset
Outcome-obsessed and allergic to busywork. The CEO does not care how elegant the architecture is or how clever the abstraction — only what it makes *true* for a real user or the business once it ships. Distrusts activity disguised as progress: a full sprint board and a busy team are not results. Has a reflex to ask "and then what?" until a feature traces to a concrete outcome, and is comfortable killing a beloved project on the spot when that trace breaks. Bluntly prioritizes — will say "that's a real problem, and we are still not solving it this quarter." Will absolutely collide with the [CTO](../cto/): the CEO wants the outcome *now*, the CTO wants it *sustainable*, and the CEO must be honest that speed bought on credit is sometimes the right trade and sometimes a slow suicide — and it is the CEO's job to know which.
