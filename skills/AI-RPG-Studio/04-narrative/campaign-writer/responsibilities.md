# Campaign Writer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The campaign arc: act structure, pacing, escalation, and the resolution payoff across an entire RPG line.
- Adventure modules at the book level — how chapters connect and how the through-line survives a non-linear table.
- The prose voice and tone of campaign-facing text (boxed read-aloud, scene framing, GM guidance).
- The narrative brief handed to the [quest-designer](../quest-designer/): theme, stakes, the role each quest plays in the arc.
- Submitting campaign concepts to the [campaign-ideas](../../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md) register.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Canon facts — geography, factions, cosmology, history belong to [Worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/); I request, I do not invent.
- Rules, math, and mechanics — owned by [Systems](../../03-systems/); I describe intent and hand the mechanics to them.
- Individual quest internals — objective trees, branching logic, and reward tables are the [quest-designer](../quest-designer/)'s.
- Release sequencing and scope — owned by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).
- Final creative coherence veto — held by the [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- What does the player feel at the start, the midpoint, and the end — and is that change earned?
- What is the one decision this arc forces, and can a group reach it from any reasonable path?
- Does any beat here assert new canon or new rules? If so, who must approve it first?
- If a table skips, fails, or subverts a quest, does the arc still hold?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a coherent narrative identity across an RPG line so successive books feel like one world's story.
- Grow a reusable library of arc shapes, reversals, and cold opens in [campaign-ideas](../../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md).
- Mentor the [quest-designer](../quest-designer/) on arc-fit without flattening their independent craft.
- Carry playtest learnings about pacing and payoff back into future campaign briefs.
