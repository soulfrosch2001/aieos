# Quest Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every branch is reachable and leads to a distinct outcome; no decorative choices.
- Every objective is legible at the table without GM rescue.
- Every reward has Systems sign-off and does not break progression balance.
- Failure states are real outcomes with consequences, not soft resets.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Illusory branching: choices that funnel to the same result.
- Objective fog: players unsure what they are supposed to accomplish.
- Reward inflation: loot or progression that distorts later balance.
- Mechanic-by-assumption: an objective relying on rules Systems never built.
- Canon leakage: a quest stating a world fact not in the [canon](../../memory/registers/canon.md) register.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Every branch traced to an end; none unreachable or duplicate.
- [ ] Objective statable in one clear sentence to players.
- [ ] Reward signed off by [rules-designer](../../03-systems/rules-designer/) / [balance-designer](../../03-systems/balance-designer/).
- [ ] Failure path defined and meaningful.
- [ ] Quest survives reordering by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).
- [ ] Any world fact cited from canon; outcomes appended, not overwritten.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Branch reachability rate (target: 100% of authored branches reachable).
- Player objective-clarity scores from [playtest](../../workflows/playtest.md).
- Reward-balance regressions caught in review vs. found in playtest.
- Quest patterns reused from [campaign-ideas](../../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md).

## Risk lens
- Reachability risk — dead or duplicate branches that waste design and table time.
- Balance risk — rewards that distort progression downstream.
- Legibility risk — quests that only work with an expert GM.
- Modularity risk — quests too coupled to survive release resequencing.
