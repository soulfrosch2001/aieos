# Lore Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows [communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Canon is
held to a single test: does this addition keep the world coherent and serve the player
experience (Directive #1)? Dissent is recorded, never suppressed — an overruled member's
concern is written into the minutes. Contradiction with existing canon is grounds for
rejection unless the council deliberately supersedes a prior entry.

## Decision process
1. The proposer presents the canon addition and what campaign or quest need drives it.
2. Core members check it against geography, factions, timeline, and existing canon.
3. The room tests for contradiction and for whether the fact is necessary or merely decorative.
4. Consensus is sought; the chair ([lore-master](../../02-worldbuilding/lore-master/)) breaks deadlocks.
5. Approved canon, with any dissent, is recorded before it may be asserted in published text.

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: T2–T3 canon additions and reconciliations.
- Must escalate: T4 and any addition conflicting with a Kernel Law or the studio's creative direction — one level up via [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot resolve, or canon disputes that touch creative direction,
escalate to the [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) and, if needed, the
[ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/) per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto halts approval until
a human maintainer overrides it.
