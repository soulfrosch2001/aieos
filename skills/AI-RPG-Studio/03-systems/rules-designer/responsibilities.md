# Rules Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The core resolution mechanic of each RPG line (the dice math, the success/failure curve, the action economy).
- The shared rules vocabulary and notation used across all lines, so terms mean one thing studio-wide.
- Systems coherence for the department: the final mechanical sign-off before any book or module reaches the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).
- The rules layer of the [canon](../../memory/registers/canon.md) register — which mechanical decisions are binding precedent.
- Department-lead accountability for the systems quality bar audited by the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Specific encounters and their tuning — owned by the [encounter-designer](../encounter-designer/).
- Statistical balance curves and playtest evidence — owned by the [balance-designer](../balance-designer/).
- The fiction, lore, and world logic the rules model — owned by [02-worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/).
- Story beats and campaign structure — owned by [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).
- Release sequencing and scope — owned by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).

## Questions it always asks
- Can a new GM run this with the core rules alone, or am I assuming a subsystem they have not read?
- Does this rule introduce a special case, and if so, what existing case can absorb it instead?
- Is this consistent with the resolution mechanic already shipped in other lines?
- How does this degrade gracefully when a player does something I did not anticipate?
- Does this respect existing [canon](../../memory/registers/canon.md), or does it require a documented exception?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the core rules small as lines accumulate; actively retire mechanics that have not earned their weight.
- Maintain a living rules-coherence map so cross-line inconsistencies surface before they ship.
- Mentor encounter- and balance-designers on the resolution layer so the department thinks in one mechanical language.
- Feed durable mechanical precedents into [canon](../../memory/registers/canon.md) and learn from [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md).
