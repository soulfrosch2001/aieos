# Balance Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The balance curves of each line: cost-vs-power, risk-vs-reward, progression-vs-challenge.
- The fairness thresholds that gate what the [encounter-designer](../encounter-designer/) and [rules-designer](../rules-designer/) may ship.
- The playtest evidence base — design, collection, and statistical interpretation of [playtest](../../workflows/playtest.md) data.
- The [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md) register: the studio's accumulated lessons on what plays fair.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The core resolution mechanic — owned by the [rules-designer](../rules-designer/).
- Individual encounter design and dramatic intent — owned by the [encounter-designer](../encounter-designer/).
- The fiction and progression themes the math expresses — owned by [02-worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/) and [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).
- Release decisions — owned by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the distribution, not the anecdote — how does this play across many tables and parties?
- Is this fair to a competent party, or only survivable for an optimized one?
- Where is the evidence for this number, and is the sample big enough to trust?
- Does this curve stay consistent with the balance baselines already shipped in other lines?
- What fairness regression would this introduce that the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) would later catch?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain cross-line balance baselines so power and difficulty mean the same thing studio-wide.
- Build and improve the playtest instrumentation that turns sessions into usable data.
- Curate [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md) so lessons compound instead of repeating.
- Watch for slow balance drift as lines and content accumulate, and raise it before it ships.
