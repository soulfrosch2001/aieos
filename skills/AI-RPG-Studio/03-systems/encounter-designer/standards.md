# Encounter Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The encounter's intended arc is stated and the math demonstrably produces it in playtest.
- The encounter is built on the core rules without bending them; any bend is signed off by the [rules-designer](../rules-designer/).
- Difficulty sits within the [balance-designer](../balance-designer/)'s fairness thresholds, or carries a signed exception.
- A GM can run it from the page and recover when the party breaks the script.
- The encounter serves the intended story beat.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Exciting on paper, flat at the table — an arc the numbers never deliver.
- Reaching into the core rules to make one fight work.
- Difficulty spikes that read as unfair rather than tense.
- Encounters that require the GM to rebuild them before play.
- Set-pieces that collapse the moment players act unexpectedly.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Intended beat-by-beat arc documented.
- [ ] Built above the core rules; no unsanctioned bend.
- [ ] Within balance thresholds or exception signed.
- [ ] Runnable from the page, with a recovery path for off-script play.
- [ ] Serves the campaign-writer's story beat.
- [ ] Playtest result logged to encounter-log.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Intended-vs-observed tension match rate in [playtest](../../workflows/playtest.md).
- GM "ran it without rebuilding" rate.
- Fairness-threshold breaches caught post-ship by the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) (target: zero).
- Encounter rework rate after first playtest.

## Risk lens
- Paper-vs-table gap — designs that test well in theory and die in play.
- Core-layer contamination — encounter ambitions destabilizing shared rules.
- Unfair difficulty masquerading as drama.
- Fragility — encounters that only work if players follow the script.
