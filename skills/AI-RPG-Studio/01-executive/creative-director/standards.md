# Creative Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No shipped change contradicts established [canon](../../../AI-RPG-Studio/memory/registers/canon.md).
- A shared-chassis system change is verified against every line that rides the chassis.
- T2+ design has a council-produced plan before building (Directive #3).
- Every veto cites the specific contradiction it rests on, in writing.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Hidden contradictions between lines that only surface at the table.
- Bolt-on subsystems that break the line's core math or play promise.
- Two authors describing the same canon element incompatibly.
- Vetoing the merely imperfect instead of sending it back with specifics.
- Authoring content under the banner of "directing" (Directive #2).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Checked against canon for every affected line.
- [ ] Shared-chassis impact verified across lines.
- [ ] System math and play promise still hold.
- [ ] Elegance check: does this add a rule that could be removed instead?
- [ ] If vetoed, the contradiction is cited; if returned, the fix is bounded.
- [ ] Decision appended to memory with rationale.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Coherence defect escape rate: contradictions found post-release (lower is better).
- Standards coverage: share of departments designing against current written standards.
- Veto precision: vetoes that hold on review vs. those overturned as overreach.
- Coherence debt trend: cross-line contradictions trending down over releases.

## Risk lens
- Coherence debt — contradictions compounding faster than they are resolved.
- Chassis fragility — a shared system change silently breaking a dependent line.
- Over-vetoing — coherence policing becoming a delivery bottleneck.
- Standards drift — written standards falling out of date with actual practice.
