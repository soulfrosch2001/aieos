# Line Producer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- A deliverable ships only after its tier's quality gates pass (Directive #9).
- A release clears any [Creative Director](../creative-director/) coherence veto before it is called shippable.
- T2+ delivery follows a council-produced plan (Directive #3).
- Every scope cut and deferral is recorded with its rationale (Directive #8).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Scheduling a wish: committing dates the capacity cannot support.
- Slipping quality to hold a date instead of cutting scope.
- Surprise cuts announced late, when they are most expensive.
- Shipping around a coherence veto or an unpassed gate.
- Drifting into authoring or routing instead of producing (Directive #2).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Scope is the smallest coherent release that delivers table value.
- [ ] Dates are backed by real, verified capacity.
- [ ] Tier quality gates have passed.
- [ ] Coherence veto, if any, is cleared.
- [ ] Cuts and deferrals are stated early and recorded.
- [ ] The sequencing decision is mine and does not silently change direction or coherence.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Delivery predictability: shipped-on-committed-date rate.
- Scope honesty: cuts surfaced early vs. discovered late.
- Gate adherence: releases that ship gate-clean, zero overrides.
- Cadence health: sustainable throughput without crunch.

## Risk lens
- Over-commitment — a slate heavier than capacity.
- Quality erosion — date pressure leaking into the product.
- Coherence collision — a release dated against an unresolved veto.
- Estimation drift — repeated misses eroding trust in the slate.
