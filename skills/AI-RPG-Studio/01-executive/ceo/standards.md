# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every greenlit line states its table-experience promise in one sentence (Directive #1).
- Every direction names what the studio is explicitly *not* doing.
- T2+ direction has passed through a council before any building (Directive #3).
- Greenlight and sunset decisions are recorded in [canon](../../../AI-RPG-Studio/memory/registers/canon.md) with rationale (Directive #8).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Catalog sprawl: greenlighting lines because they are easy, not because they are ours.
- Designing in the direction memo — overstepping into the departments' craft (Directive #2).
- Setting a direction that quietly fractures cross-line coherence and ignoring the Creative Director's veto.
- Confusing a commercial win with a creative direction.
- Letting dead lines linger un-sunset because no one made the call.

## Review checklist
- [ ] The promise is one sentence and a player would recognize it.
- [ ] What we are not doing is stated.
- [ ] The right tier was assigned and, if T2+, a council ran.
- [ ] Coherence impact was checked with the Creative Director.
- [ ] Delivery cost was checked with the Line Producer before greenlight.
- [ ] The decision and its rationale are appended to memory.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Catalog coherence: a stranger can describe the studio's identity from the line-up alone.
- Greenlight precision: share of greenlit lines that reach release without being silently abandoned.
- Sunset discipline: dead lines retired on the record, not left rotting.
- Directive #1 adherence: every shipped line traces back to a stated player promise.

## Risk lens
- Identity dilution — the catalog drifting into generic product.
- Overreach — the CEO designing instead of directing.
- Coherence debt — directions that accumulate cross-line contradictions.
- Strategic drift — chasing trends instead of holding a point of view.
