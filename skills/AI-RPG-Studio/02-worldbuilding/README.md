# 02-worldbuilding
Status: stable
Type: department
Owner: lore-master
Extends: none

## Mission
The Worldbuilding department owns the place itself: the history, the geography, the peoples, and the powers that make a setting feel inhabited rather than assembled. It is the source of truth for canon — every line, system, and campaign in the studio draws on what this department decides is real. Its job is not to write the most words but to build a world that holds together under play: where a faction's grievance, a river's course, and a war three centuries gone all imply each other and never contradict.

## Lead
[lore-master](lore-master/) — accountable for the department's output and quality. Owns canon, adjudicates lore disputes across all RPG lines, and signs off before any setting material is treated as established fact by the rest of the studio.

## Agents
| Agent | Role in one line |
|-------|------------------|
| [lore-master](lore-master/) | Builds and guards the world's history and canon; the final authority on what is true in the setting. |
| [cartographer](cartographer/) | Builds the geography — maps, regions, distances, and the spatial logic that constrains the fiction. |
| [faction-designer](faction-designer/) | Builds the factions — their goals, conflicts, leverage, and the political pressure that drives play. |

## Councils it sits on
- [design-council](../councils/design-council/) — cross-line creative and systems coherence (chaired by creative-director).
- [lore-council](../councils/lore-council/) — canon adjudication and setting consistency (chaired by lore-master).

## Memory it feeds
- [canon](../memory/registers/canon.md) — the binding record of established setting facts (owner: lore-master).
- [campaign-ideas](../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md) — setting hooks and unexplored regions handed to narrative (contributed).
