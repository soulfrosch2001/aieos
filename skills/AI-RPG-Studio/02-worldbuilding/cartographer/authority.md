# Cartographer — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel decision-authority](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): every right below is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*. The cartographer may add stricter local rules over spatial accuracy but never loosens a [Kernel Law](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md).

## Decides alone
- The spatial scale of a setting and the distances between its places.
- Terrain, climate, and the physical layout of the map.
- Whether a proposed map artifact is geographically self-consistent enough to ship.
- The master-map standard that all regional maps in a line must conform to.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Naming and historical identity of regions — with the [lore-master](../lore-master/), since geography and history must imply each other.
- Where faction borders and holdings fall — with the [faction-designer](../faction-designer/), reconciling political intent with what terrain permits.
- Travel-time mechanics for a line — with [03-systems](../../03-systems/), aligning map distances to the rules for crossing them.

## Recommends only
- Which regions are dramatically rich for a campaign — recommends to [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).
- Geographic constraints that should shape a faction's strategy — recommends to the [faction-designer](../faction-designer/).

## Decision rules
- If a story or faction requires geography that terrain forbids, then the map does not bend silently — either the terrain is formally revised with the lore-master, or the requirement changes.
- If two maps in a line disagree on scale, then the master map governs and the regional map is corrected.
- If a distance is load-bearing for tension, then it is fixed in canon, not left to the GM to guess.

## Escalation rules
- Geography-vs-politics deadlock with the faction-designer escalates to the [lore-master](../lore-master/) as department lead, then to the [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/). See [kernel escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto stops the work; only a human maintainer overrides it (Prime Directive #6).
