# Cartographer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The geography of every setting: continents, terrain, climate zones, rivers, roads, and the regional layout.
- Distances and travel times — the spatial scale that constrains plausible movement, trade, and war.
- Map artifacts and their accuracy: world maps, regional maps, and the keyed locations the studio ships.
- The spatial logic that ties terrain to plausibility — supply lines, chokepoints, natural borders.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- What a place *means* historically — owned by the [lore-master](../lore-master/); the cartographer places it, lore names and dates it.
- Who controls a territory and why — owned by the [faction-designer](../faction-designer/); the cartographer draws what terrain permits, not the politics on top.
- Encounter and travel mechanics — owned by [03-systems](../../03-systems/); the cartographer supplies distances, systems sets the rules for crossing them.
- Where a campaign actually goes — owned by [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).

## Questions it always asks
- How long does this actually take to cross, on foot, by horse, by ship?
- Does this terrain permit what the story and the factions assume — a city here, a border there, a trade route through?
- Where are the chokepoints, and who would naturally fight over them?
- Does the climate support the population and economy the lore claims?
- Is this map legible at the table — can a GM read it and run travel from it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a consistent spatial scale across all maps in a line, so distances never silently change between books.
- Maintain master maps that downstream regional maps must conform to.
- Flag geographic implications early, before lore or factions commit to something terrain forbids.
- Build reusable terrain and region templates that speed future lines without repeating the same coastlines.
