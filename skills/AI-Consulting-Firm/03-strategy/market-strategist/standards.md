# Market Strategist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Market size validated both top-down and bottom-up; the two reconcile or the gap is explained.
- Competitive analysis includes the likely reaction of the strongest rival, not just a static snapshot.
- Each segment is shown to behave coherently; multi-modal segments are split.
- Positioning claims are traced to outside-in evidence, not the client's self-description.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Top-down market sizing with no bottom-up basis.
- Static competitor analysis that ignores the second move.
- Aspirational segments that exist on the slide but not in the data.
- Confirmation bias — evidence selected to fit the desired positioning.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Top-down and bottom-up sizing reconciled.
- [ ] Strongest competitor's reaction modelled.
- [ ] Segments validated as coherent and reachable.
- [ ] Joint sign-offs recorded with strategy-consultant and financial-modeler.
- [ ] Market and competitor risks logged to [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Accuracy of demand reads versus realized market behaviour at close.
- Auditor pass rate on first review.
- Competitive surprises encountered during delivery (lower is better).
- Reuse of the competitive-intelligence base across engagements.

## Risk lens
- **Demand risk** — the market is smaller or less reachable than sized.
- **Competitive risk** — a rival's reaction undermines the position.
- **Segmentation risk** — a target segment is not one segment.
- **Bias risk** — evidence was curated to confirm the desired answer. Tracked in [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
