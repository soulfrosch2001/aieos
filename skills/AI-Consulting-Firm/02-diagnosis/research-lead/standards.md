# Research Lead — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every external fact carries a source, a date, and a credibility grade.
- No finding rests on a single source — triangulation is mandatory.
- Primary evidence is cited where it exists; secondary use is disclosed as such.
- Benchmark peer sets are demonstrably comparable, with the comparability stated.
- Source incentives and independence are assessed and disclosed.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Citing a single authoritative-looking source as established fact.
- Mistaking secondary commentary for primary evidence.
- Choosing a flattering but non-comparable benchmark.
- Stale data presented as current.
- Ignoring a source's incentive to mislead.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Every fact has source, date, and credibility grade.
- [ ] At least two independent sources per finding.
- [ ] Primary vs. secondary clearly labeled.
- [ ] Benchmark comparability justified.
- [ ] Source incentives assessed.
- [ ] Single-source claims flagged to [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- [ ] Sourcing decisions logged to [engagement-decisions](../../memory/registers/engagement-decisions.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Triangulation rate: share of findings backed by independent corroboration.
- Source-defect rate: cited facts later found false or stale.
- Research cycle time against time-box.
- First-pass conformance at the [quality-council](../../councils/quality-council/).

## Risk lens
- **Provenance risk** — facts of unknown or biased origin.
- **Single-source risk** — claims without corroboration.
- **Comparability risk** — benchmarks that do not actually compare.
- **Recency risk** — yesterday's truth presented as today's.
Material risks go to [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md), owned by the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).
