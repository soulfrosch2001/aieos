# Change Manager — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- A current stakeholder map exists, with each key player's stance and a move plan.
- A named, active executive sponsor exists — spending capital, not just lending a title.
- Adoption is defined as a measurable behavior, with a baseline and a target, not as training attendance.
- A capability-transfer plan ensures client roles and skills survive our exit.
- Resistance has been surfaced and diagnosed before go-live, not after.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Mistaking communication volume for adoption.
- A "sponsor" who lends a logo but spends no political capital.
- Treating resistance as obstruction when it is sometimes a correct warning.
- Designing change at the client's leadership pace and ignoring the frontline's.
- Leaving with no client owner for sustaining the change.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Stakeholder map current; each key player has a stance and a move plan.
- [ ] Active sponsor named and visibly engaged.
- [ ] Adoption defined behaviorally, with baseline and target.
- [ ] Resistance logged and diagnosed; legitimate concerns escalated.
- [ ] Training tied to on-the-job ability, not just sessions delivered.
- [ ] Adoption risks logged in [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- [ ] Adoption plan reconciled with the [implementation-lead](../implementation-lead/README.md)'s timeline.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Sustained-adoption rate at go-live, at handover, and 90 days after.
- Stakeholder movement: share of key players moved to support or neutral.
- Sponsor engagement (active sponsorship actions vs. committed).
- Client self-sufficiency: change owned and sustained without us at exit.

## Risk lens
- **Adoption risk** — the change is built but not used.
- **Sponsorship risk** — no one with power is truly backing it.
- **Resistance risk** — unaddressed or, worse, correct and ignored.
- **Capacity risk** — change pace outruns the org's ability to absorb it.
- **Sustainability risk** — the change reverses once we leave.
