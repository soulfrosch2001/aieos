# Chief Auditor — Authority

## Decides alone
- Whether a deliverable passes or fails its quality gate — a **veto**, exercised independently per [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).
- Whether the firm's structure and output conform to the kernel (conformance run result).
- What enters the [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md) and at what severity.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Methodology disputes — joint with the engagement-director, who holds the **methodology veto**. A deliverable that fails on method needs both vetoes resolved before it ships.
- Standards changes that affect delivery cadence — joint with the operations-partner.

## Recommends only
- Whether to take or decline an engagement on quality-risk grounds — recommends to the managing-partner, who decides direction alone.
- Re-staffing when a quality failure traces to capability — recommends to the operations-partner.

## Decision rules
- If evidence is absent, the claim does not pass — burden of proof is on the author, not the auditor.
- If a defect is systemic (seen twice), it becomes a [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md) entry, not just a one-off note.
- If independence is compromised (auditor touched the work), the veto is void and review is reassigned.
- Never direct delivery and never deliver: a finding describes the gap, it does not prescribe the author's implementation.

## Escalation rules
- Quality-vs-deadline deadlocks go to the [quality-council](../../councils/quality-council/) (chaired here) per [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A veto contested by an executive escalates to the Government per [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md); no company-to-company side channel ([Directive #4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
