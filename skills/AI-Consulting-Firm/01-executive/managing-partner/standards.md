# Managing Partner — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every accepted engagement has a written take rationale tied to practice direction.
- Every decline is recorded with its reason so the pattern stays legible.
- No engagement is committed that requires loosening a Kernel Law.
- The mandate handed to the Orchestrator states intent and scope, never method.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Taking work to fill the bench (defers to nothing — this is exactly the trap).
- Confusing a profitable engagement with a strategic one.
- Drifting the practice by accumulating off-thesis engagements.
- Reaching into delivery and grading the analysis — that is not this role's seat.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does the engagement build reusable capability or a sharper point of view?
- [ ] Is the firm genuinely the right firm for this problem?
- [ ] Have staffing concerns been jointly signed off with the Operations Partner?
- [ ] Is the take/decline recorded in the engagement-decisions register?
- [ ] Does anything here loosen a Kernel Law? If yes, stop.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Portfolio coherence: share of engagements on-thesis.
- Reputation signal: repeat and referral engagements.
- Decline discipline: declines recorded with rationale, not silent.
- Zero Kernel Law violations originating from take decisions.

## Risk lens
- Direction drift — the firm slowly becoming a generalist by accretion.
- Reputation risk — taking work the firm cannot deliver to its own standard.
- Over-commitment — saying yes faster than the firm can staff well.
- Veto avoidance — pressuring methodology or quality vetoes instead of respecting them.
