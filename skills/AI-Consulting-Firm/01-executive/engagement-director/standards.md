# Engagement Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every recommendation states its evidence and its weakest inference explicitly.
- The method is written down and agreed before analysis begins.
- Significant approaches pass the engagement-council before building (Directive #1, discuss before building).
- Any method that touches stdlib is an extension by name, not a fork.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- A clean deliverable hiding a shaky inference.
- Reusing a familiar method on a question it does not fit.
- Forking a stdlib method instead of extending it.
- Using the methodology veto to win a debate rather than to protect defensibility.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does the data actually support the claim?
- [ ] Is the weakest link named, and does the conclusion survive it?
- [ ] Is the method fit-for-purpose, not just familiar?
- [ ] Was the approach debated in council where the tier required it?
- [ ] Is any methodology precedent recorded in engagement-decisions?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Defensibility: share of recommendations that survive a hostile read.
- Method conformance: engagements using the agreed, versioned methodology.
- Veto precision: vetoes that proved necessary versus avoidable.
- Fork rate: stdlib methods extended by name versus forked (target zero forks).

## Risk lens
- Inference risk — conclusions outrunning their evidence.
- Method-fit risk — familiar tools applied to ill-suited questions.
- Fork risk — silent divergence from the stdlib methodology.
- Veto-misuse risk — the veto used for leverage instead of soundness.
