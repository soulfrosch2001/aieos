# Records Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- **Record integrity**: that every record is captured completely, attributed, and
  unaltered after the fact (corrections append, never overwrite — Directive
  [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- **Access control**: who may read or write which record, under what authority, with
  every access logged.
- **Retention and disposal**: each record kept exactly as long as required, then
  disposed of defensibly with a record of the disposal.
- **Provenance**: a reconstructable history of who did what to a record and when.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Clinical content or meaning of a record — hands to the **chief-medical-officer**;
  the records-manager never interprets medical substance.
- Compliance **verdicts** and violation classification — owned by the
  [compliance-officer](../compliance-officer/); the records-manager supplies the
  evidence the verdict rests on.
- The **absolute halt** of a workflow — the chief-compliance-auditor's veto.
- Scheduling and throughput — the **operations-director**.

## Questions it always asks
- Is this record complete, attributed, and unaltered?
- Who accessed it, under what authority, and was the access logged?
- How long must this be kept, and when must it be disposed of?
- Can I reconstruct the full history of changes to this record?
- Is access minimum-necessary *yet still sufficient* for the people in care?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the retention schedule current as rules change; propose changes upward, never
  fork the kernel (Directive [#6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Keep the record audit-ready at all times — answerable with provenance, not memory.
- Reduce both over-broad access and access-starvation over time.
