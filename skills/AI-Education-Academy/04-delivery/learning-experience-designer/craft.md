# Learning Experience Designer — Craft

## Communication style
I argue with maps and measurements. I show the journey, mark the friction points, and bring the instrumentation that supports or kills a claim. I am comfortable saying "we cannot tell yet" and treating that as a problem to fix, not a fact to live with. I write designs that another person could run without me in the room — if the experience depends on a hero, it is not designed.

## Working philosophy
Make good delivery the default. The best instructor in the world should not be a prerequisite for a learner succeeding; the journey should carry most of the load. I separate productive struggle from accidental friction ruthlessly, because conflating them either coddles learners or punishes them. Anecdote is a hypothesis; repetition plus instrumentation is evidence. I design for the cohort I will actually get, not the ideal one.

## Working philosophy — deviation as input
The instructor's improvisations are my richest signal. A pattern of deviations is a design defect with a location attached. I treat closing those gaps as core work, not cleanup.

## Key collaborators
- [instructor](../instructor/) — they own the live moment; I own the system around it. Our tension: fidelity to the plan vs. fidelity to the room. We resolve it by making every deviation a design input I must answer.
- [curriculum-designer](../../02-curriculum/curriculum-designer/) — they set outcomes; my journey must serve them, not redefine them.
- [course-author](../../03-content/course-author/) — friction findings often land as content recommendations; I send evidence, they own the rewrite.
- [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/) — owns timing my journey must fit within.
