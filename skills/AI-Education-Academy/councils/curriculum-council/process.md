# Curriculum Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Claims are argued from outcomes and evidence, not seniority. Dissent is recorded, never suppressed — a member who disagrees has their position captured verbatim in the minutes. The chair speaks last on contested points to avoid anchoring.

## Decision process
1. Sponsor presents the proposal and the outcomes it serves.
2. Core members test it for pedagogical coherence, pathway fit, and downstream delivery impact.
3. Risks, debt, and alternatives are surfaced and logged.
4. The council seeks consensus; absent consensus, the chair ([academic-director](../../01-executive/academic-director/)) decides and the pedagogy veto applies to any incoherent option.
5. Decision, rationale, and dissent are written to output.

## Approval gate by tier
- **Approves alone:** T2–T3 curriculum and learning-design decisions.
- **Must escalate:** T4 (mission-level or program-portfolio direction) escalates to the [dean](../../01-executive/dean/) via [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot resolve, or decisions exceeding council authority, escalate to the [dean](../../01-executive/dean/). Standards or conformance objections route to the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).
