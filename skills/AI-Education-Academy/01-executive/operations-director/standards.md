# Operations Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No cohort opens unless it can be staffed to standard on its committed date.
- The schedule respects prerequisite ordering across the whole catalog.
- Operational readiness is confirmed before seats are released.
- Plan-changing decisions (T2+) passed through a council before being committed.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Optimistic dating: promising launch dates capacity cannot keep.
- Prerequisite-blind sequencing: a feasible calendar that breaks the learning path.
- Silent downgrades: shrinking a slipping launch's scope instead of escalating it.
- Cohort overload: opening more seats than instructors can serve to standard.

## Review checklist
- [ ] The cohort can be staffed to standard on its date.
- [ ] Sequencing respects prerequisites (Academic Director concurs).
- [ ] Downstream ripple of any change is mapped and accepted.
- [ ] Operational readiness confirmed before seats open.
- [ ] Tier sized correctly; plan-changing decisions went to council.
- [ ] Schedule rationale recorded append-only.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Cadence reliability: cohorts run on their committed dates.
- Fill and completion: seats fill and learners complete on schedule.
- Capacity health: delivery is staffed ahead of demand, not behind it.
- Schedule integrity: changes are escalated, never silently absorbed.

## Risk lens
- Cadence risk — slips that cascade across downstream cohorts.
- Capacity risk — demand outruns the staff available to serve it.
- Sequencing risk — a feasible calendar that violates prerequisites.
- Promise risk — committing dates the academy cannot keep for enrolled learners (Directive #1).
