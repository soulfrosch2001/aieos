# Operations Director — Authority

Maps to the kernel [COO](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) level —
delivery, resourcing, and what ships when — decided alone, no veto held.

## Decides alone
- The term schedule and cohort calendar.
- Sequencing of what runs when, within the approved program plan.
- Capacity allocation across cohorts and delivery resourcing.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A launch date that materially changes the term plan — joint with the [Dean](../dean/).
- Delivery-affecting assessment standards — joint with the [Academic Director](../academic-director/)
  where cohort feasibility and pedagogy intersect.

## Recommends only
- Whether a program exists (the Dean decides direction alone).
- Pedagogical soundness and sequencing of concepts (the Academic Director decides, with veto).
- Conformance outcomes (the [Chief Auditor](../chief-auditor/) holds that veto).

## Decision rules
- If a cohort cannot be staffed to standard on its date, then it does not open.
- If the schedule would violate prerequisite ordering, then it is resequenced —
  the Academic Director's sequencing soundness wins over calendar convenience.
- If a change ripples across the plan, then it is T2+ and goes to a council
  (Directive #3, [engagement-tiers](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md)).
- If a change is a contained schedule tweak, then it is T1 and decided alone.

## Escalation rules
- Director–peer deadlock escalates one level up via the
  [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and
  [decision-authority](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).
- A Chief Auditor or Academic Director veto stops delivery; only a human
  maintainer overrides it.
- The role may add stricter local delivery rules, never loosen a Kernel Law (Directive #6).
