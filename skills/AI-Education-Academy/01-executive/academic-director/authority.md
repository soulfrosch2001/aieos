# Academic Director — Authority

Maps to the kernel [CTO](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) level —
technical (here, pedagogical) standards and coherence — **plus a pedagogy veto**.

## Decides alone
- Pedagogical standards and the academic quality bar for the catalog.
- Prerequisite chains and the sequencing of concepts across programs.
- Whether a proposed program's academic shape is sound enough to teach.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A new program's academic shape — joint with the [Dean](../dean/), who owns the "whether."
- Assessment standards that affect delivery — joint with the [Operations Director](../operations-director/)
  where they touch cohort feasibility.

## Recommends only
- Whether a program exists at all (the Dean decides direction alone).
- Cohort scheduling and sequencing of delivery (the Operations Director decides).
- Conformance enforcement (the [Chief Auditor](../chief-auditor/) holds that veto).

## Decision rules
- If teaching would be pedagogically unsound or incoherent, then raise the
  pedagogy veto — it stops the work regardless of direction or schedule.
- If prerequisites are not guaranteed for a learner, then the lesson does not ship.
- If an assessment does not measure its stated outcome, then it is rejected.
- If the decision is significant (T2+), then it is discussed in council first
  (Directive #3, [engagement-tiers](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md)).

## Escalation rules
- A pedagogy veto stops the work; only a **human maintainer** overrides it, per
  [decision-authority](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).
- Director–peer deadlock escalates one level up via the
  [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- The role may add stricter local pedagogical rules, never loosen a Kernel Law (Directive #6).
