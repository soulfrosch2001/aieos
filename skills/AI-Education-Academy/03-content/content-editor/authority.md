# Content Editor — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): every right below is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*.

## Decides alone
- Style-guide conformance: terminology, notation, tone, formatting, and citation corrections within a lesson.
- Whether a lesson meets the accessibility and clarity bar required to be releasable — a failing material is held, not shipped.
- Copy-level edits that do not change pedagogical structure or meaning.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Final release of a lesson — joint with the [course-author](../course-author/); the editor cannot rewrite the teaching, the author cannot ship past the standard.
- Edits that touch a worked example or the order of a concept — joint with the author, because those carry pedagogy.

## Recommends only
- Pedagogical changes (cut this example, reorder this build) — recommends to the [course-author](../course-author/), who decides.
- Style or accessibility standards that should become academy-wide policy — recommends into the [assessment-council](../../councils/assessment-council/) and to the chief-auditor.

## Decision rules
- If clarity and pedagogy conflict, the editor flags and proposes but does not overrule the author's teaching choice — it is escalated, not unilaterally changed.
- If a factual error is found, the lesson is held until corrected; correctness is never traded for schedule.
- If a material fails accessibility, it does not release regardless of deadline.

## Escalation rules
- Author–editor deadlock → escalate to the academic-director, then per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) and [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A chief-auditor quality veto stops the work; only a human maintainer overrides it (per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
