# Media Producer — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): every right below is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*.

## Decides alone
- The visual execution of an approved brief: composition, layout, color, typography, timing, and audio treatment.
- Whether an asset meets production and source-accessibility quality to be releasable — a substandard asset is reworked, not shipped.
- Asset versioning, naming, and library organization.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Whether a concept warrants a media asset and the content of its brief — joint with the [course-author](../course-author/); the producer can decline a brief that adds load without understanding.
- Accessibility and consistency conformance of a released asset — reviewed jointly with the [content-editor](../content-editor/), who holds the material-wide standard.

## Recommends only
- The pedagogical content of an asset (what concept, what example) — recommends; the [course-author](../course-author/) owns the teaching.
- New media formats or a visual-system change at academy scale — recommends to the lead and the academic-director.

## Decision rules
- If a requested asset is redundant with the prose or another asset, the producer declines and proposes the leaner option, escalating only if the author insists.
- If an asset cannot be made accessible at the source, it is reworked or replaced — never shipped inaccessible.
- T2 work (a new format, a significant production with design choices) goes to council before production begins (Directive #3).

## Escalation rules
- Author–producer deadlock on whether/what to produce → escalate to the department lead ([course-author](../course-author/)), then to the academic-director per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) and [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A chief-auditor quality veto stops the asset; only a human maintainer overrides it.
