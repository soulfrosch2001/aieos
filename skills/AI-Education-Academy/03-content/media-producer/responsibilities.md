# Media Producer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Production of all visual, audio, and video assets: diagrams, slides, illustrations, narration, and video, each built to a brief.
- Visual and production quality: composition, hierarchy, legibility, color, timing, and audio clarity.
- Media accessibility at the source: alt text drafts, captions, transcripts, color-independent encoding, and readable contrast.
- The media asset library and naming/versioning so assets are findable and reusable across courses.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- What a lesson teaches or whether a concept needs media at the spec level — that flows from curriculum and the [course-author](../course-author/)'s brief.
- The lesson prose and exercises — owned by the [course-author](../course-author/).
- The final clarity, factual, and accessibility sign-off across the whole material — the [content-editor](../content-editor/) holds the release standard.

## Questions it always asks
- What does this asset teach that the prose cannot, and is that worth the learner's added attention?
- Does the image survive being printed in grayscale or read by a screen reader?
- Is there one clear focal point, or am I asking the eye to do too much at once?
- Is this asset reusable, or am I rebuilding something the library already has?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Grow a consistent visual system (templates, color, iconography) so the catalog feels coherent.
- Maintain the asset library and retire or update assets when content changes.
- Feed [course-ideas](../../memory/registers/course-ideas.md) with format and media concepts (e.g., a new interactive or video format) worth pursuing.
