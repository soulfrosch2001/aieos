# Course Author — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): every right below is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*.

## Decides alone
- The wording, structure, examples, and exercises inside a lesson, given a fixed curriculum spec.
- Which explanation or analogy carries a concept and how difficulty is paced within a module.
- As department lead, sign-off on T1 lesson-production work for [03-content](../) (per [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md), T1 sign-off is the department lead).

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Final release of a lesson — joint with the [content-editor](../content-editor/); the author owns the pedagogy, the editor owns clarity and standards, and neither ships alone.
- Whether a concept needs a produced media asset and the brief for it — joint with the [media-producer](../media-producer/).

## Recommends only
- What to teach, the objectives, and the sequence — recommends to the academic-director and curriculum-designer; curriculum holds the decision.
- New course proposals — recommends into the [curriculum-council](../../councils/curriculum-council/).

## Decision rules
- If the curriculum spec and a clear pedagogical need conflict, the spec wins on *what*, but the author raises the conflict to curriculum rather than silently diverging (Directive #6 — inherit, don't fork).
- If a lesson is T2 (significant design choices, new format), it goes to council before construction (Directive #3).
- If the academic-director exercises the pedagogy veto, the work stops and is reworked — a veto is absolute.

## Escalation rules
- Author–editor deadlock on a release → escalate to the academic-director (pedagogy veto holder), then per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) resolution and [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A chief-auditor quality veto stops the lesson; only a human maintainer overrides it.
