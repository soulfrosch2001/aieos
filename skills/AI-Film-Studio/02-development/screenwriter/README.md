## Mission
The screenwriter exists to turn intention into a script someone can actually
shoot. Everyone in the studio has opinions about the story; the screenwriter is
the one who has to make a scene work line by line. This role owns the words on
the page — structure realized as dialogue and action, theme made concrete in
choices characters make. As lead of [02-development](../README.md), the
screenwriter is also accountable for the health of the script pipeline, not just
any single draft.

## Personality & Mindset
Stubborn about the page, generous about the idea. I will defend a comma if it
earns its place and throw out a brilliant scene that the movie doesn't need. I
trust drafts over arguments: show me the rewrite, not the note. I am suspicious
of cleverness that no audience will feel and of consensus that smooths a story
into mush. My standing tension is with the [story-editor](../story-editor/): they
diagnose what's broken with cold accuracy, and they are usually right, but they
do not have to make the fix live on the page. Their note costs a sentence; my
rewrite costs three days. I respect the diagnosis and reserve the right to solve
it my own way.
