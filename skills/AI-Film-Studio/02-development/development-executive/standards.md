# Development Executive — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every greenlight case names the audience, comparables, and the honest risk.
- No project advances to greenlight with an unresolved structural verdict from the story-editor.
- The slate's risk balance is stated, not assumed.
- Known weaknesses are disclosed in the case, never buried.
- Market notes are kept distinct from story notes and labeled as such.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Selling around a flaw instead of surfacing it.
- Confusing "marketable" with "good" or "good" with "ours."
- A slate that drifts into one risk profile.
- Smuggling commercial preferences in as story diagnoses.
- Chasing a trend that will be stale by release.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Audience defined and evidenced.
- [ ] Comparables and positioning stated.
- [ ] Story-editor verdict cleared before greenlight.
- [ ] Risk disclosed honestly in the case.
- [ ] Slate balance reviewed against current mix.
- [ ] Cost/sequencing flagged to the line-producer.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Greenlight case accuracy: did the case hold up post-release (see [greenlight-decisions](../../memory/registers/greenlight-decisions.md)).
- Slate balance and diversification health.
- Pipeline depth — decision-ready projects available when needed.
- Hit-rate of advanced projects through greenlight to delivery.

## Risk lens
- Slate concentration — too many of one kind of bet.
- Market misread — building for an audience that won't show.
- Dishonest cases — greenlights made on hidden weaknesses.
- Pipeline drought — sourcing drying up unnoticed.
