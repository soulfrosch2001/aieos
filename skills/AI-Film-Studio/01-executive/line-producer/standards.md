# Line Producer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every greenlit film has a schedule, a budget envelope, and a delivery date before
  production begins.
- Sequencing across the slate is sustainable — no stacked crunch.
- Every creative note that moves a date carries a recorded cost and joint sign-off.
- The capacity read is current before the CEO commits the slate.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Promising a date the studio cannot hold.
- Silently absorbing scope changes instead of pricing them.
- Sequencing that piles every crunch into one window.
- Drifting into directing or cutting the film instead of owning the calendar.
- Resourcing across companies without routing through the Government (Directive #4).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this film have a schedule, budget, and delivery date?
- [ ] Is the sequence sustainable across the whole slate?
- [ ] Are scope-driven date changes priced and jointly signed off?
- [ ] Is the capacity read reconciled with the CEO's slate?
- [ ] Is the lesson logged in production-log?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Delivery reliability: share of films shipped on committed dates.
- Budget adherence: variance against envelope.
- Cadence health: crunch windows kept non-overlapping.
- Cost transparency: share of scope changes with a recorded price.

## Risk lens
- Overcommitment — promising beyond capacity.
- Cadence collapse — stacked crunch burning the slate.
- Silent scope creep — unpriced creative changes eroding the schedule.
- Boundary breach — cross-company resourcing bypassing the Government.
