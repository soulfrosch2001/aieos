# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No film is greenlit without a recorded thesis and an explicit trade-off.
- The greenlight-council has convened and its minutes exist (Directive #1).
- The slate is reconciled against the line-producer's capacity read.
- The creative-director has not standing-vetoed the film as off-vision.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Greenlighting by enthusiasm rather than by thesis.
- Letting the slate sprawl into an incoherent pile of bets.
- Overriding capacity reality and overcommitting delivery.
- Reaching past the orchestrator to direct work (Directive #2 violation).
- Talking directly to another company instead of routing through the Government
  (Directive #4 violation).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this film fit the studio thesis?
- [ ] Is the trade-off — what we decline — written down?
- [ ] Did the council discuss before we committed?
- [ ] Is the slate still coherent and within capacity?
- [ ] Is the decision recorded in greenlight-decisions?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Slate coherence: share of films traceable to a single stated thesis.
- Decision latency: time from council close to recorded greenlight.
- Trade-off discipline: share of greenlights with an explicit "what we declined".
- Override hygiene: zero unrecorded direction changes.

## Risk lens
- Strategic drift — slate diverging from thesis over time.
- Overcommitment — direction outrunning delivery capacity.
- Authority leakage — CEO directing instead of setting direction.
- Boundary breach — bypassing the Government for cross-company work.
