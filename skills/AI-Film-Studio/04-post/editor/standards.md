# Editor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every scene reads: a first-time viewer always knows who, where, and what just
  happened — verified by a fresh-eyes screening, not by the people who made it.
- Picture lock is reproducible: the locked cut rebuilds frame-for-frame from a
  versioned timeline and a clean cut list.
- VFX hand-off is unambiguous: locked cut points, exact timecodes, and a frozen
  reference; no "we'll probably keep this" frames.
- The cut honours the greenlit film and survives the
  [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/README.md)'s veto.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Falling in love with a shot at the story's expense.
- Hidden pace problems — a film that is "fine" scene by scene but exhausting end
  to end.
- Moving lock points after VFX has started, silently breaking finished work.
- Cutting around a continuity error instead of flagging it.
- Confusing slow for profound.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Fresh-eyes screening passed; clarity notes resolved or logged.
- [ ] Timeline versioned; lock rebuilds deterministically from the cut list.
- [ ] VFX/sound/grade hand-off specs complete and frozen.
- [ ] Structural changes priced with the line-producer; lock date intact or
      re-agreed.
- [ ] Creative veto cleared; dissent (if any) recorded to
      [production-log](../../memory/registers/production-log.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Lock hit on the agreed date without late structural churn.
- Number of post-lock cut changes (lower is better) — a proxy for commitment
  discipline.
- Clarity defects caught in the room vs. raised after delivery.

## Risk lens
- **Story risk** — the cut tells a different, weaker film than the one greenlit.
- **Pace risk** — cumulative drag invisible at the scene level.
- **Hand-off risk** — unstable locks that waste downstream VFX, sound, and grade.
- **Continuity risk** — errors smoothed over rather than surfaced and fixed.
