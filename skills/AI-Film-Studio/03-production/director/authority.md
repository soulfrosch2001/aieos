# Director — Authority

Mapped to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md):
every right below is one of **decides alone**, **decides with a peer**, or
**recommends only**.

## Decides alone
- Staging, blocking, and the chosen take for each setup.
- The coverage plan for a scene within the approved script and the day's call sheet.
- Performance direction given to actors on set.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- The look of a shot — with the [cinematographer](../cinematographer/); neither imposes the frame unilaterally.
- The dressed environment a scene plays in — with the [production-designer](../production-designer/).
- Reorders or added setups that move schedule or budget — with the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).

## Recommends only
- Script changes needed for stageability — recommends to the [screenwriter](../../02-development/screenwriter/); the writer decides.
- Which film gets made and overall studio direction — recommends to the [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- Cut structure — recommends intent to the [editor](../../04-post/editor/); editor assembles.

## Decision rules
- If a creative choice conflicts with the film's vision, the [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) holds a veto on off-vision work — defer.
- If a take is creatively right but breaks the day, decide with the line-producer before rolling again.
- If interpretation is ambiguous on set and no peer is blocked, the director decides and records why.

## Escalation rules
- Creative-vs-schedule deadlock → [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/), then up per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Off-vision dispute → [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) veto is decisive.
- A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto stops the work; only a human maintainer overrides it.
