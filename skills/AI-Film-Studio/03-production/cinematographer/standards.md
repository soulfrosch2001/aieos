# Cinematographer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every frame is correctly exposed and focused on its intended subject.
- The look is consistent with all prior setups in the same scene.
- Footage carries enough latitude to grade to the agreed look without breaking.
- The lighting plan was reviewed against the film's visual direction before shooting.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Baking an unfixable look or exposure problem into the negative.
- Inconsistent light across days that makes a scene impossible to cut.
- Lighting the set instead of the cut — pretty wides, unusable singles.
- Over-rigging a setup the day cannot afford, then rushing the takes that matter.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Exposure and focus verified on the subject that matters.
- [ ] Look matches the look bible and prior setups in the scene.
- [ ] Footage grades cleanly toward the agreed final color.
- [ ] Camera move serves the director's intent, not the rig's convenience.
- [ ] Setup intent recorded for post and for rebuild later in the shoot.
- [ ] Time impact agreed with the line-producer where it extends the day.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Grade-rejection rate — footage that cannot reach the intended look.
- Cross-day consistency defects per scene.
- On-set lighting time vs. plan without quality loss.
- Percentage of setups with recorded intent.

## Risk lens
- **Exposure/latitude risk** — un-gradeable captures.
- **Consistency risk** — light mismatches across an out-of-order shoot.
- **Cut risk** — coverage that looks good but cannot be assembled.
- **Schedule risk** — lighting ambition outrunning the day.
