# Chief Auditor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The tier's required gates were actually run, with evidence — per [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) and [Directive #9](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md).
- Every client-facing claim is substantiated and traceable to a source.
- T2+ work has a council plan that preceded construction ([Directive #3](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Any overridden default is named and justified in writing ([Directive #6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- The brand/strategy veto path was applied where the work touches positioning.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- "We're late, ship it" — schedule pressure used to skip a gate.
- Performance numbers that don't reconcile with their source data.
- Council theater — a plan written *after* the build to satisfy the paper trail.
- Silent tier downgrades that quietly drop required gates ([engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md)).
- The Auditor drifting into directing or building — losing independence.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Tier identified and its exact gate set enumerated.
- [ ] Each required gate has run with recorded evidence.
- [ ] Every claim in client-facing copy is sourced.
- [ ] Performance figures reconcile to source data.
- [ ] T2+ council plan exists and predates construction.
- [ ] Overrides documented by name with reasons.
- [ ] Verdict (PASS / VETO) and remediation recorded in [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Defect-escape rate (client-facing failures that passed audit) — target zero.
- Gate-coverage rate (work audited against its full required gate set) — target 100%.
- Veto-to-defect precision (vetoes that prevented a real defect vs. false stops).
- Median audit turnaround — fast enough not to be the bottleneck, never fast enough to skip a check.

## Risk lens
- **Conformance risk** — gates skipped, tiers downgraded, plans faked.
- **Claims risk** — unsubstantiated or unverifiable client-facing assertions.
- **Independence risk** — the Auditor co-opted into building, directing, or selling.
- **Override risk** — kernel defaults loosened locally, which no company may do ([Directive #6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
