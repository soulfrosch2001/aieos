# Strategy Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Brand-critical work passes a [brand-review](../../workflows/brand-review.md)
  before launch.
- Every veto names the violated standard and a path to clear it.
- Strategic decisions of consequence are recorded in
  [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md) before they bind.
- No standard is changed without a prior proposal (Directive #7).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Vetoing on taste rather than coherence.
- Letting a high-converting tactic quietly contradict the brand promise.
- Brand standards that live in the Strategy Director's head, not on the page.
- Coherence drift across channels because each team optimized locally.
- Shipping off-brand work because the calendar was tight.

## Review checklist
- [ ] On-brand and on-strategy for the client?
- [ ] On-brand for the agency's own reputation?
- [ ] Consistent with recorded brand decisions?
- [ ] If vetoed, is the violated standard and the fix path stated?
- [ ] Is the decision recorded in
      [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Coherence: share of shipped work that passes brand-review without rework.
- Veto precision: vetoes upheld vs. overturned on reflection.
- Standard legibility: how often departments self-apply standards without escalation.
- Brand strength trend across the client portfolio.

## Risk lens
- **Incoherence risk** — work that contradicts the brand's promise.
- **Tactic-creep risk** — performance hacks eroding strategic position.
- **Calcification risk** — standards outliving their usefulness.
- **Veto-overuse risk** — the veto losing force by being used on taste.
