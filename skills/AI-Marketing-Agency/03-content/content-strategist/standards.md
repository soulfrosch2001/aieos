# Content Strategist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Traceable to strategy: every piece maps to a messaging pillar and an approved positioning.
- One-sentence argument: the content arc's claim can be stated in a single sentence.
- On-voice: passes the voice-and-tone guide, not just the editor's taste.
- Sequenced: the piece has a place in the calendar and a reason it sits there.
- Tier-correct sign-off: T1 signed by me; T2+ run through [campaign-council](../../councils/campaign-council/) before build (Directive #3).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Volume theater — filling the calendar with pieces that say nothing.
- Voice drift — small off-voice allowances that accumulate into an incoherent brand.
- Strategy laundering — content that claims a position the brand has not actually earned.
- Briefing the writer with a vibe instead of a constraint, then rejecting the result.
- Letting "it's really good" override "it's off-brief."

## Review checklist
- [ ] Maps to an approved positioning and a named messaging pillar.
- [ ] Arc argument stated in one sentence.
- [ ] Passes the voice-and-tone guide.
- [ ] Has a clear place and reason in the calendar/sequence.
- [ ] Correct tier and sign-off path followed.
- [ ] Binding rulings appended to [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Voice-consistency rate across produced content (audited sample).
- Brief-to-publish rework rate (lower is better — sharp briefs).
- Resonance of content arcs (engagement/comprehension against the intended belief).
- Plan adherence vs. justified deviation.
- Department output quality as judged by [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) conformance.

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk — the brand starts to sound like many brands.
- Strategy-drift risk — content quietly diverges from approved positioning.
- Throughput risk — coherence demands slow the calendar past what the client needs.
- Channel-capture risk — the plan bends entirely to platform algorithms and loses its argument.
