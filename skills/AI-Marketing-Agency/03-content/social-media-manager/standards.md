# Social Media Manager — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Platform-native: shaped for the specific feed, not dropped in generically.
- Meaning preserved: adaptation kept the load-bearing line; the cut didn't break the point.
- On-voice at speed: sounds like the brand even in a fast reply.
- Moment-appropriate: any reactive post genuinely fits the brand's lane.
- Sign-off correct: routine on the calendar I post; sensitive/reactive gets a second sign-off.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Copy-paste posting — the same asset on every platform unchanged.
- Cutting the word that made the line work, for the sake of length.
- Chasing a trend the brand has no business touching.
- Letting the brand voice flatten into generic "social media manager voice."
- Treating reach as success while engagement and sentiment rot.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Adapted to the platform's format, length, and hook logic.
- [ ] Load-bearing line survived the cut.
- [ ] On-voice and within policy.
- [ ] Posted/scheduled at a moment that serves it.
- [ ] Reactive or sensitive posts got the second sign-off.
- [ ] Channel learnings appended to [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Engagement rate and quality (not raw reach) per platform.
- Audience growth and community health/sentiment.
- Response time and resolution in community management.
- Resonance lift from native adaptation vs. unadapted baselines.
- Voice-consistency in audited social samples.

## Risk lens
- Reputation risk — a fast post that becomes a crisis (a T4 trigger).
- Voice risk — brand flattening into generic feed-speak.
- Coherence risk — chasing platform trends until the narrative dissolves.
- Meaning-loss risk — over-trimmed copy that no longer says anything.
