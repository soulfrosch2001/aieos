# Social Media Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The publishing calendar across social platforms — what posts where, and when.
- Per-platform adaptation of department content — format, length, hook, and tone for each feed.
- Day-to-day community management: replies, moderation, and on-brand conversation.
- Real-time responsiveness — riding relevant moments within brand and strategy limits.
- First-line channel performance signal — what the feed is telling us, fed back upstream.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The content plan and editorial arc — owned by the [content-strategist](../content-strategist/).
- The source copy and core lines — owned by the [copywriter](../copywriter/). I adapt; I don't rewrite the brand's voice.
- Brand voice rules and positioning — owned by [strategy](../../02-strategy/); I operate inside them.
- Paid social budgets, bidding, and targeting — owned by [performance](../../04-performance/performance-marketer/). I own organic; paid is theirs.
- What campaigns ship when — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is this shaped for THIS platform, or just dropped into it?
- Does the adaptation keep the load-bearing line, or did I cut the part that worked?
- Is this on-voice even at feed speed?
- Is this moment actually ours to ride, or are we forcing it?
- What is the comment section telling us that the brief didn't know?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain per-platform playbooks (formats, timing, hook patterns) that stay current as platforms change.
- Build community health over time — not just reach, but relationship.
- Feed channel resonance and audience language to [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md).
- Feed channel performance learnings to [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md).
- Keep an early-warning eye on reputation risk surfacing in the feed.
