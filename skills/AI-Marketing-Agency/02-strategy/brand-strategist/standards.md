# Brand Strategist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The platform names at least one explicit sacrifice — who/what the brand is NOT for.
- Every claim in the platform is grounded in a finding from [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md), not assumed.
- The platform passes the competitor test: a direct competitor could not honestly make the same statements.
- The position (joint with the [positioning-strategist](../positioning-strategist/)) is consistent with the platform — no contradiction between territory and point.
- The brand brief is executable: a downstream agent can act on it without asking what it means.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- "Premium yet affordable" and other strategies-in-disguise that are really two unreconciled positions.
- Platforms that describe the company instead of changing what the customer believes.
- Borrowing trend equity (a meme, a moment) in a way the brand will have to pay back later.
- Mistaking internal consensus for external distinctiveness — everyone agreeing it is "us" while customers can't tell it apart.
- Letting brand drift accumulate across many approved-but-off-platform content decisions.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is there a stated sacrifice — a customer or claim we are choosing to lose?
- [ ] Could a competitor copy this platform word-for-word and be just as true?
- [ ] Is every pillar traceable to validated audience evidence?
- [ ] Does the position contradict the platform anywhere?
- [ ] Can the copywriter, social manager, and media buyer each act on this as written?
- [ ] Is the rationale recorded in [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Brand coherence across deliverables — rate of work passing brand fit at the [campaign-council](../../councils/campaign-council/) on first review.
- Distinctiveness — measurable lift in unaided brand recall and association versus category baseline.
- Downstream usability — how often departments execute from the brief without re-briefing.
- Drift rate — frequency of off-brand work caught before launch versus after.

## Risk lens
- Sameness risk — the brand becoming indistinguishable from its category.
- Incoherence risk — platform, position, and execution pulling in different directions.
- Evidence-gap risk — a bold platform resting on assumed rather than validated audiences.
- Drift risk — slow erosion of the platform through many small, individually defensible compromises.
