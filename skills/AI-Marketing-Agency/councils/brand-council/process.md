# Brand Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the kernel debate norms and inherits the stdlib
[architecture-council](../../../councils/architecture-council/process.md):
structured trade-off debate, strongest counter-argument surfaced first, and
**dissent is recorded, never suppressed** (Directive
[#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). The chair holds the brand veto but
speaks last on contested points so authority does not anchor the room. Every
position is argued against the audience, not against the person holding it.

## Decision process
1. **Frame** — the chair states the positioning question and the audience served;
   no audience, no decision.
2. **Options** — at least two viable positioning options are presented with their
   trade-offs (a single option is not a debate).
3. **Evidence** — [market-researcher](../../02-strategy/market-researcher/) and,
   where relevant, [performance-marketer](../../04-performance/performance-marketer/)
   bring data; [positioning-strategist](../../02-strategy/positioning-strategist/)
   maps competitive whitespace.
4. **Blast radius** — name what existing campaigns and equity shift if this is
   adopted, and whether the change is reversible.
5. **Decide** — the council selects the positioning; the chair's brand veto can
   stop an incoherent choice. The rejected options are named with why they lost.
6. **Record** — minutes, the decision, and dissent are appended per
   [output.md](output.md).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: nothing at this council's level — brand calls here are
  [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) and require council **plus**
  executive sign-off.
- Must escalate: [T3–T4](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) executive
  sign-off via the [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md);
  T4 is government-coordinated.

## Escalation
- Deadlock the chair cannot break, or a call above brand (which clients/markets to
  serve) → [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo) via the
  [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Quality or process violation → the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor)
  veto stops the decision; only a human maintainer overrides it
  ([decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
