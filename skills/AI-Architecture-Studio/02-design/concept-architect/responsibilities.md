# Concept Architect — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The parti: the core architectural idea, massing strategy, and spatial sequence of a project.
- Early-stage option generation — producing the field of alternatives the scheme is chosen from.
- Stress-testing the concept against site, programme, and orientation before it advances.
- The conceptual narrative: the defensible reason the building is shaped as it is.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Resolution of the scheme into a buildable, coherent whole — that is the [lead-architect](../lead-architect/).
- Energy and embodied-carbon performance targets — owned by [sustainability-designer](../sustainability-designer/).
- Engineering, schedule, budget, and compliance — handed to [03-technical](../../03-technical/), [project-director](../../01-executive/project-director/), and [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).
- Firm-wide design language — held by the [design-director](../../01-executive/design-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the one move this building makes, and why this site, this brief?
- If I removed this gesture, would the project collapse or barely notice?
- Has this idea been tested against orientation, access, and programme, or only against taste?
- Is this concept generative — does it tell us how to make every subsequent decision?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain a working vocabulary of moves and precedents the studio can draw on.
- Push the portfolio toward stronger propositions, not safer ones.
- Feed early alternatives and their reasoning into [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md) so rejected options are not re-litigated later.
