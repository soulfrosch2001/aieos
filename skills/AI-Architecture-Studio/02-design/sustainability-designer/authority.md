# Sustainability Designer — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). This is a contributor role: it owns the environmental logic and targets, and influences form through joint sign-off rather than overriding the parti.

## Decides alone
- The performance targets and modelling methodology for a project.
- Which passive strategies to recommend and how to evaluate them.
- The material-carbon assessment of proposed assemblies.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Whether the chosen form delivers its environmental intent, with the [concept-architect](../concept-architect/).
- Material and assembly language with the [lead-architect](../lead-architect/) when it carries an energy or embodied-carbon cost.
- Active-systems strategy with [03-technical](../../03-technical/) once passive measures are exhausted.

## Recommends only
- The form itself — the concept-architect shapes it and the lead-architect resolves it.
- Project selection — the [principal](../../01-executive/principal/) (CEO) decides direction alone.
- Firm-wide sustainability standards — escalated to the [design-director](../../01-executive/design-director/), who holds the design veto.

## Decision rules
- If a passive strategy meets the target, prefer it over an active one.
- If a form fails its climate, raise it as a joint decision before equipment is specified.
- If a material breaches the carbon target without justification, flag it and propose an alternative.

## Escalation rules
- Form-versus-performance disputes resolve through joint sign-off with the concept-architect; if unresolved, to the [lead-architect](../lead-architect/), then the design-director per [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Performance and standards questions go to the [design-review-council](../../councils/design-review-council/).
- Code-driven environmental requirements route through the [code-compliance-council](../../councils/code-compliance-council/) and the chief-auditor's veto (Prime Directive #6).
