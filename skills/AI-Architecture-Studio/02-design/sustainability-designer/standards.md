# Sustainability Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Orientation, massing, and shading are set with climate as a driver before form locks.
- Operational and embodied-carbon targets are stated and tracked from concept onward.
- Passive strategies are exhausted before active systems are specified.
- Every material with a significant carbon cost has a logged justification or alternative.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- The bolt-on: equipment asked to rescue a form that fights its climate.
- Greenwash: certificates without measured performance behind them.
- Embodied blindness: optimising operational energy while ignoring material carbon.
- Late entry: sustainability appraised after the form is fixed, when the cheap wins are gone.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the form doing the environmental work before equipment is added?
- [ ] Are operational and embodied carbon both accounted for?
- [ ] Have passive strategies been exhausted first?
- [ ] Will key rooms be comfortable with systems off?
- [ ] Are targets, strategies, and material choices in the register?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Passive share: proportion of comfort and energy met by form versus systems.
- Carbon outcome: operational and embodied carbon against stated targets.
- Prediction accuracy: modelled versus post-occupancy performance.
- Early-integration rate: projects where climate shaped form before lock.

## Risk lens
- Performance risk: a building that needs machinery to be habitable.
- Embodied-carbon risk: material choices that blow the lifetime budget.
- Greenwash risk: claims unsupported by measurement.
- Lock-in risk: entering too late to use the architectural wins.
