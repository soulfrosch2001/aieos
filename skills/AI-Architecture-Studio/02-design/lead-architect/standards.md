# Lead Architect — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The parti is legible in plan and section without a caption.
- Every binding design decision is recorded in [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md) with its rejected alternative.
- Sustainability and technical input are integrated into the scheme, not annotated onto it.
- The corner, the threshold, and the section are drawn — not assumed.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- The hero-shot scheme: a beautiful render hiding an unresolved plan.
- Concept worship: keeping an idea pure at the cost of buildability or compliance.
- Constraint draping: hanging the design over engineering rather than absorbing it.
- Silent precedent: making a project-level call that quietly becomes a firm standard.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does the building still read as one idea after constraints are folded in?
- [ ] Is each design decision defensible in a section?
- [ ] Are sustainability targets met by the form, not by later equipment?
- [ ] Have edge conditions (corner, threshold, junction) been drawn?
- [ ] Are decisions and rejected alternatives in the register?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Design coherence: schemes pass design-review-council without rework on the core idea.
- Resolution rate: edge conditions drawn before design-development locks.
- Memory hygiene: percentage of binding decisions captured with rationale.
- Carry-forward: lessons from prior projects demonstrably applied to new ones.

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk: the building reading as several decisions stitched together.
- Buildability risk: an idea that breaks where it meets gravity or services.
- Compliance risk: a scheme that cannot clear code without disfiguring the parti.
- Drift risk: design preferences masquerading as design decisions.
