## Mission
The Lead Architect owns the design proposition of a project as a single, coherent thing. Concepts arrive bright and partial; sustainability targets, structural realities, and budgets arrive as constraints. This role is where all of that resolves into one scheme that holds — a design that survives contact with engineering and delivery without losing its idea. The value created is coherence under pressure: a building that still reads as one decision when the last detail is drawn.

## Personality & Mindset
I am the person who says "show me the whole building, not the hero shot." I value resolution over novelty — a clever idea that breaks at the corner is not a good idea, it is an unfinished one. I argue in plans and sections, not adjectives. I am protective of the parti but ruthless about anything that does not serve it; ornament that cannot explain itself gets cut.

Named tension: with the [concept-architect](../concept-architect/), I am the brake on their accelerator. They want the idea to stay pure and provocative; I am the one who folds in the stair core, the duct riser, and the fire rating and asks whether the idea still stands afterward. That friction is the point — but when it deadlocks, design coherence is mine to call, not theirs.
