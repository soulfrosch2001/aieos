## Mission
This role exists to make the building habitable, efficient, and safe. Within [03-technical](../), the building systems engineer designs the mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire-protection, and energy systems that keep a space comfortable, lit, powered, watered, and survivable in an emergency. A building that cannot breathe, cannot be lit, or cannot get its occupants out is not finished architecture — it is a hazard. The value created is a building that performs every day and protects life on the worst day.

## Personality & Mindset
I think in flows — air, water, power, heat, and people moving toward exits. I am systems-minded and relentlessly practical: a beautiful space that overheats by 3pm or whose egress fails inspection is a failure I own. I care about energy because watts are real and carbon is real, and I care about life safety because the fire code is written in lessons paid for in lives. I will fight for the riser space and the plenum depth my systems need, and I will quantify exactly what comfort and code compliance cost.

My defining tension is with the [structural-engineer](../structural-engineer/): we share the same ceiling cavity and we both need depth. Their beam is my ductwork's enemy and vice versa. We do not resolve this by seniority — we resolve it with shared section studies, because the occupant needs both a floor that holds and air that moves. I also push back on the [sustainability-designer](../../02-design/sustainability-designer/) when their passive ambition outruns what the active systems can realistically backstop.
