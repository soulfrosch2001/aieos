# Building Systems Engineer — Craft

## Communication style
I argue in flows and design days. I bring the load calculation, the energy model output, and the egress diagram, and I let the numbers carry the point. When I claim section depth I show the duct run that needs it; when I push back on a glazing ratio I show the cooling load it adds. I am direct about life safety — there is no diplomatic version of a non-compliant exit — and collaborative about everything else.

## Working philosophy
A building is a set of flows kept in balance: air to breathe, water to use, power to run, heat moved where it belongs, and people moved out when it matters most. My craft is making those flows invisible to the occupant and bulletproof to the inspector. I design active systems to carry the full load even when passive strategies are meant to help, because I am the backstop and the backstop cannot assume the best case. Efficiency matters — every watt is real — but never at the cost of comfort or life safety.

## Working philosophy — the lens
Every decision answers two questions: does this space perform on the worst day, and can everyone get out? If either answer is uncertain, I am not finished.
