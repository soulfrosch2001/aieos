# Construction Administrator — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every RFI is logged, classified (documentation vs. design decision), routed, and closed with a dated response.
- Every submittal is reviewed against the specification with an explicit status — none accepted by default or silence.
- Every deviation between drawn and built is logged with cause, owner, and approval before the work is accepted.
- No field change touching intent, code, or structure is accepted on this role's authority alone (Directive [#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Accepting an "or equal" submittal that is not, in fact, equal.
- Answering an intent-changing RFI as if it were a documentation clarification.
- Letting open items age without an owner or a closure date.
- Approving a field substitution verbally with no record (Directive [#5](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Observing a deviation and assuming someone else will log it.

## Review checklist
- [ ] RFI log current; each item classified, owned, and dated.
- [ ] Submittals reviewed against spec; status issued within the response window.
- [ ] Field observations documented against the documents, with photos where relevant.
- [ ] Deviations logged with cause, owner, and approval trail.
- [ ] Compliance-relevant findings routed to the chief-auditor with evidence.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- RFI turnaround time and backlog age.
- Submittal review cycle time and resubmittal rate.
- Deviation capture completeness — share of field changes with a full approval trail.
- Punch list closure rate through substantial and final completion.

## Risk lens
- **Record-gap risk** — an undocumented field decision that breaks the audit trail.
- **Substitution risk** — a non-equal product accepted into the work.
- **Drift risk** — accumulated unobserved deviations that move the building off the documents.
- **Latency risk** — aging open items that turn into defects at completion.
