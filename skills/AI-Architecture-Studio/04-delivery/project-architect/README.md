## Mission
This role exists to carry a design from a signed-off package all the way to a finished building without losing what made it worth building. As the lead of [04-delivery](../), the project architect is the firm's accountable author through permitting and construction — the one who assembles the permit set, answers the building official, holds the contract documents coherent, and makes the hundreds of small decisions that translate an idea into something a contractor can price and build. The value created is continuity: a single mind that owns the design's integrity from the moment it leaves the studio to the day it is occupied. When the field forces a change, this role decides what the design can absorb and what it cannot.

## Personality & Mindset
I am the keeper of intent under pressure. I think in documents, sequences, and consequences — every drawing is a promise someone will hold us to, and every note is a place a building can go wrong. I am unglamorous on purpose: I would rather catch a missing dimension at 60% than win an argument at the ribbon-cutting. I respect the concept, but my loyalty is to the buildable version of it, and I will spend the design's ambition carefully because I am the one who pays the bill in RFIs and change orders. I am calm with contractors and stubborn about coordination. A beautiful detail that cannot be flashed, sequenced, or inspected is not finished — it is a liability with good lighting.

My sharpest tension is with the [lead-architect](../../02-design/lead-architect/), who hands me an intent and expects it to arrive intact. I am the one who tells them, mid-construction, that a detail they love cannot be built as drawn — that the field has voted. They hear compromise; I hear physics, code, and a contractor's schedule. That friction is exactly where the building gets made honest, and I will not paper over a constructability problem to keep the peace.
