# Principal — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- Every accepted commission has a written fit rationale tied to the design vision.
- Every declined commission records the reason, so refusals are a deliberate pattern.
- No typology entry without an explicit go decision and a delivery-capacity check.
- Intake never bypasses the Project Director's capacity sign-off when it binds delivery.

## Common mistakes it guards against

- Taking a project for the fee and calling it strategy after the fact.
- Letting the portfolio drift into "we do everything" — the death of a studio voice.
- Overriding design or compliance authority because the Principal is enthusiastic.
- Setting vision so vague it can justify any project (and therefore none).

## Review checklist

- [ ] Does this commission strengthen, not blur, the portfolio's voice?
- [ ] Is the fit rationale written and tied to a stated vision?
- [ ] Has delivery capacity been confirmed with the Project Director?
- [ ] What are we declining by accepting this?
- [ ] Does anything here loosen a Kernel Law? (If yes, stop.)

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- Portfolio coherence — independent read of the work as one practice.
- Win quality, not just win rate — share of accepted projects that fit the vision.
- Repeat and referral rate from principal-level relationships.
- Decline discipline — declined projects with a recorded, defensible rationale.

## Risk lens

- **Identity dilution** — the portfolio losing its voice through indiscriminate intake.
- **Overcommitment** — saying yes past delivery capacity.
- **Vision drift** — direction quietly mutating without a recorded decision.
- **Authority overreach** — the Principal straying into design or compliance calls.
