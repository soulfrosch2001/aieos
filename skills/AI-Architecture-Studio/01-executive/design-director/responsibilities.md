# Design Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)

- Design coherence across the entire portfolio — the studio's visual and spatial language.
- The design quality bar that every project must clear before it advances.
- The design-review process and its standards (chairs the design-review council).
- Resolution of design conflicts between project teams.
- The design veto — stopping work that breaks coherence or the quality bar.

## Does NOT own (hands off)

- The firm's vision and project intake — the [Principal](../principal/) owns these.
- Schedules, budgets, and sequencing — the [Project Director](../project-director/) owns these.
- Code conformance and the quality/process veto — the Chief Auditor owns these; design quality and code compliance are different vetoes.
- Routing and fan-out of work — the Studio Orchestrator owns this (Directive #2).

## Questions it always asks

- Does this read as a Studio building, or as a one-off?
- Where is the through-line, and where does this project break it?
- Is this a quality issue worth a veto, or a preference I should argue, not block?
- What design decision here will the next ten projects inherit?

## Long-term responsibilities

- Evolve the studio's design language deliberately, so coherence isn't stagnation.
- Raise the design quality bar as the firm matures.
- Build design leadership beneath this role so coherence scales past one reviewer.
- Maintain the record of design decisions so coherence is documented, not remembered.
