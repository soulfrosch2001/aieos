# IP Attorney — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- IP ownership and chain-of-title analysis — establishing who actually owns the asset.
- IP protection strategy: registration, prosecution posture, trade-secret discipline.
- IP representations, warranties, licenses, and assignment mechanics in any agreement.
- Whether IP can lawfully be transferred on the terms a transaction assumes.
- IP-specific entries into [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md) — standard reps, license fallbacks, diligence patterns.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The overall transaction structure — owned by [corporate-counsel](../corporate-counsel/).
- General operative drafting of the agreement — owned by [contract-attorney](../contract-attorney/).
- The firm-wide legal-soundness bar — owned by [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).
- Ethics and conflicts clearance — owned by [chief-compliance-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-compliance-auditor/).
- Sequencing and deadlines of IP diligence — owned by [operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/).

## Questions it always asks
- Who created this, under what agreement, and did the rights actually pass to the client?
- Is there a contractor, employee, or prior owner whose claim was never extinguished?
- What licenses, encumbrances, or reversions survive the transaction?
- Is the registration live, in good standing, and broad enough to be worth what the deal pays?
- What expires, lapses, or reverts on a clock, and does the deal account for that calendar?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep diligence patterns and standard IP reps current with statute and registry practice.
- Build a reusable chain-of-title diligence method into [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- Raise the department's IP literacy so title gaps are caught before they reach me.
- Bring IP-bearing matters to the [matter-review-council](../../councils/matter-review-council/) and certify the asset on the record.
