# Contract Attorney — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Drafting of operative agreements: the clauses that bind, define, allocate, and condition.
- Enforceability of the language — whether a court would hold the parties to what is written.
- Negotiation of contract terms with counterparties, within the firm's mandate.
- The firm's clause library and standard fallback positions, fed into [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- Defined-terms hygiene and internal consistency across an agreement and its annexes.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The transaction structure the contract expresses — owned by [corporate-counsel](../corporate-counsel/).
- IP-specific representations, licenses, and assignment mechanics — owned by [ip-attorney](../ip-attorney/).
- The firm-wide legal-soundness bar — owned by [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).
- Ethics and conflicts clearance — owned by [chief-compliance-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-compliance-auditor/).
- Deadlines and staffing of drafting work — owned by [operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/).

## Questions it always asks
- What happens to this clause when the relationship fails and a hostile reader holds the pen?
- Is every operative term defined, used consistently, and free of "intend"-style aspiration?
- Which obligations are conditions, which are covenants, and which are mere recitals — and is that on purpose?
- What does the structure assume the words must do, and will the law actually let them?
- Where is the remedy if this promise breaks, and is it the remedy the client wants?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the clause library current with statute, case law, and market movement.
- Convert recurring negotiation outcomes into reusable fallback positions in [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- Raise the department's drafting precision so ambiguity is caught before review.
- Bring contract matters to the [matter-review-council](../../councils/matter-review-council/) and defend the operative language on the record.
