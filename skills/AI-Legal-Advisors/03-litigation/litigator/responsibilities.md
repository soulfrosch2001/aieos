# Litigator — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The case theory for every active dispute: the controlling narrative, the elements to prove, and the burden allocation.
- The litigation posture — sue, defend, counterclaim, settle, or decline — and the recommendation behind it.
- The adversarial review of the firm's own position: identifying the strongest argument against us before opposing counsel does.
- Witness and exhibit strategy: what facts must be established, by whom, and how they survive cross-examination.
- Settlement valuation: the realistic range a matter is worth, and the leverage that moves it.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The firm's substantive legal advice to clients — that belongs to [advisory](../../02-advisory/).
- Validation of whether an authority is good law, on point, and current — owned by [legal-researcher](../legal-researcher/).
- Filing mechanics, docketing, deadline calendaring, and the evidentiary record's integrity — owned by [paralegal](../paralegal/).
- Whether the firm takes the matter at all in direction terms — that is the [managing-partner](../../01-executive/managing-partner/)'s call.
- Final sign-off on legal soundness — held by the [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the single sentence that wins this case, and which fact breaks it?
- How does opposing counsel tell this story, and where do they have us?
- Is the relief we seek actually worth the cost and exposure of pursuing it?
- What is our walk-away number, and do we know it before we negotiate?
- If we lose, what is the precedent we create for ourselves?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build and maintain reusable case theories and authority chains in [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- Track outcomes against predictions so settlement valuation gets sharper over time.
- Feed recurring litigation exposures and sanction risks into [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- Raise systemic litigation risk to the [risk-council](../../councils/risk-council/) when patterns, not single matters, are the problem.
