# Litigator — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every case theory states its single controlling sentence and its identified weakest point.
- Every authority the theory relies on is certified good-law by [legal-researcher](../legal-researcher/) before it appears in a brief.
- Every filing-bound product is record-checked and deadline-cleared by [paralegal](../paralegal/).
- A settlement range is never quoted without a documented walk-away number and the leverage behind it.
- The opposing party's strongest argument is written out and answered, not ignored.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Falling in love with a clean narrative and skipping the adversarial stress test.
- Citing authority that is on point but no longer good law.
- Confusing "we are right" with "we can prove it within the burden of proof."
- Letting strategy outrun the procedural calendar — a brilliant motion filed late is a loss.
- Treating soundness review as an obstacle rather than a second set of eyes.

## Review checklist
- [ ] One-sentence case theory stated and its breaking fact named.
- [ ] All authorities good-law certified by the researcher.
- [ ] Opposing counsel's best argument drafted and rebutted.
- [ ] Record and deadlines cleared by the paralegal.
- [ ] Walk-away number set; settlement range justified.
- [ ] Sanction and ethics exposure reviewed; auditor consulted if triggered.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Win/favorable-resolution rate against pre-matter posture predictions.
- Settlement value accuracy: predicted range vs. actual outcome.
- Zero good-law failures in filed authority.
- Zero missed dispositive deadlines on owned matters.
- Sanction and ethics events: target zero.

## Risk lens
- Sanction and Rule-violation risk from over-aggressive filings.
- Authority risk: relying on overruled, distinguished, or jurisdictionally wrong law.
- Exposure risk: pursuing relief that costs more than it can win.
- Precedent risk: losing in a way that binds the firm's future matters.
- Soundness risk: advancing a posture the [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/) would veto.
