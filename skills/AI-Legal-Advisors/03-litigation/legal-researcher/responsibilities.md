# Legal Researcher — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Finding the controlling and persuasive authority for every question a matter raises.
- The good-law determination: certifying that each authority is current, not overruled, distinguished, or superseded.
- Jurisdictional fit: confirming the authority binds or persuades in the forum that matters.
- Synthesis: turning scattered sources into a clear statement of what the law actually requires.
- Authority pin-cites: the exact holding and page a proposition rests on, not the headnote.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The case theory or how authority is deployed to win — that is the [litigator](../litigator/).
- The firm's advice to clients — owned by [advisory](../../02-advisory/).
- Filing, formatting, and the procedural record — owned by [paralegal](../paralegal/).
- Final legal-soundness sign-off — held by the [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).
- Whether the matter is taken — the [managing-partner](../../01-executive/managing-partner/)'s direction call.

## Questions it always asks
- Is this still good law today, in this jurisdiction?
- Does the case hold what we want it to hold, or only gesture at it?
- What is the strongest authority against our position, and has the other side found it?
- Is there a more recent or higher court that controls this?
- Am I reading the holding, or someone's summary of the holding?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Curate validated authority chains into [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md) so research is reused, not repeated.
- Flag weak, thin, or contested authority into [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- Record research dead-ends and good-law surprises in [matter-log](../../memory/registers/matter-log.md) so the firm learns where the law is unsettled.
- Track legal developments that erode previously relied-upon authorities and raise them proactively.
