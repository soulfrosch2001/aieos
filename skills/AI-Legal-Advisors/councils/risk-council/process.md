# Risk Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel's [communication protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Risk is argued from evidence and authority, not appetite. Dissent is recorded in the minutes, never suppressed — and because this council sits closest to the absolute veto, a recorded dissent on ethics carries special weight. The chair, holding the veto, states findings last to avoid foreclosing the discussion prematurely.

## Decision process
1. Compliance-counsel presents the conflict and information-wall check and any open controls.
2. The regulatory-analyst confirms the regulatory exposure and the currency of the readings behind it.
3. The council assesses residual risk: conflict, boundary, confidentiality, privilege, ethical fitness.
4. It decides: acceptable / acceptable-with-controls / unacceptable. Controls must be operable and owned, not aspirational.
5. The chair may exercise the absolute ethics/compliance veto, which stops the matter outright.
6. Minutes — consensus, dissent, and any veto — are appended to memory per [output.md](output.md).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: T2–T3 matters where residual risk is within firm tolerance and controls are operable. See [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
- Must escalate: T4 matters and any case where the desired strategy cannot be reconciled with an acceptable risk posture — escalate per the [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
Ordinary deadlocks escalate one level up the chain per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). A [chief-compliance-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-compliance-auditor/) veto is absolute under the Government layer of [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): it stops the work, and only a human maintainer overrides it.
