# Managing Partner — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every matter-selection decision states accept/decline/refer with a written fit rationale.
- Every direction carries an owner and a date.
- New practice areas have a General Counsel soundness sign-off before entry.
- Accepted matters with delivery strain have an Operations Partner capacity sign-off.
- No decision proceeds over a standing Chief Compliance Auditor ethics veto.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Taking profitable matters that sit off the firm's line.
- Overruling the General Counsel on soundness to win a matter.
- Issuing direction without an owner or date.
- Reaching into delivery sequencing or engagement tiering that belongs to peers.
- Drifting the portfolio into too much novelty at once.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is this matter on the firm's line?
- [ ] Is the worst credible outcome carriable across the portfolio?
- [ ] Has the GC confirmed it can be made legally sound (where relevant)?
- [ ] Has the Operations Partner confirmed capacity (where strained)?
- [ ] Is there any live ethics/compliance veto?
- [ ] Is the decision recorded in the matter-log with rationale, owner, and date?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Portfolio coherence: share of active matters on the firm's stated line.
- Decline quality: declined matters that later proved correctly avoided.
- Direction legibility: percentage of directions with owner and date.
- Balance-of-power health: GC soundness vetoes and Auditor vetoes respected without override attempts.

## Risk lens
- Direction risk: chasing revenue off-line.
- Portfolio risk: too much concurrent novelty or exposure.
- Governance risk: eroding peer authority by overruling soundness or ethics.
- Reputational risk: representing clients or matters that damage firm standing.
