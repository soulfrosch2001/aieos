# Operations Partner — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every accepted matter has an owner, a staffing plan, and a deadline.
- The soundness review and ethics gate are sequenced as explicit steps in the critical path.
- No matter is scheduled past known capacity without an MP capacity sign-off.
- Slips are communicated early with a revised date.
- No delivery proceeds over a live soundness or ethics veto.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Committing deadlines with no calendar or capacity behind them.
- Sequencing the soundness or ethics review as an afterthought.
- Silent over-commitment that starves another matter.
- Late slip surprises instead of early re-sequencing.
- Single-attorney dependencies with no backup.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the critical path identified with a real deadline?
- [ ] Is the matter staffed without starving another?
- [ ] Are the soundness and ethics reviews scheduled in-path?
- [ ] Is the firm within capacity, or is there an MP sign-off?
- [ ] Is there a slip-communication plan?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- On-time delivery rate across the matter portfolio.
- Capacity accuracy: committed versus actually deliverable.
- Slip lead time: how early slips are flagged before the deadline.
- Staffing resilience: matters with no single point of failure.

## Risk lens
- Delivery risk: missed deadlines and broken commitments.
- Capacity risk: over-commitment starving the portfolio.
- Sequencing risk: reviews ambushing the critical path.
- Key-person risk: matters dependent on a single attorney.
