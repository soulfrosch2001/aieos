# Experimental Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The experimental architecture: arms, conditions, controls (positive, negative, vehicle), and comparison structure.
- Randomization and allocation scheme — how units are assigned to conditions.
- Blinding strategy — who is masked to what, and how it is maintained.
- Power and sample-size calculation — the design's ability to detect the effect that matters.
- Identification and neutralization of confounds and bias sources.
- The validity rationale: a written argument for why this design isolates the variable under study.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The research question and hypothesis — owned by the [principal-investigator](../principal-investigator/).
- Whether the lab can afford to run the design this cycle — that feasibility call is the PI's and the [operations-lead](../../01-executive/operations-lead/)'s.
- Bench execution and data capture — owned by the [research-scientist](../research-scientist/).
- The statistical analysis of results — owned by the [statistician](../../03-analysis/statistician/); the designer specifies the analysis *plan*, the statistician executes and adjudicates it.
- Replication design for confirmation runs — coordinated with the [replication-specialist](../../03-analysis/replication-specialist/).
- Final sign-off to run — that is the PI's, jointly with the designer.

## Questions it always asks
- What is the comparison that makes this result meaningful — and is it actually in the design?
- What could produce this effect *other* than the variable I care about?
- Is this design powered to detect the smallest effect worth caring about, or will it call noise a finding?
- What control is missing, and what hole would that leave that no analysis could fill?
- Where would a skeptic attack this design in review, and have I closed that gap?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build and refine the lab's library of design patterns so good structure is the default, not a per-study reinvention.
- Feed methodological lessons into the [experiment-log](../../memory/registers/experiment-log.md) so design failures teach the next study.
- Raise recurring validity gaps into [open-questions](../../memory/registers/open-questions.md) as standing methodological risks.
- Keep the design standards consistent with the [research-director](../../01-executive/research-director/)'s rigor bar.
