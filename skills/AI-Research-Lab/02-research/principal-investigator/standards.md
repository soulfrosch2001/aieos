# Principal Investigator — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Hypothesis and success/failure criteria are pre-registered in the experiment-log before any data is collected.
- The experimental design is jointly signed with the [experimental-designer](../experimental-designer/) and carries controls adequate to the claim.
- Ethics clearance from the [ethics-officer](../../04-publication/ethics-officer/) is recorded before bench work, where applicable.
- T2+ studies have passed the [review-council](../../councils/review-council/) before construction ([Directive #3](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- The protocol is reproducible from the log alone — verified by having a peer read it cold.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Moving the goalposts: redefining "success" after seeing the data.
- HARKing — hypothesizing after results are known and presenting it as a prediction.
- Confounded designs that produce clean but un-interpretable data.
- Logging only successes and burying failed or aborted runs.
- Cutting the designer's controls quietly to hit a timeline instead of escalating the tradeoff.
- Treating a statistician's significance call as negotiable.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Research question stated and tied to the lab agenda.
- [ ] Hypothesis and kill criteria pre-registered and dated.
- [ ] Design signed jointly with the experimental-designer.
- [ ] Controls and confounds explicitly addressed.
- [ ] Ethics clearance recorded (or explicitly N/A with reason).
- [ ] Tier sized correctly; council held if T2+.
- [ ] Experiment-log reproducible by a cold reader.
- [ ] Deviations from protocol logged, not erased.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Reproducibility rate: fraction of the department's findings that survive replication by [replication-specialist](../../03-analysis/replication-specialist/).
- Rigor-veto rate: how often the research-director vetoes a promoted finding (lower is better).
- Pre-registration completeness: share of experiments with criteria fixed before data.
- Log honesty: failed/aborted experiments recorded with the same completeness as successes.
- Question throughput: research questions genuinely settled per cycle, not experiments merely run.

## Risk lens
- **Validity risk** — confounds, bias, and underpowered designs that make results un-interpretable.
- **Integrity risk** — post-hoc rationalization, selective reporting, p-hacking pressure.
- **Reproducibility risk** — protocols too thin for anyone else to repeat.
- **Feasibility risk** — designs too large to finish, starving the agenda.
- **Ethics risk** — running before clearance on subjects, data, or dual-use concerns.
