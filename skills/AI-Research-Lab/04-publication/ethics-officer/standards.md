# Ethics Officer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Consent is documented, informed, and voluntary for any subject involvement.
- Personal data is de-identified to standard, or explicit, justified exemption is
  recorded.
- Dual-use / misuse potential is assessed; risky methods carry a mitigation or
  disclosure plan.
- All conflicts of interest and funding sources are disclosed.
- No sign of fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism.
- The accepted-risk record names what was accepted and who accepted it.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Consent gaps treated as paperwork rather than a precondition.
- "It's just research" rationalization of clear dual-use risk.
- Re-identifiable data shipped as "anonymized."
- Undisclosed funding or conflicts of interest.
- Suppressing a finding when staged disclosure would serve better — or rushing a
  dangerous one out because deadlines loomed.
- Accepting risk verbally with no named owner and no written record.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Consent documentation present and valid.
- [ ] Data privacy / de-identification verified.
- [ ] Dual-use assessment done; mitigation/disclosure plan attached if needed.
- [ ] Conflicts of interest and funding disclosed.
- [ ] Misconduct check clear.
- [ ] Accepted risks recorded with named owner.
- [ ] Ethics gate decision appended to [findings](../../memory/registers/findings.md).
- [ ] Dissent recorded via [ethics-council](../../councils/ethics-council/README.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Ethics issues caught pre-release vs. raised post-publication.
- Time-in-gate (review should be early and fast, not a deadline bottleneck).
- Accepted-risk records with a named owner (target: 100%).
- Zero undisclosed conflicts or consent failures in published work.

## Risk lens
- **Harm risk** — to human/animal subjects or third parties.
- **Dual-use risk** — methods that can be weaponized or misused.
- **Privacy risk** — re-identification, data exposure.
- **Integrity risk** — fabrication, falsification, plagiarism.
- **Disclosure risk** — undisclosed conflicts, funding, or competing interests.
