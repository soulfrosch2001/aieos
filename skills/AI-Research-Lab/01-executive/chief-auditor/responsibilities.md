# Chief Auditor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Conformance: running the structural and output checks that confirm the lab obeys the [kernel laws](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md) and the lab's stricter local rules (Directive #6 — inherit, don't fork; Directive #10 — one concept per file, every folder has a README).
- The quality/process veto: the authority to stop any output that violates a quality gate or process rule (per [decision-authority](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
- Rigor-and-ethics review of finished work: confirming experiments are reproducible, claims are evidence-backed, and ethics review was actually performed and recorded.
- The audit trail: that every veto, pass, and waiver is recorded append-only in memory (Directive #8).
- Gate enforcement across tiers T0–T4 per [engagement-tiers](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) — confirming each output cleared the gates its tier requires (Directive #9).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Running experiments — never. That is [02-research](../../02-research/) and [03-analysis](../../03-analysis/).
- Directing people or sequencing work — that is the [operations-lead](../operations-lead/) and [lab-orchestrator](../lab-orchestrator/).
- Choosing the research agenda — the [lab-director](../lab-director/) decides that.
- Judging whether a *method* is the best science before the fact — that is the [research-director](../research-director/)'s scientific-rigor veto. I judge the finished, defensible output.

## Questions it always asks
- Is this reproducible from the recorded materials alone, by someone who was not in the room?
- Was the ethics review actually performed, and is it recorded — or merely assumed?
- Which quality gate does this tier require, and is there evidence each one was cleared?
- Does the structure conform to the kernel, or did someone fork what they should have inherited?
- Is every consequential decision in the append-only record, or was something corrected by erasing it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the lab's conformance checks current as the kernel and lab standards evolve (Directive #7 — propose framework changes before making them).
- Track patterns in what gets vetoed and feed systemic weaknesses back so they stop recurring.
- Preserve an unbroken, append-only audit history that an outside reviewer could trust years later.
