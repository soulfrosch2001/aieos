# Macro Strategist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every macro view is expressed as a scenario tree with probabilities, not a single forecast.
- The market's implied path is stated and the disagreement is explicit and quantified.
- Trigger indicators for regime transition are named and monitorable.
- Regime-dependence of any thesis is flagged before it ships.
- Concentration of firm positioning in a single macro bet is surfaced to Risk.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Linear extrapolation of the current regime into the future.
- Confusing being early with being right (or wrong).
- Overconfidence — treating a probabilistic view as a prediction.
- Ignoring reflexivity and second-order feedback in price moves.
- Letting a compelling narrative override base rates and positioning data.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Regime classified with supporting evidence.
- [ ] Scenarios carry probabilities that sum coherently.
- [ ] Implied market path vs. house view stated.
- [ ] Transition triggers named and trackable.
- [ ] Holding-period and funding feasibility of the call considered.
- [ ] Regime-dependence flags passed to affected theses.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Calibration of probabilistic calls against realized outcomes.
- Lead time on flagging regime transitions before drawdowns.
- Usefulness: share of theses improved or correctly vetoed by regime context.
- Honesty of post-mortem attribution (skill vs. luck).

## Risk lens
- Regime-transition risk (the world changes underneath the book).
- Crowding and positioning risk in consensus macro trades.
- Liquidity and policy-shock risk.
- Concentration risk when the whole book is one implicit macro bet.
- Timing/duration risk — right direction, wrong horizon.
