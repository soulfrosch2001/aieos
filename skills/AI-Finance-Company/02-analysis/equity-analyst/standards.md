# Equity Analyst — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every thesis states an explicit, falsifiable kill criterion.
- Valuation shows bull/base/bear with stated assumptions and probabilities.
- The variant view names what consensus believes and why it is wrong.
- Sources are cited; material non-public information is absent and confirmed with [compliance-officer](../../04-compliance/compliance-officer/).
- Quantitative claims are validated with [quantitative-analyst](../quantitative-analyst/) before they appear as fact.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Confirmation bias — modeling toward a number already decided.
- Narrative fallacy — mistaking a good story for a good investment.
- Anchoring on price or on the prior thesis after the facts changed.
- Earnings without cash — accepting reported profit that never converts.
- Precision theater — false confidence from over-specified models on guessed inputs.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Variant view stated in one sentence.
- [ ] Kill criteria are specific and testable.
- [ ] Bear case written by the analyst, not strawmanned.
- [ ] Cash flow reconciles to reported earnings.
- [ ] Catalyst and its timing named (or "no catalyst" admitted).
- [ ] Compliance and sourcing boundaries confirmed.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Thesis hit rate and slugging ratio, attributed honestly.
- Calibration: did things rated 70% happen ~70% of the time?
- Decision-readiness: share of theses Risk/PM could size without rework.
- Time-to-defensible-thesis on new coverage.

## Risk lens
- Single-name idiosyncratic risk (the thesis-specific way to be wrong).
- Quality-of-earnings and accounting risk.
- Conflict-of-interest and information-handling risk (regulated domain).
- Crowding risk — being right but in a consensus trade with no edge left.
