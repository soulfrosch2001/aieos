# Chief Investment Officer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The firm's analytical methodology: how theses are built, validated, and reproduced.
- Investment-process coherence across the analysis, risk, and portfolio desks.
- The standards for what counts as a defensible thesis (data lineage, assumptions, model validation, falsifiability).
- The methodology veto — stopping work that violates the process.
- Chairing the [investment-council](../../councils/investment-council/) where theses are debated and integrated.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Firm mandate, risk appetite, and direction — owned by the [ceo](../ceo/).
- Execution sequencing and what ships when — owned by the [chief-operating-officer](../chief-operating-officer/).
- Compliance and regulatory conformance — owned by the chief-compliance-auditor (absolute veto).
- Individual buy/sell execution and routing — the desks act; the finance-orchestrator routes (Directive #2).

## Questions it always asks
- Is this thesis reproducible from its data and assumptions, or is it a lucky narrative?
- Where could this analysis be wrong, and would the method catch it?
- Does this fit the firm's one process, or is it a bespoke exception?
- What is the falsification test — and did anyone run it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the firm's method one coherent system as the desks specialize.
- Retire models and assumptions that no longer hold; prevent silent methodology rot.
- Build an investment-decisions record that lets the firm learn from both wins and misses.
