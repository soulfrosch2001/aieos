# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every accepted engagement names the studio-direction reason it serves, in writing.
- Every decline records why, so the reasoning is reusable next time a similar brief is routed.
- No brief is accepted that the balance-director has flagged as unevidenceable without an explicit re-scope.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Saying yes to prestige work the methodology cannot support.
- Confusing volume of engagements with strength of direction.
- Letting a loud client studio set priority instead of the studio's own direction.
- Drifting toward direct client contact instead of routing through the Government (Directive #5).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this engagement sharpen the studio's identity?
- [ ] Can the studio stand behind the balance calls it will have to make?
- [ ] Is priority set by direction, not by the loudest advocate?
- [ ] Is the *why* recorded in [balance-decisions](../../memory/registers/balance-decisions.md)?
- [ ] Did this request arrive through the Government?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Share of engagements that ship balance calls which hold up in live play.
- Repeat-routing rate from client studios (trust earned).
- Ratio of focused engagements to scattered ones — direction, not volume.

## Risk lens
- Reputation risk: shipping a call the studio could not actually evidence.
- Focus risk: portfolio sprawl diluting what the studio is known for.
- Boundary risk: any drift toward company-to-company contact outside the Government (Directive #5).
