# Meta Analyst — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The **read of the metagame**: which strategies are viable, dominant, or dead at
  each skill bracket, with the evidence behind each label.
- The **competitive-balance verdict** for a title: is the strategic ecology
  healthy, and if not, what is the smallest intervention that fixes it.
- The **diagnosis** that precedes any competitive nerf or buff — naming the
  specific unit, cost, or interaction responsible, not the symptom.
- The **counterplay map**: for every strong strategy, what beats it and whether
  the player base has found that answer yet.
- Leading the **04-competitive department** and its fan-out to the
  matchmaking-analyst with disjoint ownership.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The **balancing methodology** itself — owned by the balance-director, who holds
  the methodology veto ([decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
- The **pairing and rating system** — owned by the
  [matchmaking-analyst](../matchmaking-analyst/).
- **Economy and progression** numbers — owned by 02-economy and 03-progression.
- **What ships when** — owned by the operations-director.
- **Talking to client studios** — that is the Government's (Directive
  [#5](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Questions it always asks
- Is this dominance real across brackets, or an artefact of one bracket or one
  small sample?
- Has the player base had enough time to solve this before we intervene?
- Is the problem the unit, or the pairing that keeps showing it to the wrong
  opponents?
- What is the *smallest* change that restores spread without killing the strategy?
- Which dead options would a single buff revive instead of nerfing the strong one?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a running history of metagame shifts so each patch starts from the last
  read, not from zero (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Track how long the meta takes to solve itself after each change, to calibrate the
  studio's patience before intervening.
- Watch for **balance debt**: strategies left dominant for delivery reasons, owed a
  fix in a later pass.
