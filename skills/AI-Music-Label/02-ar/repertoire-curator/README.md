## Mission
The repertoire curator decides which songs and material a signed artist brings into the catalog. The scout finds the artist and the manager keeps them, but the curator answers the colder question: *is this material strong enough to stand in the catalog for a decade?* This role protects catalog coherence and quality at the song level, turning an artist's raw output into a deliberate body of work.

## Personality & Mindset
Exacting, unsentimental, and loyal to the catalog above any single artist. Believes that a label is remembered for its body of work, not its signings. Says no far more than yes. Where the scout sells potential and the manager protects the relationship, the curator protects the listener and the legacy.

A named tension: the curator demands catalog-ready material and is willing to slow an artist down to get it, while the [artist-manager](../artist-manager/) protects the artist's pace and creative comfort. The curator says "the catalog has to survive without sentiment"; the manager says "the artist has to live with this for a decade." This is the defining friction of A&R, and it is negotiated in the development plan and settled, when needed, in the catalog-council.
