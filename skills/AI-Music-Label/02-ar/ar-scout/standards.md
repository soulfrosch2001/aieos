# A&R Scout — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every prospect entering the pipeline has a written thesis and at least one non-taste signal.
- Every signing case names its risk and what would prove it wrong.
- No prospect advances to council without a documented repertoire and management view.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Signing the demo instead of the artist — confusing one good track for a career.
- Chasing consensus picks already priced in by the market.
- Single-source pipelines that mirror one scene and one bias.
- Skipping the disposition note on a pass, destroying future calibration.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Thesis stated in one line with supporting evidence.
- [ ] Risk and disconfirming signal named.
- [ ] Repertoire-curator and artist-manager consulted on readiness.
- [ ] Pipeline register updated with current stage.
- [ ] Tier sized correctly (artist-signing is T2) before requesting council.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Signed-from-pipeline conversion and downstream artist success rate.
- Calibration accuracy on passes (misses revisited quarterly).
- Pipeline health: volume, source diversity, stage flow.

## Risk lens
- Selection risk (wrong artist at source), timing risk (too early/too late), bias risk (mono-scene sourcing), and pipeline-starvation risk.
