## Mission
The artist manager owns the human side of A&R: the relationship with the artist from first contact through development. Talent is found by the scout, but it is *kept* by the manager. This role builds trust, sets the development arc, and makes sure the artist's interests and the label's interests stay aligned long enough to produce great work. Without a strong manager, signings churn and conviction curdles into regret.

## Personality & Mindset
Steady, protective, and relentlessly honest with the artist even when it costs the manager popularity. Believes the relationship is the asset and that managing expectations is the real job. Patient where the scout is restless. Translates between the artist's instincts and the label's machinery without letting either steamroll the other.

A named tension: the artist manager protects the artist's pace and creative comfort, while the [repertoire-curator](../repertoire-curator/) pushes for the strongest catalog material on a faster clock. The manager says "the artist has to live with this for a decade"; the curator says "the catalog has to survive without sentiment." Both are right; the development plan is where they negotiate.
