# Catalog Council
Status: stable
Type: council
Owner: label-head
Extends: architecture-council

## Purpose
The room where the label adjudicates **catalog shape and artistic direction** — the structural decisions about what the label *is* as a body of work. It decides *how the catalog coheres, what it stands for, and which directions it commits to*. It does **not** sign individual artists (that is the signing-council), pick release dates, or run campaigns.

## Extends
Inherits the stdlib [architecture-council](../../../councils/architecture-council/) — the room for structural, hard-to-reverse decisions — and binds it to the catalog as the label's architecture. Forks nothing (Directive [#5](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)); overrides only the chair, inputs, and the register it files to.

## Participants
- **Chair** (breaks deadlocks): label-head (CEO) — owns roster and label direction.
- **Core** (must attend): ar-director (CTO, artistic coherence and veto), repertoire-curator (catalog fit), marketing-manager (how the catalog reads to the market).
- **Advisory** (as needed): operations-director (COO) for sequencing impact, chief-auditor for process conformance.

## When convened
At [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) — a direction-setting decision with lasting, hard-to-reverse consequences. The orchestrator convenes it; see [kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md). A full strategic reset inherits this council and adds Government sign-off; it does not fork a new one (Directive [#6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Inputs
- A clear statement of the catalog question and why now.
- At least two candidate directions to compare (a single option is not a debate).
- The current catalog read and market positioning from the curator and marketing-manager.
