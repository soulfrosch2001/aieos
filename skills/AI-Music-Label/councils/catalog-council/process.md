# Catalog Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the kernel discussion norms — see [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Directions are argued against named alternatives; dissent is recorded, never suppressed (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). Every Core seat speaks before the chair rules. No company speaks to another company directly — cross-company input routes through the Government (Directive [#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Decision process
1. The chair (label-head) frames the catalog question and why it matters now.
2. At least two candidate directions are presented and compared.
3. The ar-director tests each for artistic coherence; the marketing-manager tests how each reads to the market; the curator tests catalog fit.
4. Risks and one-way doors are surfaced and named — catalog moves are hard to reverse.
5. The chair calls for consensus; remaining dissent is recorded by name.
6. The chair rules. The ar-director's artistic veto can block a direction that breaks coherence.

## Approval gate by tier
- **Approves alone:** T3 catalog directions — see [kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
- **Must escalate:** T4 strategic resets → add Government sign-off. See [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot break escalate to the Government via the orchestrator. A chief-auditor process veto stops the council and only a human maintainer overrides it (Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
