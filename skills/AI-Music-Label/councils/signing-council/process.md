# Signing Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the kernel discussion norms — see [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). The case is argued from evidence, not taste; dissent is recorded, never suppressed (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). Every Core seat speaks before the chair rules. No company speaks to another company directly — cross-company input routes through the Government (Directive [#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Decision process
1. The ar-scout presents the signing thesis and the evidence behind it.
2. Core seats challenge it: development feasibility (artist-manager), catalog fit (repertoire-curator), market case (marketing-manager).
3. At least one alternative use of the same capacity is weighed against the signing.
4. Risks and one-way doors are surfaced and named.
5. The chair (ar-director) calls for consensus; remaining dissent is recorded by name.
6. The chair rules. The artistic veto may block a signing outright; it cannot force one through over a no.

## Approval gate by tier
- **Approves alone:** T2 signings — see [kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
- **Must escalate:** T3+ roster bets → add label-head (CEO) sign-off. See [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot break escalate to the label-head. A chief-auditor process veto stops the council and only a human maintainer overrides it (Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
