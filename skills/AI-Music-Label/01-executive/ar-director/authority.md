# A&R Director — Authority

Maps to the **CTO** level in [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md):
cross-cutting standards and the catalog's evolution, plus a **veto** on artistic
incoherence (the label's local analogue of the CTO's technical-incoherence veto).
A company may add stricter local authority, never loosen a Kernel Law.

## Decides alone
- Artistic standards the catalog and its releases must meet.
- Whether a finished track is artistically coherent enough to enter the calendar.
- Artistic direction within the bounds of the label direction the CEO sets.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A signing — recommended into the [signing-council](../../councils/signing-council/), but the roster call is co-owned with the label-head.
- Holding a release that affects the calendar — coordinated with the operations-director on the consequences.

## Recommends only
- Roster additions and drops — the label-head decides direction alone.
- Release timing — the operations-director sequences.

## Decision rules
- If it is *does the catalog cohere* → artistic veto applies; the A&R Director decides.
- If it is *who is on the label / what the label is* → recommend; the label-head decides.
- If it is *when it ships* → defer to the operations-director.
- The artistic veto is overridden only by a written label-head decision recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).

## Escalation rules
- Coherence-vs-direction deadlock with the label-head escalates one level up per [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A chief-auditor quality veto stops the work and is overridden only by a human maintainer; it sits above the artistic veto on process grounds.
- Cross-company artistic questions route through the Government (Directive #4).
