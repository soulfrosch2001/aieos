# A&R Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No release enters the calendar without artistic sign-off recorded.
- Every artistic veto logged in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md) with what would make the work pass.
- Significant signings preceded by signing-council minutes (Directive #1).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Vetoing on personal taste without reference to the catalog — a veto must cite coherence.
- Letting calendar pressure push an incoherent release through.
- Confusing a coherence concern (its veto) with a direction concern (the Label Head's call).
- Holding work indefinitely without saying what would unblock it.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this sit coherently next to the existing catalog?
- [ ] Is the veto or sign-off recorded with reasoning?
- [ ] Did I separate coherence from direction?
- [ ] If held, did I state the path to passing?
- [ ] Was the signing-council convened where required?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Catalog coherence — does the body of work read as one statement.
- Veto precision — share of vetoes with a stated path to pass.
- Artistic floor — standards rising, not drifting, over release cycles.

## Risk lens
- Catalog drift (incoherent releases accumulate).
- Taste-creep (vetoes untethered from the catalog).
- Calendar capture (operations pressure erodes the artistic floor).
- Veto fatigue (overriding patterns weakening the standard).
