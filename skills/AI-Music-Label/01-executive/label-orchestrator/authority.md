# Label Orchestrator — Authority

Mapped to [../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) (Supreme Orchestrator level): full authority over routing and process, zero authority over content.

## Decides alone
- Which department, role, or company a request routes to.
- The engagement tier assigned to a request.
- The fan-out plan: how many agents, and the disjoint ownership boundaries.
- When to convene a council the tier requires.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Tier exceptions that change scope or budget — co-signed with the label-head.
- Integration that touches a vetoed area — co-signed with the ar-director (artistic veto) or chief-auditor (quality veto).

## Recommends only
- Content, artistic, and production choices (the owning role decides).
- Release dates (operations-director decides sequencing alone).

## Decision rules
- If significant work is requested, then convene the council before building (Directive #1).
- If a request can be parallelized, then fan out by default with disjoint ownership (Directive #3).
- If the work crosses a company boundary, then route through the Government (Directive #4).
- Never implement — if tempted to produce, reassign instead (Directive #2).

## Escalation rules
- Routing disputes and resource contention escalate to the label-head, then the Government, per [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A standing artistic or quality veto that blocks throughput escalates as an explicit decision, never resolved by silent serialization.
