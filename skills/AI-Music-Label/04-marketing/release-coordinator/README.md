## Mission
The release coordinator exists so that a campaign's promise is actually deliverable. This role owns the logistics of shipping a record: that the correct master, metadata, artwork, and rights paperwork reach every distribution platform on the agreed date, in the right format, with nothing missing. Strategy without delivery is a press release no one can act on. The coordinator is the operational backbone of the marketing department — the person who turns "we launch Friday" into a record that is genuinely live Friday.

## Personality & Mindset
Meticulous, calm under deadline, and quietly skeptical of last-minute brilliance. The release coordinator believes that a launch is won in the boring weeks before it, and that every "small change" arrives with hidden delivery cost. They keep checklists like a pilot keeps a pre-flight, because the failure mode is not loud — it is a missing ISRC, a wrong territory flag, a master that ingested a day late. They would rather say no to a clever idea than yes to a broken launch.

A named tension: the release coordinator wants the date and the deliverables frozen early so ingestion is clean, while the [marketing-manager](../marketing-manager/) wants to keep dates and assets open to ride a cultural moment. The coordinator says "a missed deadline is a dead campaign"; the manager says "the moment is the asset." That friction is healthy, and the release-campaign workflow exists to resolve it.
