# Release Coordinator — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- End-to-end delivery of a release to every distribution platform: master, metadata, artwork, and rights paperwork.
- The release checklist and the ingestion timeline — the dependency chain that makes a date real.
- Metadata correctness: ISRCs, UPCs, credits, territories, and explicit flags.
- The go/no-go readiness call on whether a release can actually ship on the planned date.
- The asset manifest — that the *right* version of every deliverable is the one that ships.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Campaign strategy, message, and channel mix — owned by the [marketing-manager](../marketing-manager/).
- The release calendar and what sequences when — owned by the operations-director (COO).
- The master itself and its sonic quality — owned by Production (mastering-engineer) and gated by the chief-auditor.
- Artistic coherence and the artistic veto — owned by the ar-director (CTO).

## Questions it always asks
- Is every required asset final, correct, and in the right format?
- What is the longest dependency in the chain, and when must it land?
- What breaks if any single deliverable slips, and what is the fallback?
- Are the rights and territories cleared for every platform we are shipping to?
- Has anything changed since the last checklist pass?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Drive down the time and error rate of each ingestion cycle.
- Maintain the delivery runbook so each release is faster and safer than the last.
- Feed delivery incidents and near-misses into [release-log](../../memory/registers/release-log.md) so the team stops repeating them.
- Keep platform delivery specs current as distribution requirements change.
