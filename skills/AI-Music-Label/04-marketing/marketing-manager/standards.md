# Marketing Manager — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- A one-sentence positioning statement exists and every asset traces back to it.
- A named target audience with evidence it exists and is reachable — not "everyone."
- A defined first-listener source and a path from there to scale.
- Budget approved and within envelope before any spend commits.
- Launch date jointly signed with the operations-director and delivery-feasible with the release-coordinator.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Reach with no retention — buying attention the record cannot convert.
- A campaign with no point of view — generic assets that say nothing specific.
- Positioning that quietly contradicts the artistic identity the ar-director protects.
- Locking a launch to a "moment" that delivery cannot actually hit.
- Spending the same conviction thin across too many simultaneous releases.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Positioning statement is one sentence and every asset serves it.
- [ ] Target audience is named, evidenced, and reachable.
- [ ] Channel mix maps to where that audience actually is.
- [ ] Budget is approved and tracked against the envelope.
- [ ] Date is jointly signed (COO) and confirmed deliverable (coordinator).
- [ ] Retention/follow-on metric is defined, not just reach.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Reach-to-listen conversion rate per campaign.
- First-week saves and retention curve.
- Owned-audience growth attributable to the release.
- Playlist/editorial placement rate against pitches sent.
- Cost per retained listener, not cost per impression.

## Risk lens
- **Audience risk** — targeting a crowd that does not exist or cannot be reached.
- **Coherence risk** — positioning that erodes the catalog's identity.
- **Timing risk** — missing the cultural or editorial window the campaign was built around.
- **Spend risk** — committing budget that outruns conviction or the envelope.
