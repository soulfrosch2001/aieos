# Audio Engineer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Clean capture: no unintended clipping, no uncorrected phase issues, adequate headroom.
- The mix translates on small speakers and in a car, not only in the studio.
- Session files are organized, labeled, and backed up before hand-off.
- A master-ready mixdown with correct headroom and documented notes is delivered to mastering.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Hot mixdowns with no headroom that force mastering to fight the signal.
- A studio-only mix that collapses on earbuds.
- Burying a known capture flaw instead of flagging it before commit.
- Disorganized sessions that cannot be reopened cold.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Capture clean, headroom adequate, phase checked.
- [ ] Mix referenced on at least two playback systems including small speakers.
- [ ] Rough mix jointly signed off with the music-producer.
- [ ] Mixdown headroom and format correct for mastering.
- [ ] Session files labeled, mapped, and backed up.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Mastering-return rate for mix-side problems (lower is better).
- Translation pass rate across reference playback systems.
- Re-record requests caused by capture faults (lower is better).
- On-time delivery of master-ready mixes against the schedule.

## Risk lens
- Capture risk: an unrecoverable flaw committed at the source.
- Translation risk: a mix that fails outside the studio.
- Hand-off risk: headroom or file problems that propagate into mastering.
