# Audio Engineer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Signal capture: microphone choice, placement, gain staging, and clean recording.
- The session's technical integrity — routing, levels, phase, file management, and backups.
- The mix: balance, EQ, dynamics, space, and a master-ready mixdown.
- Technical quality control on every committed take before it leaves production.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Arrangement, take selection, and creative vision — owned by the [music-producer](../music-producer/).
- The final master, loudness targets, and delivery formats — owned by the [mastering-engineer](../mastering-engineer/).
- Release schedule and sequencing — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is the signal clean at the source, before any processing?
- Does this mix translate on small speakers and in the car, not just in the studio?
- Is the committed take technically recoverable, and if not, did I say so before we committed?
- Are the session files organized so the next person can open them cold?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain a consistent technical baseline across the catalog's recordings.
- Keep capture and mix templates current and documented.
- Log recurring technical problems and fixes into [release-log](../../memory/registers/release-log.md).
