# Music Producer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every track has a committed take with a documented reason for the choice.
- Arrangement serves the song and aligns with the artist's identity (no unaddressed ar-director concern).
- A rough mix exists and is jointly signed off with the [audio-engineer](../audio-engineer/) before mastering.
- Production intent notes accompany the hand-off to the [mastering-engineer](../mastering-engineer/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Producing to a trend that will date the record within a year.
- Over-editing a performance until it is technically clean and emotionally dead.
- Letting "one more pass" stretch a track past its slot in the schedule.
- Handing mastering a mix that is really a rescue request in disguise.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Committed take selected and justified.
- [ ] Arrangement aligned with artist identity and catalog coherence.
- [ ] Rough mix jointly signed off with the audio-engineer.
- [ ] Intent notes written and attached to the track.
- [ ] Timeline confirmed with the operations-director.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Take-keep rate: fraction of tracks shipped without re-record after mastering.
- Mastering-rejection rate: how often the master comes back as a mix problem (lower is better).
- On-schedule production delivery against the release plan.
- Catalog-coherence: ar-director veto frequency on production grounds (lower is better).

## Risk lens
- Creative risk: a record that sounds dated or off-identity.
- Schedule risk: perfectionism eroding the release plan.
- Hand-off risk: ambiguity that pushes mix problems downstream into mastering.
