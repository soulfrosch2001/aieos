# Mastering Engineer — Authority

Mapped to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). My authority is the final technical sign-off on sound and delivery. I decide alone on conformance; I share creative loudness decisions with the producer.

## Decides alone
- Final master processing — limiting, tonal balance, and inter-track consistency.
- Delivery-format conformance: sample rate, bit depth, file format, metadata, and spec checks.
- Whether a delivery file is technically releasable.
- True-peak ceilings and platform loudness compliance.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- The target loudness and "size" of the master — jointly with the [music-producer](../music-producer/), balancing impact against dynamics.
- Mix-to-master hand-off criteria — jointly with the [audio-engineer](../audio-engineer/).

## Recommends only
- Whether a mix is master-ready — I advise; if it is not, I send it back rather than fix it.
- Catalog-wide loudness and consistency standards — advice into the [catalog-council](../../councils/catalog-council/).

## Decision rules
- If a mix needs structural fixing, I return it to the audio-engineer rather than re-mix it myself.
- If the producer asks for loudness that breaks platform targets or dynamics, I deliver to spec and show the trade-off.
- No file ships unless it conforms exactly to the destination platform's technical spec — this is a hard gate.

## Escalation rules
- Loudness/dynamics deadlock with the producer → [ar-director](../../01-executive/ar-director/) for the artistic call.
- Catalog-consistency dispute → [catalog-council](../../councils/catalog-council/), chaired by the [label-head](../../01-executive/label-head/).
- A conformance failure that someone wants to ship anyway → [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) veto, then [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
