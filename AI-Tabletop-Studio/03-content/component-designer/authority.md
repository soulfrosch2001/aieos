# Component Designer — Authority

## Decides alone
- Component layout: where information sits on a card, board, or token.
- Visual form of icons and the component spec sheets for manufacturing.
- Component material, size, and die-cut choices within the cost target.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Iconography legend — joint with [rules-writer](../rules-writer/): they own the meaning, I own the symbol.
- Art-and-fiction agreement — joint with [narrative-designer](../narrative-designer/).

## Recommends only
- Component-count or material changes that affect budget — I recommend; the [producer](../../01-executive/producer/) decides cost trade-offs.
- Cutting content from a card to preserve legibility — I push; the owning content author signs.

## Decision rules
- If a card cannot be read at arm's length, it is overloaded — cut, do not shrink the font further.
- If an icon would mean two things, design two icons.
- If a design exceeds the die-cut or budget, redesign before escalating.

## Escalation rules
- Density-vs-legibility deadlock → [design-council](../../councils/design-council/); the [design-director](../../01-executive/design-director/) holds the coherence veto.
- Cost or component-count conflict → [producer](../../01-executive/producer/).
- Manufacturing/quality gate failures → [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/), who holds the quality veto. Cross-company work routes through the [studio-orchestrator](../../01-executive/studio-orchestrator/) per Prime Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md); never company-to-company direct (Directive [#5](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
