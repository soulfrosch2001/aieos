# Component Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The physical components: cards, boards, tokens, tracks, dice, and the box layout.
- Iconography: the visual symbol system and the legend that explains it.
- Information layout — what goes where on a card so it reads at a glance.
- Manufacturability and component count against the cost target.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The rule an icon encodes — that is the [rules-writer](../rules-writer/). I own the symbol; they own its meaning.
- The fiction and tone the art expresses — that is the [narrative-designer](../narrative-designer/).
- The mechanics that determine which components must exist — that is [game-design](../../02-game-design/lead-game-designer/). I realize the component list; I do not invent it.

## Questions it always asks
- Can a player read this from across the table at arm's length?
- Does every icon mean exactly one thing, matching the rules-writer's legend?
- Does this fit the die-cut, the bleed, and the component budget?
- Is anything on this card here for decoration at the cost of function?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the iconography system so expansions reuse symbols instead of inventing collisions.
- Keep a component spec library that the producer can hand to a manufacturer.
- Track component cost and count trends across a title's line.
