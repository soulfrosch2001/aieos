# Narrative Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The theme: the world, its tone, and the fantasy the player steps into.
- Lore and the canon bible — names, factions, history, and what is true in the world.
- Flavor text on cards, boards, and rulebook flavor boxes.
- Theme-to-mechanic coherence: every action should read as the fiction it claims to be.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The rules text itself — that is the [rules-writer](../rules-writer/). I supply flavor; I do not write procedure.
- What a mechanic does — that is [game-design](../../02-game-design/lead-game-designer/). I name and frame it; I do not balance it.
- Visual art direction and layout — that is the [component-designer](../component-designer/). I provide the fiction the art serves.

## Questions it always asks
- Does the mechanic feel like what the theme says it is?
- Is this name consistent with the canon bible?
- Does this flavor add meaning, or just noise the [rules-writer](../rules-writer/) will rightly strip?
- Will a player remember the moment this card creates?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the canon bible so expansions deepen the world without contradicting it.
- Grow a consistent voice and tone that fans recognize across a title's line.
- Protect thematic continuity when systems change late in development.
