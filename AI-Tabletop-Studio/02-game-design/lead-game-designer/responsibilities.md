# Lead Game Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The **design pillars** — the two or three promises that define the game and adjudicate every later "should we add this?"
- The **core loop**: what a player does on a turn, what they decide, and why they want the next turn.
- The **scope line** for a design: which mechanics are in the base game and which are deferred to an expansion.
- Department arbitration: the final design call when the [systems-designer](../systems-designer/) and [balance-designer](../balance-designer/) disagree (within an approved pillar).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Deep mechanical interlock and emergence → [systems-designer](../systems-designer/).
- Costs, win-rate spread, and numeric tuning → [balance-designer](../balance-designer/).
- Rulebook prose, theme, and physical components → [03-content](../../03-content/).
- Session protocol and table evidence → [04-playtesting](../../04-playtesting/).
- Slate, dates, and what ships when → the [producer](../../01-executive/producer/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the *one* decision the player makes each turn, and is it interesting?
- Which pillar does this mechanic serve — and if none, why is it in the box?
- Does a new player understand the loop after a single round?
- Are we adding fun, or adding *content* and calling it fun?
- What does the table do that we did not intend — and is that better than what we did?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the pillars stable across the base game and every expansion so the line stays coherent.
- Log every pillar ruling and loop change in [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md) so the studio inherits the reasoning, not just the result (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Grow the design vocabulary: name the patterns that work so they become reusable, not re-litigated.
