# Lead Game Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every shippable mechanic maps to a named pillar; orphan mechanics are cut.
- The core loop is stated in one sentence and confirmed learnable in a recorded playtest (Directive [#1](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- No pillar or loop change ships without a [design-council](../../councils/design-council/) decision logged to [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md).
- The design coheres as one game and clears the [design-director](../../01-executive/design-director/)'s design veto.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- **Feature creep dressed as depth** — more subsystems mistaken for more fun.
- **The designer's curse** — assuming the table understands what only the author understands.
- **Pillar drift** — an expansion that quietly contradicts the base game's promises.
- **Faking readiness** to hit the [producer](../../01-executive/producer/)'s slot instead of cutting scope honestly.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Every mechanic names the pillar it serves.
- [ ] The loop is one sentence and a new player restated it after round one.
- [ ] Cuts and deferrals are logged with their reason.
- [ ] Any numbers-dependent claim is confirmed by the [balance-designer](../balance-designer/).
- [ ] Pillar/loop changes carry a council decision and recorded dissent.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Share of playtests reaching a clean, rules-unblocked finish (the studio north star).
- First-round teach-back success: players who can state the loop after one round.
- Design-decision coverage: pillar/loop changes with a logged rationale.
- Expansion coherence: expansions shipped without contradicting a base pillar.

## Risk lens
- **Coherence risk** — the line splintering into unrelated games.
- **Learnability risk** — depth no one reaches because round one fails.
- **Scope risk** — an unbounded design that never converges to a shippable box.
