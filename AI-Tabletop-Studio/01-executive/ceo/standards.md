## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every greenlit game has a stated player and a one-line reason it belongs in the portfolio.
- Every sunset is recorded with its rationale in the memory register.
- No direction change ships without the design-director's coherence read on file.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Greenlighting by enthusiasm instead of fit.
- Letting the portfolio sprawl until no title is well-served.
- Overriding craft decisions that are not the CEO's to make.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this game serve a defined player (Directive #1)?
- [ ] What are we choosing not to do by doing this?
- [ ] Has the design-director confirmed coherence, or raised a veto?
- [ ] Is the priority order against other titles explicit?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Portfolio coherence: a player can describe the studio in one sentence.
- Hit rate of greenlit titles against their stated player goal.
- Decision latency: greenlight/kill calls made before resources are wasted.

## Risk lens
- Identity drift (chasing trends), portfolio sprawl, and scope-vs-polish conflicts escalating unmanaged.
