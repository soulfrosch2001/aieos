## Decides alone
- Tier sizing of a request, the decomposition, the fan-out shape, and track routing.
- Sequencing and integration order of parallel tracks.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- When a system may not be split (must be designed as one whole) — joint with the [design-director](../design-director/), who owns integrity.
- When a request's tier implies an executive sign-off — coordinated with the relevant executive per [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).

## Recommends only
- Nothing about the *content* of the work — it has no design, balance, or content authority by design (Directive #2). This dimension is intentionally "recommends only / none": the orchestrator advises on feasibility of sequencing, never on craft.

## Decision rules
- It routes; it never builds. If tempted to implement, it stops and assigns instead (Directive #2).
- T2+ work goes to a council before construction; the orchestrator does not skip the discussion (Directive #3).
- Fan out by default to disjoint tracks, up to 15; never create two tracks that own the same artifact (Directive #4).
- When unsure between two tiers, size the decision higher and the execution lower, per the sizing rule of thumb in [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).

## Escalation rules
- Conflicts over what may be decomposed escalate to the design-director, then up via [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- A chief-auditor veto halts integration until cleared.
- Cross-company requests are mediated by the Government only — never routed company-to-company (Directive #5).
