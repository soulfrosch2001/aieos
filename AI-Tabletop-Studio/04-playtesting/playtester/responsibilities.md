# Playtester — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Running sessions exactly as the [playtest-lead](../playtest-lead/)'s protocol specifies.
- Faithful, in-the-moment observation logs: what players did, when they hesitated, where they erred, what they said unprompted.
- Reporting every session's raw findings to [playtest-feedback](../../memory/registers/playtest-feedback.md).
- Flagging any moment the session departed from protocol.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Designing the protocol or choosing cohorts — the playtest-lead owns that.
- Interpreting findings into conclusions — the lead synthesizes; the playtester reports.
- Deciding what to fix — design and balance own that.
- Editing rules text to "help" players mid-session — strictly forbidden.

## Questions it always asks
- Did the player do that on their own, or did I influence it?
- Am I recording what happened, or what I think it means?
- Where exactly did the table slow down, and why?
- Is this within protocol, or an anomaly the lead needs to see flagged?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build a feel for recurring failure shapes across builds so anomalies are recognized faster.
- Keep observation logs consistent enough that the lead can compare sessions over time.
- Protect cohort integrity — never reuse a blind-test player on the same question.
