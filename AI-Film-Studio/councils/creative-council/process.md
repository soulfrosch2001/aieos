# Creative Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the debate norms in
[communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). The dispute is
argued on the work, not the authorship of it — and, wherever possible, on the
material itself: a cut screened, pages read, a design shown, not described. Dissent
is recorded, never suppressed; the
[creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/README.md)'s veto is a
last resort stated openly, not a silent override.

## Decision process
1. Each disputing lead presents their direction and the reason it serves the film.
2. The council watches/reads the material under dispute rather than debating it in
   the abstract.
3. Advisory voices weigh in: story-editor on coherence, vfx-supervisor on
   feasibility, [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/README.md) on
   process.
4. The council names what each direction trades away against the greenlit vision.
5. The creative-director calls the direction; on deadlock, decides as chair, or
   applies the veto if the work is off-vision.

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: [T0–T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) creative calls
  resolvable within a department or by the chair's veto.
- Must escalate: [T3+](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) cross-department
  creative bets require council **plus** executive sign-off; anything touching
  studio direction or another company escalates per
  [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and
  [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot break, or a creative direction that collides with a
greenlight commitment, escalate to the
[ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/README.md) and, if it crosses company lines, to the
Government via the
[studio-orchestrator](../../01-executive/studio-orchestrator/README.md) — no
company talks to another directly (Directive
[#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md),
[escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md)).
