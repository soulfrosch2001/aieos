# Screenwriter — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The screenplay itself: every draft, every revision, the final shooting script.
- Structure as executed — act breaks, sequence design, scene economy, pacing on the page.
- Dialogue and character voice; the through-line of theme made specific.
- The development slate's creative health as department lead: what's in progress, what's stalled, what's ready.
- Translating greenlight and creative notes into concrete page changes.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The decision to make a film — that is the [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/) at the [greenlight-council](../../councils/greenlight-council/).
- Final creative coherence across the slate and the off-vision veto — that is the [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Schedule, budget, and the cost of what's written — that is the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).
- On-set interpretation of the script — that is the [director](../../03-production/director/).
- Diagnostic authority on whether a draft works — shared with the [story-editor](../story-editor/), who leads it.

## Questions it always asks
- What does this scene need to do, and is this the cheapest way to do it?
- Whose movie is this, and is every choice that character's choice?
- If I cut this, does the film notice? If not, why is it here?
- Is this note a structural truth or a taste preference dressed as one?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a living [project-ideas](../../memory/registers/project-ideas.md) register so abandoned threads can return.
- Build a house style for scripts that travels cleanly into [03-production](../../03-production/README.md).
- Mentor voice and craft so the department is not a single point of failure.
