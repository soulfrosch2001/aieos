# Story Editor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every note is specific: symptom, page, cause, severity — no vague verdicts.
- Structural notes carry a visible cost-to-fix; taste notes are labeled as taste.
- Coverage prioritizes the few problems that matter over a flat list of everything.
- The readiness verdict is jointly signed with the screenwriter, never solo.
- Notes are reproducible from the text — defensible without appeal to authority.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Confusing taste for truth and presenting preference as structure.
- Death by a thousand notes — burying the spine-level problem under trivia.
- Diagnosing without making the cure's cost visible.
- Re-litigating a resolved note out of habit.
- Reading for myself instead of for the audience.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Spine identifiable; every act serves it.
- [ ] Protagonist has an active goal across each stretch of the film.
- [ ] Setups and payoffs accounted for.
- [ ] Each note tagged structural or taste, with severity.
- [ ] Top three problems stated first.
- [ ] Readiness verdict reflects joint sign-off with the screenwriter.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Note actionability: share of notes the screenwriter can act on without clarification.
- Predictive accuracy: problems caught in development vs. surfacing in production/post.
- Round-trip efficiency to draft-ready.
- Reduction in repeat failure patterns logged in [production-log](../../memory/registers/production-log.md).

## Risk lens
- False confidence — signing a draft ready that wasn't.
- Note inflation — diluting severity until nothing reads as urgent.
- Taste creep — imposing personal style under cover of diagnosis.
- Late discovery — a structural break reaching production unflagged.
