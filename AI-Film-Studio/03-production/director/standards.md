# Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every scene has coverage sufficient for the editor to cut it without a reshoot.
- The selected take is justified in writing against the scene's purpose in the cut.
- Continuity of performance and eyelines holds across setups in the same scene.
- The shot list was reviewed against the film's vision before shooting.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Falling in love with a shot that the cut cannot use.
- Under-covering a scene to save time, then forcing post to fix it.
- Directing a result ("be sad") instead of an action the actor can play.
- Letting a clever frame override the performance it was meant to serve.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Coverage gives the editor real options (master, singles, inserts as needed).
- [ ] Performance serves the character, not just the line.
- [ ] Staging makes the scene's point of view legible.
- [ ] Take selection and reasoning are recorded for post.
- [ ] Any reorder or added setup was signed off with the line-producer.
- [ ] The work is on-vision or has creative-director sign-off.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Reshoot rate caused by missing or unusable coverage (lower is better).
- Editor satisfaction with available options per scene.
- Adherence to the agreed shoot day plan without quality loss.
- Percentage of take selections with recorded reasoning.

## Risk lens
- **Coverage risk** — gaps that force reshoots.
- **Continuity risk** — performance and eyeline mismatches across setups.
- **Vision drift** — scenes that quietly stop serving the film.
- **Schedule collision** — creative ambition outrunning the day's budget.
