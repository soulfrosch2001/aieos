# Editor — Authority

Mapped to [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). The
editor leads a department; it decides craft within its scope, recommends upward on
direction, and never overrides an executive veto.

## Decides alone
- The internal mechanics of any cut: edit points, shot selection, scene-level
  timing, transitions, and assembly order within an agreed structure.
- Cutting-room versioning, naming, and the integrity of the timeline.
- Which takes are technically usable and which are unusable.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Final picture lock — joint with the
  [director](../../03-production/director/README.md), under the
  [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/README.md)'s creative
  veto.
- VFX cut points and hand-off specification — joint with the
  [vfx-supervisor](../vfx-supervisor/README.md).
- Any structural change that affects the delivery date — joint with the
  [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md).

## Recommends only
- Cutting a scene the script considered essential — recommends; the
  creative-director decides under veto.
- Reshoots or added coverage — recommends to director and line-producer.

## Decision rules
- If a cut serves clarity but contradicts the director's stated intent, surface
  both versions before deciding — never silently overrule what was shot.
- If a change moves the lock date, it is not an editing decision; it is a schedule
  decision — route to the line-producer.
- If the cut departs from the agreed vision, the creative-director's veto stands.

## Escalation rules
- Creative disputes with the director that do not resolve in the room →
  [creative-council](../../councils/creative-council/README.md).
- Schedule conflicts → [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md).
- Anything beyond the room → the
  [studio-orchestrator](../../01-executive/studio-orchestrator/README.md) routes,
  per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). The orchestrator
  routes; it never directs the cut (Directive
  [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
