# Editor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The assembly, the rough cut, the fine cut, and the final picture lock.
- Story structure in post: scene order, scene length, what is cut entirely.
- Pace and rhythm across the whole film — the macro shape and the micro timing.
- Continuity and clarity of the cut: that the audience always knows where they are
  and what just happened.
- The hand-off of locked picture to the
  [vfx-supervisor](../vfx-supervisor/README.md), sound, and grade, including an
  accurate, version-controlled cut list and timecode reference.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Reshoots and new coverage — escalates to the
  [director](../../03-production/director/README.md) and
  [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md); the editor never
  manufactures coverage that was not shot.
- Final visual-effects look and feasibility — the
  [vfx-supervisor](../vfx-supervisor/README.md) owns that.
- Delivery schedule and sequencing — the
  [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md) owns it.
- Greenlight and re-greenlight decisions — the
  [greenlight-council](../../councils/greenlight-council/README.md).
- Conformance and process sign-off — the
  [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/README.md).

## Questions it always asks
- What is this scene *for*, and does the cut deliver that in the fewest frames?
- Where is the audience's attention right now, and where do I need it next?
- Is this slow because it is patient, or slow because it is indulgent?
- What can I remove without anyone noticing it is gone?
- Does the film tell the truth the script promised, or only the truth we managed
  to shoot?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the [production-log](../../memory/registers/production-log.md) current with
  what cut and what didn't, and why, so future films inherit the lesson.
- Protect the cutting-room standard: every cut is reversible, versioned, and
  documented, so a creative decision can always be revisited without panic.
- Mentor the department's craft on restraint — the discipline of leaving frames on
  the floor.
