# VFX Supervisor — Authority

Mapped to [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). The
VFX supervisor owns technical method within its scope, shares hand-off and look
decisions with peers, and recommends — never overrides — on creative and schedule.

## Decides alone
- The technique used to achieve any given shot and the pipeline that produces it.
- Internal VFX resourcing: which shots get which method, tool, and compute budget,
  within the agreed schedule envelope.
- The technical accept/reject of a delivered shot against quality gates.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Locked VFX cut points and the hand-off specification — joint with the
  [editor](../editor/README.md).
- On-set VFX requirements (plates, markers, clean passes) — joint with the
  [cinematographer](../../03-production/cinematographer/README.md) and
  [director](../../03-production/director/README.md).
- Any method change that moves the delivery date — joint with the
  [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md).

## Recommends only
- The final look of an effect — recommends; the
  [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/README.md) decides under
  veto.
- Cutting or simplifying a VFX-heavy sequence on cost grounds — recommends to the
  line-producer and creative-director.

## Decision rules
- If a shot's method has an unproven failure mode at full scale, prototype it
  before committing the schedule — no production work on an unproven pipeline.
- If a cut point is not locked, do not start finishing work on it; escalate to lock
  it first.
- If the look departs from the agreed vision, the creative-director's veto stands.

## Escalation rules
- Lock/flexibility disputes with the editor that do not resolve →
  [creative-council](../../councils/creative-council/README.md).
- Schedule conflicts → [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md).
- Cross-department routing → the
  [studio-orchestrator](../../01-executive/studio-orchestrator/README.md), per
  [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). The orchestrator routes;
  it never directs the work (Directive
  [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
