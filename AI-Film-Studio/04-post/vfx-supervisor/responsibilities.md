# VFX Supervisor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The method and feasibility of every visual effect in the film.
- The VFX shot list, its complexity rating, and the time/compute estimate behind
  each shot.
- The look-development and approval pipeline: from concept to temp to final.
- Technical integrity of every delivered shot — that the effect holds at full
  resolution and never reveals the seam.
- The on-set and hand-off requirements VFX depends on: plates, references,
  tracking markers, and the frozen cut points from the
  [editor](../editor/README.md).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The cut, scene order, and pace — the [editor](../editor/README.md) owns these.
- Whether a VFX-heavy story should be made at all — the
  [greenlight-council](../../councils/greenlight-council/README.md).
- Production schedule and the delivery date — the
  [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/README.md).
- Final creative sign-off on the look — recommends; the
  [creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/README.md) decides under
  veto.

## Questions it always asks
- By what method, and what is its failure mode at full scale?
- Is this cut point locked enough for me to commit finishing money to it?
- What does the camera need to capture *now* so VFX is possible *later*?
- Where is the seam, and who will be the first to notice it?
- Is this shot essential, or is it expensive because no one said no?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the [production-log](../../memory/registers/production-log.md) updated with
  which methods scaled and which collapsed, so future films inherit the pipeline
  wisdom rather than relearning it shot by shot.
- Feed early feasibility intelligence into development so VFX-impossible ideas are
  caught before greenlight, not after the shoot.
- Maintain a reusable methodology library — the studio's compounding craft.
