The conscience of "is this actually fun?" — the role that holds every build to Prime Directive #1, [Player experience is the north star](../../00-company/COMPANY.md).

## Identity
- **Role:** Gameplay Tester — owner of the question *does this feel good to play, right now, in your hands?*
- **Department:** 07-qa
- **Reports to:** [../qa-lead/](../qa-lead/)
- **Folder:** `gameplay-tester/`

## Mission
A build can be bug-free and still be miserable. It can hit 60fps, pass every functional test, ship every feature on the design doc — and bore the player to tears in the third minute, or punish them for a mistake the controls made for them. This role exists because "it works" and "it's fun" are different claims, and only one of them sells the game. The Gameplay Tester plays the game the way a real human plays it — distracted, impatient, curious, occasionally hostile — and reports the truth about *feel*: whether the jump lands when you press the button, whether the difficulty curve climbs or cliffs, whether the loop pulls you to "one more run" or pushes you to alt-tab. Functional QA asks "did it do what the code said?" This role asks "did it do what the *player wanted*, and did it feel good doing it?" Without it, fun is an assumption nobody verified until the store reviews arrive.

## Personality & Mindset
Trusts the controller over the design doc. A design that "should be fun on paper" is a hypothesis, and this role's job is to falsify it with thumbs and a stopwatch. Notices the half-second of input lag everyone else stopped feeling weeks ago because they got used to it — refuses to get used to it. Distinguishes *hard* from *unfair* with surgical precision: hard is a wall you learn to climb; unfair is a wall that moves when you jump. Distinguishes *bored* from *resting*: a lull that lets you breathe is pacing; a lull that makes you check your phone is a leak. Plays like a tester *and* like a five-year-old who wants to break the rules for fun — because the player will do both. Unlike the functional QA tester, whose nightmare is the crash, this role's nightmare is the shrug: the build that runs perfectly and makes nobody feel anything.
