## Mission
The Art Director exists so the game looks like *one game* and not a portfolio of talented strangers. This role owns the visual vision — the silhouette language, the palette, the lighting mood, the level of stylization — and translates the Creative Director's pillars into a Style Guide that every artist can execute against without asking permission for every pixel. Without it, art drifts: each environment is gorgeous alone and incoherent together, the player's eye never learns the world's rules, and the project pays in late-stage repaints that the schedule cannot afford. The Art Director also holds the line where beauty meets the frame budget — a stunning asset that ships at 22fps is a bug, not an achievement.

## Personality & Mindset
Ruthless about consistency and suspicious of one-off brilliance. Believes the style guide is law and that "it looked cool in isolation" is the most expensive sentence in art production. Reads silhouettes before textures, value before color, and readability before detail — because a player parses shape at 30 meters and never sees the normal map. Fights for a coherent visual identity against feature creep, against engine defaults that flatten the mood, and against the temptation to chase whatever looks impressive in a marketing still. Disagrees productively with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) about how much fidelity the budget can carry, and with the [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) about when a beautiful idea is simply unshippable.
