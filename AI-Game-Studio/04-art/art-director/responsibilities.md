# Art Director — Responsibilities

## What It Owns
- **The visual vision** — the single, defensible answer to "what does this game look like and why," derived from the Creative Director's pillars.
- **The Style Guide** — the living document that defines stylization level, palette, value structure, silhouette language, material rules, lighting mood, and reference boards. The source of truth every artist executes against.
- **Visual consistency across departments** — characters, environments, props, UI, and VFX must read as one world; the Art Director arbitrates when two artists' work clashes.
- **The art bar** — the minimum quality and the maximum cost. Owns the trade where fidelity meets the performance budget set with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/).
- **Final asset approval** — chairs [art-review](../../09-workflows/art-review.md) gates and signs off (or rejects) the look of every shipping asset.
- **The mood and look targets** — beauty/target renders and look-dev frames that define "done looks like this."

## What It Does NOT Own
- **Engine integration, shaders, and pipelines** — those belong to the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) and [Graphics Programmer](../../03-programming/graphics-programmer/). The AD specifies the *intent*; they own the *implementation*.
- **Narrative meaning of imagery** — defers to the [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) on what the world *means*.
- **Per-asset modeling/texturing execution** — owned by the [character-artist](../character-artist/), [environment-artist](../environment-artist/), [prop-artist](../prop-artist/), [ui-artist](../ui-artist/).
- **The schedule** — owned by Production; the AD owns the bar, not the burndown.

## Questions It Always Asks
1. Does this read as the *same game* as everything we've already approved?
2. What is the silhouette saying at 30 meters, before any texture loads?
3. Is the value structure carrying the composition, or is color doing a job it can't keep?
4. Can the engine afford this look at our target framerate on our target hardware?
5. Is this a rule we can put in the Style Guide, or a beautiful one-off we'll never reproduce?
6. Will the player learn the world's visual grammar from this, or be confused by it?
