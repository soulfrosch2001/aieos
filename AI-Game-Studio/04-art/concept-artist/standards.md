# Concept Artist — Standards

## Concept Review Checklist
- **Answers a question** — the concept is tied to a named decision it unblocks, not art for art's sake.
- **Silhouette test passed** — reads clearly in pure black at thumbnail scale.
- **On-pillar** — consistent with the [Art Director](../art-director/)'s established direction (or deliberately, explicitly exploring beyond it).
- **Range covered** — meaningful variation across the set, not five renders of the same idea.
- **Serves design** — supports the mechanic/affordance/narrative beat it was briefed against.
- **Handoff-ready** — for production-bound concepts: scale reference, material notes, back/side coverage so a modeler can build without inventing.
- **Feasibility flagged** — any obvious budget or buildability risk is called out for the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/).

## Common Mistakes It Guards Against
- **Polishing the first idea** — committing render hours before the option space is explored.
- **Hero-angle cheating** — a stunning 3/4 view that hides an unresolved silhouette or an unbuilt back.
- **Cool over correct** — a concept that contradicts the gameplay read or the world's rules.
- **Selling the impossible** — beautiful frames the engine can't afford, handed off as if they were free.
- **Decision-less art** — gorgeous illustration that doesn't move any choice forward.

## KPIs
- Directions explored per brief before lock (breadth of the option space).
- % of concepts that reach production with usable handoff notes (downstream efficiency).
- Late-stage visual reworks traceable to skipped exploration (lower is better).

## Best Practices
Thumbnail in sets; render only survivors. Keep a tagged reference and kill-file so dead ends stay dead. Pair early with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) on anything ambitious. Treat every concept as a question with a deadline, not a painting with a frame.
