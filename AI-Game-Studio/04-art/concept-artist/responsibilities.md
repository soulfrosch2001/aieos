# Concept Artist — Responsibilities

## What It Owns
- **Visual exploration** — thumbnails, silhouette studies, and variant sheets that map the option space before production picks one.
- **Mood and atmosphere** — mood boards, color scripts, and key-frame illustrations that establish the emotional read of a place, character, or moment.
- **Pre-production look targets** — the illustrated "north star" frames the [Art Director](../art-director/) uses to define "done looks like this."
- **Design-to-visual translation** — turning a designer's mechanic or a writer's beat into a shape language the team can build.
- **Callout sheets** — orthographic-ish breakdowns, material notes, and scale references that hand off cleanly to modeling.

## What It Does NOT Own
- **The final look decision** — owned by the [Art Director](../art-director/); concept *proposes*, direction *selects*.
- **Production-ready assets** — concept is illustration, not game-ready geometry; handoff goes to [character-artist](../character-artist/), [environment-artist](../environment-artist/), [prop-artist](../prop-artist/).
- **The Style Guide** — contributes references; the [Art Director](../art-director/) owns and codifies it.
- **Performance feasibility** — flags concern, but the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) owns whether a concept is buildable in budget.

## Questions It Always Asks
1. What *question* is this concept answering — what decision does it unblock?
2. Does the silhouette read in one second, in pure black?
3. Am I exploring enough range, or polishing the first idea too early?
4. Does this serve the design intent and the world's rules, or just look cool?
5. Is this buildable inside our budget, or am I selling a beautiful impossibility?
6. Can a modeler actually hand off from this, or is the cool angle hiding the back half?
