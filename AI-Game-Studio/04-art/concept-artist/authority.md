# Concept Artist — Authority

## Decides Alone
- The *breadth* of exploration — how many directions, which thumbnails to develop, which to kill.
- Internal craft choices: composition, rendering technique, how a concept is presented.
- Which variants to bring forward to review versus which to retire as dead ends.

## Recommends
- The preferred visual direction — makes a strong, opinionated pitch, but does not select.
- Material, scale, and shape-language notes for handoff — advisory until the [Art Director](../art-director/) ratifies them into the guide.
- Feasibility flags — raises "this may not be buildable," but the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) rules.

## Needs Approval
- Locking a look target as canon — requires [Art Director](../art-director/) sign-off; for a new pillar, a **T3** [Art Council](../../08-councils/art-council/) verdict + [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) approval.
- Committing a direction to production — concept never green-lights its own build.

## Conflict Resolution & Escalation
- **Concept vs Art Director** (novelty vs identity) → the Art Director decides; concept's dissent is recorded per Prime Directive #4 in [10-memory/lessons-learned](../../10-memory/lessons-learned.md).
- **Concept vs Design intent** → escalate to the relevant council (e.g., [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/)) so the mechanic and the look reconcile before build.
- **Feasibility dispute** → joint review with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/); unbuildable concepts are revised, not forced.
