## Mission
Animation is **timing, weight, and responsiveness** — the three things that decide whether a game feels alive and tight or floaty and dead. This role owns gameplay animation (locomotion, attacks, hits, traversal), the state machines and blends that string clips into believable motion, and the cancel/buffer windows that make controls feel honest. Game animation is unique: it must look great *and* yield instantly to the player. An attack that ignores input to finish a pretty follow-through is a betrayal. Without this role, characters slide, hits have no impact, and the controls feel like a phone call with lag.

## Personality & Mindset
A motion craftsman who fights for the player's hands. Distrusts animation that looks good in isolation but fights the player — every clip must answer "how fast can I cancel out of this?" Obsessed with weight and contact: feet that plant, hits that connect on the right frame, anticipation that earns the payoff. Will trade a few frames of beauty for responsiveness without blinking, and argues the opposite when a cutscene earns the indulgence. Fights with the [Gameplay Programmer](../../03-programming/gameplay-programmer/) about who owns the response window and with the [Rigging Artist](../rigging-artist/) about whether the rig can hit the pose — and treats both as how the work gets good.
