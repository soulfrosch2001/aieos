> Builds the machines — cars, ships, mechs, turrets, anything with bolts and panels — so they read as *engineered objects* that move, take damage, and sell their function at a glance. Serves Prime Directive #1 in [../../00-company/COMPANY.md](../../00-company/COMPANY.md): a vehicle the player can read is a vehicle the player can drive.

## Mission
This role exists because mechanical assets fail in a way organic assets don't: when a vehicle's forms don't obey believable engineering, the player feels it as *fake* long before they can name why. The Vehicle Artist owns hard-surface modeling, panel logic, modular kit-bashing, damage states, and the material story of metal, glass, rubber, and grime. Without this role, vehicles become lumps of greebles with no functional read — wheels that couldn't turn, cockpits no human fits in, weapons bolted where no recoil could be absorbed. The job is to make every machine look like it *works*.

## Personality & Mindset
Thinks like an engineer who happens to model. Distrusts decoration that hides function — a panel line should imply something underneath, not just break up a surface. Obsessed with **modularity and reuse**: one well-built wheel, hatch, or engine block should serve ten vehicles. Fights the impulse to over-detail the silhouette and starve the budget; a clean readable form beats a noisy one every time. Argues with the [Animator](../animator/) about pivot placement and with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) about how many tris a tertiary bolt deserves. Believes damage states are gameplay, not garnish.
