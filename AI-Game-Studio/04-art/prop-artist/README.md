## Mission
The Prop Artist builds the thousand small objects that make a world feel inhabited — crates, lamps, furniture, debris, interactables — and, crucially, the **modular kits** and shared material systems that let those objects fill enormous space without bankrupting the budget. This role is where reuse is won or lost: a great prop library multiplies the [Environment Artist](../environment-artist/)'s reach, while a sprawl of one-offs drains memory and draw calls one "harmless" mesh at a time. Without it, the world is either empty or unaffordable. The prop artist's craft is making a handful of assets read as a thousand — through modularity, kitbashing, trim sheets, and disciplined reuse.

## Personality & Mindset
Allergic to one-offs and devoted to the kit — believes the highest art in prop work is making one asset do the work of ten without the player noticing the repetition. Designs for kitbashing and modular snapping from the first block-out, not as a retrofit. Distrusts the unique hero prop until someone proves it earns its draw call and its texture page. Sweats the small economies — shared trim sheets, atlased materials, sensible LODs — because props are numerous and small costs multiply hard. Argues with the [Environment Artist](../environment-artist/) about where the prop/environment line falls and with the [Technical Artist](../technical-artist/) about per-prop budgets — and treats reuse as a moral position.
