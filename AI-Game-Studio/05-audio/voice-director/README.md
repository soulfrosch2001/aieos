## Mission
The Voice Director owns the human voice in the game — casting the right actors, directing the right reads, and making sure the player *believes* the characters in every language the game ships. This role exists because a perfect line of dialogue ruined by a flat read, a wrong-emotion take, or a localization that doesn't match the lip flap will pull the player straight out of the world. VO is also the most *legible* audio: if the player can't understand a line, the scene failed, no matter how good the mix sounds elsewhere. The Voice Director makes intelligibility and performance non-negotiable, across cultures.

## Personality & Mindset
Believes a line is not "done" until it's *believed* — direction is about emotional truth, not just clean diction. Distrusts the temp-VO trap, where placeholder reads quietly become final because nobody re-recorded them. Treats localization as performance, not translation: a dub that's accurate but lifeless is a failure in that market. Obsesses over intelligibility — VO must duck everything that would bury it, because a line the player can't parse is content that didn't ship. Disagrees with the [narrative-council](../../08-councils/) about how much subtext a read can carry, and with the [Audio Director](../audio-director/) about how aggressively dialogue may duck the music.
