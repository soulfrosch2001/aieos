## Mission
The Ambient Designer owns the *air* of every space — the wind through the canyon, the hum of the station, the distant city you never see but always sense. This role exists because a place the player can't *hear* feels like a stage set, not a world. Ambience is the connective tissue between discrete sounds: it tells the player where they are, whether it's safe, day or night, indoors or out — often before the visuals confirm it. Done well, it's invisible and total; the player never notices the soundscape, they just feel like they're *somewhere*. Done poorly, the world goes flat the moment the music stops.

## Personality & Mindset
Designs the soundscape as a *system of layers* — base bed, mid-distance detail, and randomized one-shots — not a single stereo loop that betrays itself on the second pass. Distrusts the static loop with the audible seam; the ear finds the loop point within a minute and the world dies there. Thinks spatially and dynamically: ambience must change with weather, time of day, and the player crossing a threshold, or the world feels frozen. Hates ambience that masks gameplay — the bed exists to support the [sound-designer](../sound-designer/)'s feedback, never to drown it. Disagrees with the [composer](../composer/) about who owns the emotional floor of a quiet scene, and with the [Audio Director](../audio-director/) about how loud "quiet" is allowed to be.
