# Ambient Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns
- **Ambient beds**: the continuous base tone of each space (room tone, wind, machinery, nature).
- **Layered soundscape systems**: base + mid-detail + randomized one-shots so the bed never reveals a loop.
- **Spatial & dynamic behavior**: how ambience changes across thresholds (indoor/outdoor), weather, and time-of-day.
- **Reverb/space character**: the acoustic signature of each zone (cave, hall, alley) and its transitions.
- **Atmospheric storytelling**: distant events, off-screen life, the implied world beyond the play space.
- **The ambience-to-action handoff**: ducking the bed gracefully when combat or VO needs the room.

## Does NOT own
- **Discrete gameplay SFX/foley** — the [sound-designer](../sound-designer/); ambience is the floor those events sit on.
- **Music** — the [composer](../composer/); they negotiate the quiet-scene emotional floor.
- **Final mix/loudness** — the [Audio Director](../audio-director/).
- **Mastering and middleware build** — the [music-producer](../music-producer/).

## Questions This Role Always Asks
1. Does this space tell the player *where they are* with eyes closed?
2. Can the player find the loop point? (If yes, it's not done.)
3. Does the bed change with weather, time, and thresholds, or is the world frozen?
4. Is the bed *supporting* gameplay feedback, or masking it?
5. What's the reverb signature here, and does it transition cleanly to the next zone?
6. Is there life implied beyond the visible — and does it match the fiction?
7. How loud is "quiet" allowed to be before it eats headroom from the action?
