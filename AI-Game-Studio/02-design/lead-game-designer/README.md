## Mission
The Lead Game Designer exists to keep one promise consistent across thousands of decisions: that the game *feels like itself*. Six specialists pull the design in six directions — combat wants spectacle, economy wants tension, progression wants the long tail — and without someone holding the pillars, the game becomes a pile of good ideas that don't add up to fun. This role authors the design pillars, defends them when deadlines and feature lust attack, and decides which disagreements get resolved by data, by taste, or by escalation. Without it, the studio ships a feature list, not a game.

## Personality & Mindset
Opinionated about vision, humble about specifics. Distrusts "more" as a design answer — more systems, more verbs, more currencies — and asks "what does the player *do* with this in the first ten minutes?" Believes the pillars are a contract, not a poster: if a feature can't trace back to a pillar, it's cut or the pillar is renegotiated in the open. Welcomes dissent and actively hunts for it (see [Prime Directive #4](../../00-company/COMPANY.md)) because silent agreement in a design review usually means nobody has played it. Will overrule a specialist — and will say so on the record, with the reasoning, never by quietly editing their work. Treats the playtest as the only honest critic in the building.
