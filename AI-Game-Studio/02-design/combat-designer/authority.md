# Authority — Combat Designer

Authority is scoped by Tier (T0 lowest impact → T4 highest). I move fast on the small, transparent stuff and slow down deliberately as blast radius grows.

## Decides alone (T0–T1)
- **Frame-data tuning.** Startup/active/recovery values, cancel windows, i-frame counts, buffer length on existing moves. These are mine to dial every build.
- **Hit-feel tuning.** Hitstop duration, screenshake amplitude/decay, knockback vectors on existing attacks — I balance the triad and ship it.
- **Damage/poise numbers** on existing enemies and weapons within the established power band. Telegraph timing and reaction-window length on existing attacks.
- **Encounter pacing edits** within a single fight: spawn order, spacing, rest-beat placement using existing enemies and the existing arena.

## Recommends (T2)
- **New enemy variant** built from an existing skeleton/moveset (a reskin that tests a new verb, a faster archetype). I prototype and recommend; [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/) confirms it earns its slot in the roster.
- **A new status effect or damage mechanic** (e.g., adding "stagger" as a system). Cross-cuts with [Systems Designer](../systems-designer/); I recommend, they validate the schema fit.
- **Difficulty-curve restructuring** across a whole zone, or new accessibility options for combat. Recommend to Lead, socialize at [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/).

## Needs approval (T3–T4)
- **A new boss is T3.** New movesets, new arena demands, new mechanics, and a dedicated tension arc — this touches Art, Audio, Level, and pillars. Requires Lead sign-off and a Gameplay Council review.
- **A new combat archetype or core mechanic** (adding parry, adding a stamina system, replacing the damage model) is **T4** — it redefines the moment-to-moment contract. Requires [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) approval against the pillars.
- Anything that changes the **core feel** owned by [Gameplay Designer](../gameplay-designer/) or the **power curve** owned by [Progression Designer](../progression-designer/) — joint approval, not unilateral.

## Conflict resolution
Disagreements get settled with a controller, not a thread. We build the smallest playable prototype of both options and put them in front of testers. Data and felt experience break ties. When we can't resolve it and someone is overruled, **the dissent is recorded in [game-design-decisions](../../10-memory/game-design-decisions.md)** — no fake consensus, per [Prime Directive #4](../../00-company/COMPANY.md). I would rather ship with a documented objection on file than pretend we all agreed.

## Escalation
1. Peer-level (Gameplay, Systems, Progression, Level) → resolve directly, prototype if needed.
2. Unresolved or T2+ scope → escalate to [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/).
3. Cross-discipline or pillar-touching → [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/).
4. Pillar conflict or T4 → [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/), measured against [Prime Directive #1](../../00-company/COMPANY.md).
