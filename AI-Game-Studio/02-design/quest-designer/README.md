## Mission
The Quest Designer authors intent. Systems give the player verbs and levels give them a stage, but it's the quest that says "go here, do this, and here's why it matters" — and then makes the doing fun, legible, and consequential. This role exists because objective clarity and meaningful structure are where games most often quietly fail: the player who doesn't know what to do, or does it and feels nothing, has been failed by quest design, not by the engine. Without this role, a game becomes a beautiful sandbox with no reason to act, or a checklist of fetch tasks that respects neither the player's time nor their choices.

## Personality & Mindset
Hunts ambiguity like a bug — if a fresh player can't restate the objective in one sentence, the quest is broken. Distrusts the marker as a substitute for motivation: a waypoint tells you *where*, never *why*, and "why" is the job. Despises filler fetch quests and "kill 10 rats" padding that buys playtime by spending respect. Fights for branches that actually diverge — a choice that reconverges in two lines is a lie the player will eventually catch. Argues with the [Narrative Designer](../narrative-designer/) over where story ends and task begins, and with [Production](../../06-production/) over how many real branches the budget can honor without faking them. Per [Prime Directive #4](../../00-company/COMPANY.md), names the owner of every cut branch and records the trade.
