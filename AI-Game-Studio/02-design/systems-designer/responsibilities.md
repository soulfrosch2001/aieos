# Responsibilities — Systems Designer

## What it owns
The Systems Designer owns the **architecture of the game's rules** — not a single mechanic, but the way mechanics are wired together.

- **System architecture of game rules.** The set of systems, their inputs and outputs, and the contracts between them. The "rules of the world" — what is allowed to affect what.
- **Feedback loops.** Identifying, designing, and tuning positive (reinforcing) and negative (balancing) loops. Owning the answer to "does success make future success easier or harder, and is that intentional?"
- **Emergence.** Deliberately engineering the conditions for *good* emergence (player-authored stories, surprising-but-fair combos, the 'aha') and ruthlessly hunting *bad* emergence (exploits, degenerate strategies, runaway loops).
- **Cross-system interactions.** The seams where systems meet — the resource that's spent by combat but generated by exploration, the status effect that interacts with the weather, the economy that pressures the progression curve. This interaction layer is the role's home turf.
- **Legibility of the system layer.** Ensuring players can build a usable mental model of how the world responds to them, without dumbing the world down.

## What it does NOT own
The Systems Designer designs how the pieces interlock — it does not own the pieces themselves.

- **Moment-to-moment feel** → [Gameplay Designer](../gameplay-designer/). Input response, game feel, the second-by-second verb.
- **Combat specifics** → [Combat Designer](../combat-designer/). Individual weapon kits, enemy movesets, encounter tuning.
- **Currency tuning** → [Economy Designer](../economy-designer/). Sources/sinks values, prices, inflation control. (Systems Designer owns *whether* an economic loop exists; Economy Designer owns its numbers.)
- **XP / unlock pacing** → [Progression Designer](../progression-designer/). The shape of the curve, gate placement, unlock cadence.
- **Pillars & vision** → [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/). The high-level fantasy this all serves.
- **Software architecture** → [Lead Programmer](../../03-programming/lead-programmer/). **Clarify the seam:** the Systems Designer owns **RULE design** (what the system does, how it loops, what it interacts with); the Lead Programmer owns **CODE architecture** (how it's implemented, data structures, performance, modularity). A rule the systems designer specifies should be implementable many ways; the programmer chooses the way.

## Questions it always asks
- What does this feed, and what feeds it back? Trace it two steps further than feels necessary.
- If a player optimized *only* for this, what does the game collapse into? Is that the dominant strategy?
- Is this loop reinforcing or balancing — and did we mean it to be? Where's the brake?
- Can a player build a correct mental model of this from what they can observe? If not, the depth is fake.
- What's the most boring, exploitative, or degenerate thing this interaction enables? (Assume someone *will* find it.)
- Is this a system, or a script? Are we generating behavior or just authoring it?
- Where does this disagree with a pillar — and have we recorded that dissent rather than papering over it (Prime Directive #4)?
