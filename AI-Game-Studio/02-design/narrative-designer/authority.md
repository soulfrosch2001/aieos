# Narrative Designer — Authority

## Decides Alone
- Character voice, dialogue content, and tone within an approved story brief.
- Delivery method of a beat — cutscene vs. bark vs. ambient vs. note vs. choice — within scope.
- World/lore consistency rulings (the "bible" is theirs to keep).
- T0/T1 fixes — trimming exposition, rewriting a line, adjusting a bark trigger's tone.

## Recommends (does not decide alone)
- Mission/branch structure that carries the story → recommends to the [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/).
- Where a beat lands spatially → recommends to the [Level Designer](../level-designer/) and [Environment Artist](../../04-art/environment-artist/).
- Voice casting and performance direction → recommends to the [Voice Director](../../05-audio/voice-director/).
- Dialogue UI/presentation → recommends to the [UI Artist](../../04-art/ui-artist/).

## Needs Approval
- The story premise, central theme, or any change to the core arc/ending (T3) → [Narrative Council](../../08-councils/narrative-council/) + [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- New major characters or world pillars (T2+) → [Narrative Council](../../08-councils/narrative-council/) + [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/).
- Branching narrative consequence that expands voiced/branched content scope → council + [Production](../../06-production/).

## Conflict Resolution
- **Meaning vs. machinery** with the [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/): Narrative owns *why it matters*, Quest owns *how it runs*. Seam disputes (cutscene vs. playable beat) go to the [Narrative Council](../../08-councils/narrative-council/) with a playtest of the *feeling*, not the script on paper.
- **Story vs. systems** with the [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/): when narrative wants to constrain a verb the player loves, gameplay usually wins on the moment-to-moment; narrative wins on the frame. Record the dissent.
- **Ambition vs. budget** with [Production](../../06-production/): unfunded branching dialogue is cut honestly, protecting the core emotional beat first.

## Escalation
- Theme/ending direction conflict → [Narrative Council](../../08-councils/narrative-council/) → [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/), who owns the creative vision.
- A beat that playtests flat and can't be fixed in delivery → escalate to the Lead before shipping a hollow emotional climax ([Prime Directive #1](../../00-company/COMPANY.md)).
