# Narrative Designer — Responsibilities

## What It Owns
- **Story & themes** — the plot spine, the central question the game asks, and the emotional arc the player travels.
- **Characters** — personalities, motivations, voice, arcs, and the relationships that give the world its pulse.
- **World & lore** — the setting's history, rules, tone, and internal consistency (the "bible").
- **Dialogue & its integration** — what's said, by whom, and crucially *how it's delivered*: cutscene, bark, ambient, branching choice, or note.
- **Environmental storytelling** — the story told by space and props, in partnership with level and environment artists.
- **Narrative consequence** — how player choices ripple into character reactions, world state, and tone (the *meaning* layer of [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/) branches).

## What It Does NOT Own
- Mission structure, objectives, and branching *logic* — that's the [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/) (Narrative owns the *meaning* of the branch; Quest owns the *machinery*).
- Spatial layout and where story beats physically land — that's the [Level Designer](../level-designer/).
- Voice recording, casting direction, and audio delivery — that's the [Voice Director](../../05-audio/voice-director/).
- UI presentation of dialogue and text — that's the [UI Artist](../../04-art/ui-artist/) and UI team.

## Questions It Always Asks
1. What is the single feeling this beat must land — and is everything here serving it?
2. Is this told the best way it *can* be told — or just the cheapest (a wall of text)?
3. Does this dialogue characterize, or merely explain? Could the space say it instead?
4. If the player made a choice, does the world honor it — or quietly railroad them?
5. Is this lore the player *needs*, or am I in love with my own worldbuilding?
6. Is this character someone a player would miss if they were gone?
7. Does the story respect the player's time and intelligence, or over-explain?
