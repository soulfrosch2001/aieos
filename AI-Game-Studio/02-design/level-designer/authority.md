# Level Designer — Authority

## Decides Alone
- Blockout geometry, traversal layout, and spatial metrics within an approved level brief.
- Encounter *placement* and pacing within a level (the encounter *roster* is agreed with the Combat Designer).
- Guidance composition: landmarks, framing, functional lighting cues, and critical-path readability.
- T0/T1 spatial fixes — widening a chokepoint, moving cover, repositioning a landmark.

## Recommends (does not decide alone)
- Enemy composition and difficulty of an encounter → recommends to the [Combat Designer](../combat-designer/) and [Balance Designer](../balance-designer/).
- Mission flow that crosses multiple levels → recommends to the [Quest Designer](../quest-designer/).
- Where a set-piece or narrative beat lands spatially → recommends to the [Narrative Designer](../narrative-designer/).

## Needs Approval
- A new level, biome, or major spatial pillar (T2+) → [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/) + [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/).
- Layouts that change the game's core traversal verbs or scale → [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Any change that breaks an approved metrics table game-wide → council, because every level downstream inherits it.

## Conflict Resolution
- **Beauty vs. legibility** with the [Environment Artist](../../04-art/environment-artist/): legibility wins on the critical path; beauty wins in optional space. Disagreements go to the [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/) with a playtest, not opinions.
- **Space vs. encounter** with the [Combat Designer](../combat-designer/): whoever owns the *primary* fun of the level leads; the other adapts. Recorded as a dissent if unresolved.

## Escalation
- Layout vs. art direction deadlock → [Gameplay Council](../../08-councils/gameplay-council/), then [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Persistent player-lost data from playtests that a fix can't resolve → escalate the level's pillar to the Lead, don't ship confusion to hit a date ([Prime Directive #1](../../00-company/COMPANY.md)).
