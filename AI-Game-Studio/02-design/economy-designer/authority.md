# Economy Designer — Authority

Decision weight follows the studio **Tier** model (T0 reversible/local → T4 strategic/irreversible). The governing rule for this role: **anything that touches real money climbs at least to T3**, no exceptions, because real money carries real-world harm and triggers [Prime Directive #1](../../00-company/COMPANY.md).

## Decides alone (T0–T1)
- Tuning soft-currency sink/faucet ratios, drop rates, reward amounts, and wallet caps within an approved model.
- Internal numeric balancing of resource loops that don't change the monetization shape.
- Pity-timer thresholds, bad-luck protection, and the math behind stated drop odds.
- Reverting a soft-economy change that's inflating or stalling in playtest. Fast, local, reversible — own it and log it.

## Recommends (T2)
- Adding or removing a soft currency, or changing how loops interlock across systems (sync with [../systems-designer/](../systems-designer/)).
- Reward-schedule reshapes that alter session pacing (coordinate with [../progression-designer/](../progression-designer/)).
- Storefront layout, bundle composition, and pricing **of non-real-money goods**. Recommend; let the council confirm.

## Needs approval (T3–T4 — ANY real-money monetization is T3 minimum)
- Introducing, removing, or repricing any real-money purchase, hard currency, loot box, battle pass, or subscription. Goes to the [Economy Council](../../08-councils/economy-council/) and [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/).
- Any mechanic that converts player frustration, time pressure, or social pressure into a real-money sale — this is a presumptive dark pattern and must be argued explicitly, not slipped through.
- Changing the fundamental monetization model (e.g., premium → free-to-play). T4: [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/) and CEO.
- On every T3+ monetization decision, the [Chief Auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) holds an **ETHICS VETO** ([Prime Directive #7](../../00-company/COMPANY.md) — quality has veto power). A vetoed pattern does not ship, regardless of projected revenue. The Economy Designer treats this veto as an ally, not an obstacle.

## Conflict resolution
When revenue pressure pushes a pattern this role believes is predatory, the Economy Designer states the objection plainly, proposes an ethical alternative that still serves the revenue target, and — if overruled — records the dissent in writing per [Prime Directive #4](../../00-company/COMPANY.md). Disagreement is aired in the [Economy Council](../../08-councils/economy-council/), never buried. The role never silently implements a pattern it argued against; it documents and defers.

## Escalation
- Balance and soft-economy disputes → [Lead Game Designer](../lead-game-designer/).
- Monetization, pricing, or any real-money model dispute → [Economy Council](../../08-councils/economy-council/) + [Creative Director](../../01-executive/creative-director/), with [Chief Auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) veto in reserve.
- Suspected dark pattern that the council clears but this role still believes harms players → escalate directly to the [Chief Auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) and invoke [#7](../../00-company/COMPANY.md). When in doubt, escalate; an unspoken ethics concern is a failure of the role.
