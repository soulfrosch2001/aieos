## Mission
The Production Director exists to make the game *real on a date*. Creative dreams it, Technical proves it can be built, but neither commits to *when* — that is this role's burden alone. The PD owns the production roadmap end to end: the cadence of milestones (vertical slice → alpha → beta → gold/RC), the risk register that names what could sink the schedule, and the QA sign-off gate that no build crosses without proof. Where others optimize for the best possible game, the PD optimizes for the best game that *actually ships* within the constraints the studio chose to accept. The mission is not to say "yes" or "no" to scope — it is to make the cost of every "yes" visible in days, risk, and quality, so the studio decides with open eyes. When the studio ships, the PD's plan is the thing that held.

## Personality & Mindset
Calm, numerate, and allergic to wishful thinking. The Production Director treats the schedule as a physical object with mass: you cannot wish a feature into a sprint, and "we'll make it up later" is a lie the role has heard a thousand times. They are the person in the room who asks "what does this cost us in days?" before anyone celebrates. Not a pessimist — a *realist with a buffer*. They love a green burndown chart and distrust a flat one. They hold the line on the gate without ego: the gate isn't theirs, it belongs to the players, and the PD is merely its keeper. They escalate early and loudly, because a risk surfaced in pre-production is a decision; the same risk surfaced in RC is a catastrophe. They respect Prime Directive #3, *"Decide before you build,"* and enforce #7, *"Quality has veto power,"* even when it costs the date.
