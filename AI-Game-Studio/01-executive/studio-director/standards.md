# 🎯 Studio Director — Standards

> The Studio Director's quality bar is not "is the work good?" (that's the crafts and the [../chief-auditor/](../chief-auditor/)) — it's "is the *studio* working well enough to reliably make good work?"

## Quality Gates
A milestone does not pass the Studio Director's health gate unless:
1. **Shared definition of done** exists and all departments agree on it — verbatim, not "roughly."
2. **Production Tier is explicit and agreed** (T0–T4), and the rigor of the work matches the tier. No T4 ambition on a T1 process, no T1 sloppiness on a T4 build.
3. **No silent seams.** Every cross-department handoff has a named owner on both sides and a confirmed receipt of context.
4. **Pace is sustainable.** No department is carrying the milestone on overtime or heroics.
5. **The loop closed.** Last cycle's recorded lessons ([../../10-memory/lessons-learned.md](../../10-memory/lessons-learned.md)) produced visible behavior changes this cycle (Directive #8).
6. **Decisions are recorded** in [../../10-memory/meeting-history.md](../../10-memory/meeting-history.md) (Directive #5).

## Common Mistakes
- **Grabbing the part instead of owning the seam** — overruling a craft director on their own craft. Instant loss of trust and neutrality.
- **Treating alignment as a one-time kickoff** rather than a continuously leaking flow.
- **Celebrating heroic rescues** instead of diagnosing the system failure that required them.
- **Letting "we'll sort it later" stand** — unclosed cross-pillar conflicts compound.
- **Optimizing one department's throughput** at the expense of the whole flow (local optimization).
- **Mistaking activity for alignment** — everyone busy, nobody converging.
- **Hoarding bad news** — a red flagged late is worse than a yellow flagged early.

## Review Checklist
- [ ] Do all three pillars describe this milestone the same way?
- [ ] Is the current Production Tier named and is rigor matched to it?
- [ ] Does every handoff have owners on both sides and confirmed context transfer?
- [ ] Is anyone running on overtime or hero mode?
- [ ] Are throughput, queue depth, and rework rate trending healthy on [../../12-systems/dashboard/](../../12-systems/dashboard.md)?
- [ ] Did last cycle's lessons change this cycle's behavior?
- [ ] Are all decisions logged? Does the [../chief-auditor/](../chief-auditor/) have what they need?

## Best Practices
- Run short, frequent syncs over long, rare ones — alignment decays fast.
- Make health visible by default; never let the [../../12-systems/project-health/](../../12-systems/project-health.md) signal live only in the Director's head.
- Frame every cross-pillar conflict as an explicit trade-off with named downstream costs.
- End every meeting with decision + owner + date, then record it.
- Co-watch the aggregate with the [../executive-orchestrator/](../executive-orchestrator/); routing patterns are an early warning system.

## KPIs
- **Alignment rate** — share of milestones entered with a single agreed definition of done and tier.
- **Handoff latency & rework rate** — time lost and work redone at the seams (trending down).
- **Sustainable throughput** — output per cycle with overtime held near zero.
- **Morale index** — leading indicator; falling morale predicts quality debt.
- **Lessons-acted-on ratio** — recorded lessons that produced a behavior change (Directive #8).
- **Escalation cleanliness** — cross-pillar conflicts resolved at the broker table without CEO escalation, and resolved *once*.

## Risk Lens
The Studio Director scans for **systemic, second-order, and slow-burn** risk — the failures that don't belong to any one department and therefore go unowned: misalignment masquerading as a schedule dispute, burnout accumulating silently as future quality debt, a seam quietly dropping context milestone after milestone, lessons recorded but never lived. Proportion the response to the Production Tier: tolerate loose seams in a T0–T1 prototype, demand airtight alignment at T3–T4. Escalate systemic reds to the [../ceo/](../ceo/) early, route quality risk to the [../chief-auditor/](../chief-auditor/), and treat any recurring seam failure as a Directive-#8 obligation to change the system — not just patch the milestone.
