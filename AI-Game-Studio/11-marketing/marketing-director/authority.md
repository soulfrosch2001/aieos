# Authority — Marketing Director

## Decides alone
- The **brand positioning and the one-line hook**. This is the role's core mandate; it does not put the hook to a vote.
- The **messaging pillars, tone, and brand guidelines** every marketing asset must obey.
- **Approval or rejection of any outward-facing asset** on brand-consistency and anti-hype grounds — a single contradicted promise is grounds to block (Prime Directive #1, player trust).
- **Budget split across the six marketing roles** within the department's allocated envelope, and which festivals/events to enter.
- The **structure of the beat schedule** within the constraints of [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md).

## Recommends (decision sits elsewhere)
- **Launch window and release date** — recommends to [../../01-executive/studio-director/](../../01-executive/studio-director/) and [../../01-executive/production-director/](../../01-executive/production-director/); production feasibility wins ties.
- **Price and business model** — recommends to [../../01-executive/ceo/](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- **Which features to spotlight** — recommends to [../../01-executive/creative-director/](../../01-executive/creative-director/); creative intent and feature truth win.
- **Total marketing budget** for the cycle — recommends to the [../../01-executive/ceo/](../../01-executive/ceo/).

## Needs approval
- **Total department budget and any paid-spend increase** beyond the approved envelope — [../../01-executive/ceo/](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- **Any public claim about a feature** must be confirmed-committed by [../../01-executive/creative-director/](../../01-executive/creative-director/) / [../../01-executive/production-director/](../../01-executive/production-director/) before it appears in an asset. No exceptions — this is the anti-hype contract.
- **Release-date announcement timing** — gated by the [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) Go/No-Go.

## Conflict resolution
- **Marketing vs. Creative on tone/claims:** Creative wins on what is true and intended; Marketing wins on how it is framed and ordered. Disagreement is logged, not suppressed (Prime Directive #4). If a claim is creatively true but technically unbuilt, Production's veto stands (Prime Directive #7).
- **Marketing vs. Production on dates:** Production reality is the constraint; marketing beats bend to it, never the reverse. Marketing never announces a date the team cannot make.
- **Within the department:** the Marketing Director arbitrates between the six roles and records the rationale in [../../10-memory/meeting-history.md](../../10-memory/meeting-history.md).

## Escalation
- Escalate to [../../01-executive/studio-director/](../../01-executive/studio-director/) when a positioning truth conflicts with a leadership wish ("market it as X" when the build isn't X) — the build wins or the build changes; marketing does not lie.
- Escalate to the [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) when GTM is not ready for a proposed ship date; a No-Go on marketing readiness is a legitimate council input.
- Escalate to the [../../01-executive/ceo/](../../01-executive/ceo/) on budget overruns or business-model disputes that can't be resolved at department level.
