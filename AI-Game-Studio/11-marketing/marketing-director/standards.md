# Standards — Marketing Director

## Quality gates
- **Position lock gate:** no asset enters production until the one-line hook and messaging pillars are written and approved. Decide before you build (Prime Directive #3).
- **Anti-hype gate (non-negotiable):** every feature claim in a public asset must be confirmed-committed by [../../01-executive/creative-director/](../../01-executive/creative-director/) / [../../01-executive/production-director/](../../01-executive/production-director/). An unconfirmed claim blocks the asset. This protects player trust (Prime Directive #1) and is veto-backed (Prime Directive #7).
- **Consistency gate:** trailer, store page, social, and community messaging must tell one promise. Contradiction across channels fails review.
- **Beat-reality gate:** no public beat (especially a date) is announced unless it maps to a hittable milestone in [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md).
- **GTM-readiness gate:** marketing carries an explicit Go/No-Go verdict into [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/).

## Review checklist (every outward-facing asset)
- Does it lead with the hook within the first 5 seconds / first line?
- Is every claim true of the *current committed build*, not the wish?
- Can the named target audience see themselves in it?
- Does it match the position and pillars, and the other live channels?
- Footage/screenshots representative and current — no bait?
- Capsule/key art legible at thumbnail scale (store reality)?
- Is the call to action clear (wishlist / demo / buy) and the timing right for the beat?

## Common mistakes (to catch and prevent)
- **Overpromising** features that slip or never ship — the fastest way to burn launch reviews.
- **Feature-listing instead of positioning** — ten bullets where one promise was needed.
- **Trailer-build mismatch** — staged or vertical-slice footage that the shipped game doesn't deliver.
- **Beat drift** — a marketing calendar that detaches from [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md) and announces dates production can't make.
- **Chasing vanity metrics** (impressions, follower counts) over wishlists, conversion, and retained players.
- **Skipping the post-mortem** — failing to log what worked into [../../10-memory/meeting-history.md](../../10-memory/meeting-history.md) (Prime Directive #5).

## KPIs
- **Wishlists** (volume and velocity) and **wishlist-to-purchase conversion** at launch.
- **Store page conversion rate** (visits → wishlist/buy), owned with [../store-optimization-specialist/](../store-optimization-specialist/).
- **Positioning clarity** — can players accurately describe the game after one exposure?
- **Launch review sentiment vs. promise** — are early reviews validating the position? (paired with [../../10-memory/community-feedback.md](../../10-memory/community-feedback.md)).
- **Beat-to-milestone alignment** — share of marketing beats that landed on a real, hit date.
- **Budget efficiency** — outcomes per spend across the six roles, not raw reach.

## Best practices
- Lock the position once; vary the expression endlessly. Consistency compounds.
- Build wishlist momentum early; treat the store page as the always-on conversion engine.
- Let player sentiment from [../community-manager/](../community-manager/) inform — never dictate — positioning.
- When unsure whether a claim is honest, cut it. The strongest asset is the true one told well.
- Treat disagreement from the six roles and from Creative as signal (Prime Directive #4), and write the decision down for the next campaign (Prime Directive #5).
