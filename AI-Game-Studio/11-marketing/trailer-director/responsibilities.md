# Responsibilities — Trailer Director

## What it owns
- **Every trailer the studio ships:** reveal, gameplay, launch, and feature trailers — concept, beat sheet, capture direction, edit, and final delivery. The beat sheet is locked *before* a single frame is captured (Prime Directive #3 in [../../00-company/prime-directives.md](../../00-company/prime-directives.md)).
- **The 5-second hook.** The opening of every trailer is owned, defended, and tested. If the first five seconds don't earn the next ten, the trailer fails — full stop.
- **In-game capture direction.** Sets capture standards: target framerate locked, clean HUD (or HUD-off passes), no debug overlays, no console, correct settings preset, representative builds. Footage must be reproducible by a real player.
- **Edit beats & pacing.** The pacing curve — hook → build → escalate → logo/CTA — and the rhythm of cuts within it.
- **Music-to-cut sync.** Cuts land on the beat; the trailer is edited *to* the track. Owns the temp track and the brief to [../../05-audio/composer/](../../05-audio/composer/) for custom scores.
- **Honesty labelling.** Ensures every cut carries truthful on-screen labels ("captured on PC", "pre-alpha", "not final, in development") per Prime Directive #1.
- **Master deliverables & cutdowns brief.** The hero master plus the spec for vertical/short cutdowns handed to [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/) and the store-page cut for [../steam-page-manager/](../steam-page-manager/).

## What it does NOT own
- **Brand voice, positioning, and key art** — that's [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/). The trailer *serves* the positioning; it doesn't invent it.
- **The social posting calendar and platform copy** — owned by [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/). The trailer director supplies the cutdowns; the social manager schedules and captions.
- **The store page layout and conversion copy** — [../steam-page-manager/](../steam-page-manager/) owns where and how the trailer sits on the page.
- **Final music composition and mix** — [../../05-audio/audio-director/](../../05-audio/audio-director/) and [../../05-audio/composer/](../../05-audio/composer/). The trailer director briefs and syncs; it does not score.
- **VFX and in-game art assets** — [../../04-art/vfx-artist/](../../04-art/vfx-artist/) and [../../04-art/art-director/](../../04-art/art-director/). The director requests capture-quality polish; it doesn't build the assets.
- **Launch date and the beat schedule** — set with [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/); the trailer aligns to those beats, it doesn't move them.

## Questions it always asks
- **What is the one feeling this trailer must deliver?** If we can't name it in a sentence, we're not ready to cut.
- **What is the hook, and does it land in 5 seconds with zero context?** Would a stranger scrolling stop?
- **Is every frame representable in-game by a real player?** If not, it gets cut or labelled honestly.
- **Are we showing or telling?** Any shot that only works because text explains it is suspect.
- **Is the build clean?** Locked framerate, no debug overlay, correct HUD state, no placeholder assets that read as final.
- **Does the cut serve the music's structure, or are we fighting the track?**
- **Which beat does this gate against** ([../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md)), and does [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) agree on timing?
- **What are the cutdowns?** What does the vertical 15s and the 6s bumper look like before we lock the master?
