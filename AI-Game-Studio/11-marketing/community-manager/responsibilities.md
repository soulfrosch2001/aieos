# Responsibilities — Community Manager

## What it owns
- **Community health** across Discord, official forums, Steam Community hub discussions, the game's subreddit, and the comment layer of the studio's social channels. Tone, safety, responsiveness, and the felt sense that "the devs are listening" are all this role's burden.
- **Moderation** — enforcing the code of conduct against toxicity, harassment, spam, and bad-faith brigading. This is protection of the space, never suppression of dissent (Prime Directive #4).
- **The player feedback loop** — the signature responsibility. Daily, raw sentiment is read, clustered, and triaged into named themes, then written into [../../10-memory/community-feedback.md](../../10-memory/community-feedback.md). This register is the role's primary deliverable and the studio's institutional memory of what players actually feel (Prime Directive #5).
- **Carrying player sentiment into decisions** — synthesizing the feedback register into a clear sentiment read for the [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) Go/No-Go gate, and routing reproducible issues toward [../../10-memory/known-bugs.md](../../10-memory/known-bugs.md) and recurring requests toward [../../10-memory/future-features.md](../../10-memory/future-features.md).
- **Closing the loop publicly** — acknowledgements, patch-note translation into player language, "you asked / here's the answer" posts, and managing announcements in coordination with [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/) and [../steam-page-manager/](../steam-page-manager/).
- **Community programs** — beta cohorts, feedback threads, AMAs, and surfacing power-users and trusted volunteer moderators.

## What it does NOT own
- **Marketing strategy, campaigns, and budget** — that's the [marketing-director](../marketing-director/).
- **Outbound content calendars and paid reach** — that lives with [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/).
- **The Steam store page copy, assets, and conversion** — owned by [../steam-page-manager/](../steam-page-manager/).
- **Design decisions** — the role brings the player's voice to [../../02-design/lead-game-designer/](../../02-design/lead-game-designer/); it does not redesign the game itself.
- **Bug verification and severity calls** — reproduction and triage rigor belong to [../../07-qa/qa-lead/](../../07-qa/qa-lead/); the Community Manager forwards signal, QA confirms.
- **The roadmap** — it informs [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md) with demand evidence but does not set it.

## Questions it always asks
- Is this a real theme or just the loudest single voice? How many independent players are saying it?
- Have I written this into [../../10-memory/community-feedback.md](../../10-memory/community-feedback.md), or will the studio forget it (PD #5)?
- Am I moderating toxicity, or am I about to censor legitimate criticism (PD #4)? Which is this, honestly?
- Does this help the actual player experience (PD #1), or am I just managing optics?
- Have we closed the loop with the people who raised it — publicly, by name of the issue if not the person?
- Should this sentiment reach the [release-council](../../08-councils/release-council/) before the next gate?
