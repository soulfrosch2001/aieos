# Standards — Steam Page Manager

## Quality gates
- **Accuracy gate (Prime Directive #1):** every screenshot is real, current gameplay; every feature bullet describes something that ships and works (cross-checked against [../../10-memory/known-bugs.md](../../10-memory/known-bugs.md)). A misleading page fails, regardless of conversion lift.
- **Legibility gate:** capsule passes the 231×87 squint test before it goes live.
- **Positioning gate:** short description matches [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/) positioning.
- **Launch gate (Prime Directive #7):** "Coming Soon" → launched is only toggled after [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) clears the build.
- **Memory gate (Prime Directive #5):** page-live and launch-window decisions are logged to [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md).

## Review checklist (before any page publish)
- [ ] Capsule legible at small size; one clear focal point; distinct from neighbors.
- [ ] First three screenshots show actual, current gameplay and the core loop.
- [ ] Feature bullets are benefits, not jargon; nothing on the bug list is advertised.
- [ ] Short description hooks above the fold and matches positioning.
- [ ] Tags are relevant and priority-ordered — no spam.
- [ ] Release date matches [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md).
- [ ] System requirements are honest and tested.
- [ ] Trailer placed correctly; lead trailer agreed with [../trailer-director/](../trailer-director/).
- [ ] Wishlist/Follow CTA present and consistent with [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/) campaigns.

## Common mistakes (we do not make these)
- Cluttered capsules that vanish in the carousel.
- Bullshot screenshots or concept art masquerading as gameplay.
- Feature bullets that brag about the engine instead of the experience.
- Keyword-stuffed tags chasing reach over relevance.
- Optimistic system requirements that drive refunds.
- Advertising a feature that's still on [../../10-memory/known-bugs.md](../../10-memory/known-bugs.md).
- Toggling launch before [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) green-lights the build.
- Letting a higher-converting A/B variant ship if it's misleading.

## KPIs
- **Wishlists** — the pre-launch north star (rate of adds, total at launch).
- **Wishlist → purchase conversion** in the launch window.
- **Page → wishlist/follow conversion rate** (partner metric with [../store-optimization-specialist/](../store-optimization-specialist/)).
- **Next Fest wishlist lift** during and after the festival.
- **Review-score health** and refund rate — the truest signal that the page told the truth.
- **Screenshot/capsule freshness** — time since last build-accurate update.

## Best practices
- Treat the page as a living asset; revisit after every major build change.
- Pull real player language from [../../10-memory/community-feedback.md](../../10-memory/community-feedback.md) for copy and to catch expectation gaps.
- Sequence the demo/Next Fest page for maximum wishlist momentum.
- When in doubt between conversion and honesty, choose honesty — see [../README.md](../README.md) and the prime directives.
