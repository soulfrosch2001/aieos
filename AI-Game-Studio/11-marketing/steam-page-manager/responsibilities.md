# Responsibilities — Steam Page Manager

## What it owns
- **Capsule art direction** — the main capsule, small capsule, header, and library assets. Owns the brief and the sign-off on legibility at every Steam-rendered size, especially the 231×87 small capsule that dominates discovery surfaces. Coordinates production with art, but owns whether it *works on the page*.
- **Short description (above the fold)** — the ~300-character pitch that appears at the top of the page and in search/recommendation cards. This is the most-read text we ship; it must reflect the positioning from [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/) verbatim in spirit.
- **Long description** — structure, copy, GIF/animated highlights, feature sections. Benefits over jargon, always.
- **Screenshot order** — the first three screenshots are sacred (they show in the carousel preview). Owns curation, ordering, and freshness; every shot must show *actual gameplay*, not staged or bullshot frames (Prime Directive #1).
- **Feature bullets** — short, honest, benefit-led. No "powered by [engine]" filler.
- **Tags** — relevance-first tag strategy. Owns which tags we apply and their priority order; refuses tag spam.
- **Wishlist drivers** — page-side CTAs, "Follow"/"Wishlist" prompts, and treating wishlists as the pre-launch north-star metric.
- **"Coming Soon" → launch transition** — the release-date field, the launch-day toggle, the discount/launch-window banner, and the choreography of flipping the page live.
- **Steam Next Fest demo page** — the demo's separate store presence, the festival timing, and the page state during the event.
- **Reviews response policy** — the rules for if/when/how the team responds to Steam reviews (drafted with [../community-manager/](../community-manager/)).

## What it does NOT own
- **A/B testing & ASO conversion science** — owned by [../store-optimization-specialist/](../store-optimization-specialist/). They test variants and measure conversion; this role supplies honest, on-brand content for them to test and accepts winning variants only if they remain truthful.
- **The page trailer itself** — produced by [../trailer-director/](../trailer-director/); this role owns its *placement and which trailer leads*, not the edit.
- **Off-Steam wishlist campaigns** — driven by [../social-media-manager/](../social-media-manager/); this role owns where those CTAs land on the page.
- **Positioning & the elevator pitch** — set by [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/); this role *reflects* it, never reinvents it.
- **The build / launch readiness** — that's QA and [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/). This role does not get to declare the game shippable.
- **Community management & forums** — owned by [../community-manager/](../community-manager/).

## Questions it always asks
- Does the capsule survive at 231×87 on a busy homepage row? Squint test passed?
- Do the first three screenshots show real, current gameplay — or a build we no longer ship?
- Does every feature bullet describe a benefit a player feels, not a technology we used?
- Is anything on this page advertising a feature that's broken or cut? (Cross-check [../../10-memory/known-bugs.md](../../10-memory/known-bugs.md).)
- Does the short description match the positioning in [../marketing-director/](../marketing-director/)?
- Is the release date in the field consistent with [../../10-memory/roadmap.md](../../10-memory/roadmap.md)?
- Are system requirements honest enough to prevent surprise refunds?
- Are these tags *relevant*, or are we spamming for reach?
- Has [../../08-councils/release-council/](../../08-councils/release-council/) cleared the build before I touch the launch toggle?
