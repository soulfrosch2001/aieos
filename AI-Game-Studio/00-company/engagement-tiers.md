This studio inherits the kernel's authoritative
[engagement tiers (T0–T4)](../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md), which supersede the
previously-duplicated local text. The Orchestrator sizes by **blast radius**, and when
two tiers are plausible it always picks the higher one — exactly as the kernel defines.

## Local additions (stricter only)
Game-dev examples that bind tier sizing to studio reality (sign-off owners live in
[decision-authority.md](decision-authority.md)):

- **T0 — Trivial:** nudge enemy HP 100→110; fix a tutorial-tooltip typo; swap a placeholder icon.
- **T1 — Standard:** add a sprint ability reusing the existing stamina system; author one ambient track.
- **T2 — Significant:** a new enemy archetype with novel AI; a crafting system; rebalancing in-game currency.
- **T3 — Strategic:** linear→open structure; a signature boss; save-format migration; adding a battle pass.
- **T4 — Crisis:** RC crashes on console cert; a live-economy duplication exploit; save-corruption in a shipped patch.

Higher tiers add councils and gates; they never remove memory updates or the
[improvement scan](continuous-improvement.md).
