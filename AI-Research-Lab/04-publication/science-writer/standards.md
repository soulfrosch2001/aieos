# Science Writer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every claim in the abstract and conclusion traces to a specific result, figure,
  or citation. Unsupported claims are cut or caveated.
- Limitations and threats-to-validity section is present, specific, and honest.
- Every number is consistent across abstract, results, figures, and tables.
- Figures pass the honesty check: labeled axes, stated scales, error bars or
  uncertainty shown, no truncated-axis distortion.
- Disclosures complete: funding, conflicts of interest, data/code availability.
- The [ethics gate](../ethics-officer/README.md) is green before submission.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Overclaim: "proves," "demonstrates," "novel," "significant" used beyond what the
  evidence supports.
- p-value theater: significance asserted without effect size or interval.
- Buried limitations or a missing negative-result discussion.
- Figure distortion (truncated axes, cherry-picked windows, missing error bars).
- Abstract that promises more than the body delivers.
- Quiet edits to a published record instead of an appended correction.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Each claim tagged with its evidence (result / figure / citation / unsupported).
- [ ] Abstract numbers match the body.
- [ ] Limitations section is candid and complete.
- [ ] Figures pass the honesty check.
- [ ] Disclosures and data-availability statements present.
- [ ] Rigor sign-off from the [research-director](../../01-executive/research-director/README.md).
- [ ] Ethics gate green from the [ethics-officer](../ethics-officer/README.md).
- [ ] Minutes appended to [findings](../../memory/registers/findings.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Overclaim defects caught before submission vs. surfaced in external peer review.
- Reviewer requests for clarification per artifact (lower is better).
- Post-publication corrections required (calibration failures).
- Time-to-submission once science is validated.

## Risk lens
- **Credibility risk** — overclaim that damages the lab's reputation when it fails
  to replicate.
- **Misinterpretation risk** — prose or figures that mislead a fair reader.
- **Disclosure risk** — missing conflict, funding, or data-availability statements.
- **Record-integrity risk** — the public record drifting from what was actually done.
