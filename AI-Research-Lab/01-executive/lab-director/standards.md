# Lab Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every active program has a written question, a conviction level, and an explicit
  kill condition.
- The agenda traces to end-user value (Directive #1); no program survives on novelty
  alone.
- T2+ direction is debated in a council before construction (Directive #3).
- The portfolio fits resourcing reality (signed off with the Operations Lead) — no
  agenda that the lab cannot staff.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Sunk-cost programs that should have been killed two cycles ago.
- Over-diversification — too many small bets, none with enough conviction to win.
- Overriding the Research Director's rigor veto to hit a deadline.
- Drifting from direction into method (Directive #2) and routing the science itself.
- Agenda whiplash — repivoting on every fresh result instead of holding conviction.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Each program has a stated question, conviction, and kill condition.
- [ ] No program violates a rigor or ethics veto.
- [ ] The agenda is feasible under current resourcing (Ops Lead concurs).
- [ ] T2+ items went through a council before any build (Directive #3).
- [ ] open-questions register is current this cycle.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Share of programs that produced a consequential, used finding.
- Kill discipline: programs retired on time vs. carried past their kill condition.
- Conviction accuracy: how often high-conviction bets paid off.
- Agenda stability: pivots driven by evidence vs. by noise.

## Risk lens
- **Portfolio risk** — over-hedged or over-concentrated bets.
- **Relevance risk** — asking questions no end user needs answered.
- **Authority-overreach risk** — straying into method or routing (Directives #2, #6).
- **Conviction risk** — holding a dead program, or abandoning a live one too soon.
