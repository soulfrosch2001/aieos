# Research Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The method is sound: controls present, confounds addressed, design pre-registered
  where applicable.
- The result is reproducible from what is written — a competent stranger could repeat it.
- Statistical claims carry honest error bars and stated assumptions.
- T2+ work passed the [review-council](../../councils/review-council/) before
  construction (Directive #3).
- No unsound or irreproducible work proceeds (the scientific-rigor veto).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Post-hoc storytelling — narrating the data after the fact as if it were a hypothesis.
- p-hacking and silent multiple comparisons.
- Single-shot results presented as established without replication.
- Missing or weak controls; effects buried inside the noise.
- Letting rigor become a backdoor into setting the agenda (that is the Lab Director's).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Was the design pre-registered or is this post-hoc?
- [ ] Is there an adequate control and a clear falsification condition?
- [ ] Has the key result been (or can it be) reproduced?
- [ ] Are statistical assumptions stated and error bars honest?
- [ ] Did T2+ work clear the review-council before any build?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Reproducibility rate of the lab's published findings.
- Survival rate of findings under external peer review.
- Veto precision: vetoes that caught real defects vs. false alarms.
- Methodological floor: standards trend upward, never down.

## Risk lens
- **Validity risk** — unsound method producing a confident-looking false result.
- **Reproducibility risk** — results that cannot be repeated.
- **Overreach risk** — rigor used to steer the agenda (Directives #2, #6).
- **Veto-fatigue risk** — vetoing so often the veto loses force, or so rarely it
  loses meaning.
