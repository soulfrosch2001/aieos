# Balance Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The balancing methodology: how the studio models economies, curves, encounters, and metas, and what counts as evidence.
- Coherence across departments — that an [02-economy](../../02-economy/) change does not silently break a [03-progression](../../03-progression/) curve or a [04-competitive](../../04-competitive/) meta.
- The standard of proof a change must meet before it can ship.
- The methodology register and its evolution ([balance-decisions](../../memory/registers/balance-decisions.md)).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Which games the studio takes on — that is the [ceo](../ceo/).
- When committed work ships — that is the [operations-director](../operations-director/).
- Conformance to process and gates — that is the [chief-auditor](../chief-auditor/).
- The act of tuning individual values — that belongs to the department balancers; the director sets the method, not every number.

## Questions it always asks
- What model produced this number, and what assumption is it resting on?
- Where is the playtest or telemetry signal that this change is real, not imagined?
- Does this fix stay coherent with economy, progression, and competitive at once?
- Could we reproduce this balance pass from the recorded inputs alone?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the methodology evidence-first and reproducible as the studio takes on new genres.
- Maintain coherence so cross-department changes compose instead of colliding.
- Evolve the method deliberately — propose framework-level changes before adopting them (Directive #6, Directive #7), recorded in [balance-decisions](../../memory/registers/balance-decisions.md).
