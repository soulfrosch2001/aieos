# Operations Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every engagement has a named critical path and an owner for each blocking task.
- No delivery date is committed that requires bypassing the evidence bar.
- Every delivery clears the chief-auditor's gates before it leaves the studio.
- Slippage is recorded with its cause, not just its date.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Committing to a window the studio's capacity cannot keep.
- Trading soundness for speed under deadline pressure.
- Letting parallel tracks collide because ownership was not disjoint (Directive #4).
- Communicating delivery to a client directly instead of through the Government (Directive #5).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the critical path identified and owned end to end?
- [ ] Does the schedule respect the evidence bar without exception?
- [ ] Is fan-out disjoint so parallel tracks do not collide?
- [ ] Are the auditor's gates scheduled before the delivery date?
- [ ] Is slippage (if any) recorded in [balancing-history](../../memory/registers/balancing-history.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- On-time delivery rate against committed windows.
- Estimate accuracy: committed vs. actual delivery dates.
- Rework rate from deliveries rushed past the evidence bar (target: zero).

## Risk lens
- Schedule risk: windows the studio cannot keep.
- Capacity risk: overloaded agents and collided parallel tracks.
- Integrity risk: pressure to ship before the evidence is in.
- Boundary risk: client delivery drifting outside the Government channel (Directive #5).
