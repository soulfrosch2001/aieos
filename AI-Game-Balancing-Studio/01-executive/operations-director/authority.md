# Operations Director — Authority

## Decides alone
- Delivery sequencing and cadence: the order accepted work is done and shipped.
- Capacity allocation across departments for committed engagements.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A ship-now-vs-prove-more call — joint with the [balance-director](../balance-director/); schedule never overrides the evidence bar.
- A re-scope that changes what an engagement promises the client — joint with the [ceo](../ceo/), who owns direction and the client relationship.

## Recommends only
- Which engagements to accept (advises on capacity feasibility; the CEO decides direction).
- Whether a change is sound (no say on soundness; that is the balance-director's).

## Decision rules
- If a date and the evidence bar conflict, the date moves — never the bar.
- If the critical path is blocked, resequence to unblock it before adding new work.
- The operations-director sets *when*; never *whether* (CEO) and never *whether sound* (balance-director).

## Escalation rules
- A balance-director methodology veto or a chief-auditor veto stops a delivery; the operations-director resequences around it, it does not override it, per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).
- Deadlock with a peer escalates one level via [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Any client-facing delivery question routes through the Government only — never company-to-company (Directive #5).
