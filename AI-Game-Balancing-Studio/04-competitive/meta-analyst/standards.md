# Meta Analyst — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every dominance claim cites a **win-rate, skill bracket, sample size, and
  window** — no adjective-only verdicts.
- Dominance is reproduced across **at least two brackets** before it is ruled, not
  observed in one.
- A **full solve window** has elapsed (or its absence is explicitly flagged) before
  any nerf is recommended.
- Every competitive change carries a **diagnosis** naming the responsible unit,
  cost, or interaction — not just the symptom.
- The change is backed by **playtest or telemetry evidence** before it reaches the
  [balance-council](../../councils/balance-council/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Nerfing on the loudest complaint instead of the distribution.
- Intervening before the meta has had time to self-correct.
- Moving the unit knob and the pairing knob in the same pass, making the result
  unreadable.
- Nerfing a strong strategy when buffing a dead one would have restored spread.
- Treating one bracket's meta as the whole game's meta.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Win-rate, bracket, sample, and window stated for every claim.
- [ ] Dominance confirmed across multiple brackets.
- [ ] Solve window elapsed or its absence flagged.
- [ ] Root cause named, not just the symptom.
- [ ] Buff-the-dead alternative considered before nerf-the-strong.
- [ ] Only one knob moves, or both moves are jointly signed with the matchmaking-analyst.
- [ ] Evidence attached; entry filed to balancing-history and balance-decisions.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- **Strategy spread** — count of viable strategies per bracket, trending up or flat.
- **Dominance dwell time** — how long a single strategy stays above threshold before
  it is corrected.
- **Dead-option rate** — share of designed strategies nobody runs.
- **Reversion rate** — competitive changes later undone, trending down.

## Risk lens
- **False dominance** — acting on noise or a single bracket.
- **Over-intervention** — patching what the meta would have solved itself.
- **Choice collapse** — nerfs that converge the game on fewer viable strategies.
- **Knob entanglement** — unit and pairing changes confounded in one pass.
- **Balance debt** — known dominance left unfixed for delivery reasons.
