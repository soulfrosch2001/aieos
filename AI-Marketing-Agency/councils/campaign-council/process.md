# Campaign Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the kernel debate norms in the
[discussion protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and inherits the
stdlib [feature-council](../../../councils/feature-council/process.md): structured
debate, strongest counter-argument first, and **dissent is recorded, never
suppressed** (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). The chair
speaks last on contested points so authority does not anchor the room. Attack the
plan, never the person.

## Decision process
1. **Frame** — the chair states the campaign objective and the end user it serves;
   if there is no clear user value, the campaign is sent back, not gated.
2. **Evidence** — the [performance-marketer](../../04-performance/performance-marketer/)
   presents budget, channel mix, target metrics, and proof that conversion
   tracking is verified. No verified measurement → no gate (local strictness).
3. **Compare** — at least two candidate approaches are weighed; the rejected ones
   are named with the reason they lost.
4. **Brand check** — if the campaign touches brand or positioning, it is referred
   to the [brand-council](../brand-council/); the
   [strategy-director](../../01-executive/strategy-director) veto is honored, not
   debated around.
5. **Gate** — the council sets the launch decision, the guardrail thresholds, and
   the **kill criterion** before any spend.
6. **Record** — minutes and dissent are appended per [output.md](output.md).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: [T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
- Must escalate: [T3–T4](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) — council **plus**
  executive sign-off; T4 is government-coordinated. See the
  [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
- Brand/positioning conflict → [brand-council](../brand-council/); the brand veto
  stands.
- Capacity / sequencing conflict the chair cannot resolve → up one level via the
  [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Quality or process violation → the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor)
  veto stops the launch; only a human maintainer overrides it
  ([decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
