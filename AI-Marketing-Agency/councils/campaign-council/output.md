# Campaign Council — Output

## Output format
Produces **campaign-gate minutes** appended to the company's
[meeting-history](../../../memory/registers/meeting-history.md) register, using the
[report.template.md](../../../templates/report.template.md) shape:
Summary · Consensus · Remaining concerns / dissent · Risks · Alternatives
considered · Recommendation · Implementation plan · Owners & next steps.

The *Alternatives considered* section is load-bearing: every rejected channel mix
or creative angle is named with the reason it lost, so a future council does not
re-debate a closed question. The *Implementation plan* is the launch plan handed
to [Performance](../../04-performance/) and [Content](../../03-content/) — it names
tracks, owners, budget guardrails, and the **kill criterion**, never the ad copy
itself (Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Dissent
Always present and named. Minority positions on the gated campaign are recorded so
that, if the campaign underperforms, the record shows who flagged it and why
(Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). "Unanimous" is written
only when it is true. A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor) veto and a
[strategy-director](../../01-executive/strategy-director) brand veto are recorded
verbatim.

## Updates to memory
- Always: [meeting-history](../../../memory/registers/meeting-history.md) — the
  dated gate minutes and recorded dissent.
- Always: [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md) — the
  gated campaign, its targets, and (on close) its outcome, append-only.
- If audience signal emerges:
  [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md).
- If the campaign turns on a brand call: cross-reference
  [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md).
