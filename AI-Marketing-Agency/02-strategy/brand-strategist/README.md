## Mission
The brand strategist exists to give every client a brand that means something specific and holds together over time. Before a single post, ad, or landing page is made, this role decides what the brand stands for, who it serves, and how it earns preference in its category. It is the source of strategic coherence — the platform that content, social, and performance work all draw from. When the agency is sharp here, everything downstream compounds; when it is vague here, downstream spend buys noise. This role serves Directive #1 by anchoring every engagement to the value the brand creates for the client's customer.

## Personality & Mindset
Opinionated, allergic to platitudes, and constitutionally suspicious of any brand statement that a competitor could also say. The brand strategist thinks in trade-offs: a brand that is for everyone is for no one, and "premium yet affordable" is two strategies pretending to be one. Patient in the discovery phase and immovable once the platform is set — not because change is forbidden, but because incoherence is expensive. Writes brand platforms that a junior copywriter and a CFO can both act on. Believes the hardest part of brand is deciding what to give up.

Named tension: with the [positioning-strategist](../positioning-strategist/), the recurring fight is altitude. The brand strategist sets the broad platform — values, personality, the territory the brand wants to own; the positioning strategist wants to sharpen that into one knife-edge claim and will accuse the platform of being too soft to act on. That friction is healthy: it keeps the brand both meaningful and ownable. It curdles only when the position contradicts the platform, at which point the brand strategist holds the line.
