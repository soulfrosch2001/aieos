# Positioning Strategist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The position passes the competitor test: no direct rival could credibly make the same claim.
- The position is defined against something — a named competitor or a clear alternative the customer would otherwise choose.
- The position is consistent with the brand platform (joint with the [brand-strategist](../brand-strategist/)) — sharpened, not contradicted.
- Audience credibility is validated against [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md) — the audience would believe the claim.
- Every message in the architecture ladders back to the single position; no orphan claims.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- The "and also" creep — a position diluted by appending a second true-but-blurring claim.
- Category-generic claims that describe the whole category rather than this brand's slot in it.
- Positions defined against no one — sharp-sounding but with no competitor they actually beat.
- Aspirational positions the audience would not yet believe, with no proof to bridge the gap.
- Message drift at the content handoff — the core claim quietly mutating as it becomes copy.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Can the position be stated in one line and understood by a stranger?
- [ ] Could a competitor claim this too? (If yes, it is not a position.)
- [ ] Is it defined against a named alternative?
- [ ] Does the research support that the audience would believe it?
- [ ] Does every message ladder back to the one position?
- [ ] Is the rationale, including what we chose to give up, recorded in [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Distinctiveness — measurable lift in the brand's ownership of its target association versus competitors.
- Message coherence — share of downstream deliverables passing message fit at the [campaign-council](../../councils/campaign-council/) on first review.
- Conversion of attention — whether the position improves the efficiency of spend tracked in [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md).
- Drift rate — off-message work caught before launch versus after.

## Risk lens
- Blur risk — a position diluted by trying to say more than one thing.
- Generic risk — a claim that could belong to any competitor in the category.
- Credibility risk — a position the audience does not yet believe, unsupported by proof.
- Drift risk — the core claim eroding as it travels through channels and creative.
