# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every accepted client maps to the agency thesis with a one-line rationale.
- Every "yes" names what it costs in capacity, confirmed with the
  [operations-director](../operations-director/).
- Direction decisions of consequence are recorded before they take effect.
- No direction overrides a strategy or audit veto.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Chasing revenue into work that dilutes the brand.
- Saying "yes" to everything and turning the agency generic.
- Confusing a flagship logo with a flagship engagement.
- Setting direction in conversation and never writing it down.
- Routing or building work personally instead of handing it to the orchestrator
  (Directive #2).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this serve the client's end customers? (Directive #1)
- [ ] Does it fit or sharpen the agency thesis?
- [ ] Is the capacity cost confirmed and acceptable?
- [ ] Has the strategy-director raised no brand veto?
- [ ] Is the decision recorded in
      [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Portfolio fit: share of active clients that match the thesis.
- Reputation trend: brand strength and referral rate over time.
- Decline discipline: ratio of declined-but-on-thesis vs. accepted-off-thesis work.
- Direction legibility: how often departments self-direct without escalating.

## Risk lens
- **Dilution risk** — taking work that blurs who the agency is.
- **Overcommitment risk** — direction outpacing delivery capacity.
- **Brand-erosion risk** — short-term revenue at long-term reputation cost.
- **Concentration risk** — over-dependence on a single client or market.
