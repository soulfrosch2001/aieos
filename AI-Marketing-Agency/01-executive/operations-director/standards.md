# Operations Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every commitment has a confirmed capacity cost before it is accepted.
- Every campaign has a sequenced plan with a named critical path before launch.
- T2+ work is planned in council before construction (Directive #3).
- Execution fans out to the tier's ceiling where the work allows (Directive #4).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Silent overcommitment — saying "yes" without confirming capacity.
- Hero-mode delivery that burns slack the agency needs for crises.
- Shipping off-brand work to hit a date (defer to the strategy veto).
- Silently downgrading a request that grew mid-flight.
- Sequential execution where parallel fan-out was possible.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is capacity confirmed and the cost stated?
- [ ] Is the critical path named and the slack identified?
- [ ] Is the tier sized correctly and fan-out matched to it?
- [ ] Does the sequence respect any active strategy veto?
- [ ] Are outcomes recorded in
      [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- On-time delivery rate across campaigns.
- Capacity-promise accuracy (committed vs. delivered).
- Slip recovery: how fast re-sequencing absorbs a disruption.
- Fan-out utilization vs. the tier ceiling.

## Risk lens
- **Overcommitment risk** — promises beyond capacity.
- **Bottleneck risk** — a single dependency stalling the pipeline.
- **Burnout risk** — sustained delivery without slack.
- **Schedule-over-coherence risk** — shipping off-brand to hit a date.
