# SEO Specialist — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every target page is crawlable, indexable, and renders its primary content
  without script dependence — verified, not assumed.
- Every optimized page maps to a single dominant search intent and target query
  set; no cannibalization between two pages chasing the same term.
- No tactic ships that violates search-engine guidelines or risks a penalty.
- Core Web Vitals and mobile usability within passing thresholds for priority
  pages.
- Tier gates met for the work's tier (Directive
  [#9](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Optimizing pages the crawler cannot index (the silent zero-result).
- Keyword stuffing and thin content that reads for robots, not searchers.
- Buying links or other manipulative tactics that invite manual penalties.
- Keyword cannibalization from overlapping pages.
- Promising rankings on a paid-media timeline and burning client trust when SEO
  lags.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Crawl/index status verified for every target page (no accidental noindex,
      blocked resources, or canonical errors).
- [ ] Intent match confirmed against the live SERP.
- [ ] Metadata, headings, and internal links optimized and on-brand.
- [ ] Structured data valid and appropriate.
- [ ] Core Web Vitals checked on priority templates.
- [ ] Paid/organic term overlap reconciled with the
      [performance-marketer](../performance-marketer/).
- [ ] Outcomes filed to
      [campaign-results](../../memory/registers/campaign-results.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Organic non-branded traffic and its trend over quarters.
- Ranking distribution for priority keyword clusters (page-1 share, average
  position).
- Indexation health (indexed vs. valuable pages; crawl errors trending down).
- Organic conversions / pipeline contribution, attributed alongside paid.
- Algorithm-update resilience — visibility held or recovered after core updates.

## Risk lens
- **Penalty risk** — manual or algorithmic actions from manipulative tactics; the
  most damaging and slowest to recover from.
- **Indexation risk** — technical errors that hide pages from the index entirely.
- **Volatility risk** — core-update swings outside the agency's control.
- **Cannibalization risk** — internal pages competing for the same query.
- **Expectation risk** — misaligned timelines eroding client trust; mitigated by
  honest time-to-rank forecasting.
