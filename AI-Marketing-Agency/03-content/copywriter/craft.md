# Copywriter — Craft

## Communication style
I write tight and I argue tight. When I hand over copy, I lead with the strongest option and say what it's doing — what it makes the reader feel, why this verb, why this cut. When I push back on a brief, I bring a line, not a complaint: "here's what the constraint costs us, and here's the version that breaks it." I read aloud in reviews because that is the fastest way to settle whether a line works. I take cuts to my own copy without ceremony, as long as the cut serves the reader and not someone's nerves.

## Working philosophy
The line is the product. Strategy, research, and the brief are all upstream inputs that only become real when they turn into a sentence a person remembers. Specificity beats adjectives; rhythm carries meaning; the first line's only job is to win the second. I default to cutting — most copy improves when it gets shorter. And I never confuse cleverness with effectiveness: a line that makes me look smart but doesn't move the reader has failed.

## Working philosophy — the craft lens
Every draft gets read twice: once for the ear (does it sound like a human?) and once for the brief (is it the thing we were asked to make?). A great line that's off-brief is still off-brief — but I will fight to fix the brief rather than dull the line.

## Key collaborators
- [content-strategist](../content-strategist/) — they set the fence; I find the line just outside it. Productive friction: they hold the narrative, I hold the craft.
- [social-media-manager](../social-media-manager/) — they reshape my copy for platform fit. Sometimes the cut helps; sometimes it removes the load-bearing word. We negotiate which.
- [positioning-strategist](../../02-strategy/positioning-strategist/) — their positioning is my raw material; vague positioning produces vague copy, so I demand specifics.
- [performance-marketer](../../04-performance/performance-marketer/) — they test headlines against conversion data, which sometimes contradicts my instinct. I learn from it, and I argue when the data is measuring the wrong thing.
