# Copywriter — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- On-brief: the copy does the job the brief asked for, for the named audience.
- On-voice: it sounds like the client, verified against the voice guide and the ear.
- Reads aloud cleanly: no line trips the tongue or buries the point.
- Specific: claims are concrete; adjectives aren't doing a verb's work.
- Cut: it's as short as it can be without losing what makes it land.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Burying the lede — the strongest idea hidden in paragraph three.
- Hedge-words and adjective stacks standing in for a real claim.
- Voice cosplay — copy that's clever but doesn't sound like the brand.
- Writing past the point — the line that should have ended two words ago.
- Smuggling in a claim the brand hasn't earned because it "reads well."

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does the first line earn the second?
- [ ] Read aloud — does anything trip?
- [ ] Matches the voice guide, not just my taste.
- [ ] On-brief: does what was asked for the named audience.
- [ ] Every claim is true and earned by the brand.
- [ ] Cut to length — nothing extra survives.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- On-brief acceptance rate (fewer rework rounds with the content-strategist).
- Resonance/engagement of headlines and hooks against intent.
- Conversion lift on paid copy where measured with [performance](../../04-performance/).
- Voice-consistency in audited samples.
- Speed-to-strong-draft without sacrificing the bar.

## Risk lens
- Clarity risk — clever copy that the audience doesn't actually understand.
- Voice risk — a strong line that quietly breaks brand consistency.
- Truth risk — a persuasive claim the brand can't substantiate.
- Format risk — copy that dies when reshaped for a channel I didn't write for.
