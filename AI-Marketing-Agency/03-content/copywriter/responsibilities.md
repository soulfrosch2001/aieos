# Copywriter — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The words: headlines, subheads, body copy, taglines, CTAs, scripts, and long-form prose against a brief.
- The line-level execution of voice — making the abstract voice guide audible in actual sentences.
- The craft quality of copy: rhythm, clarity, specificity, and the cut.
- Multiple distinct options when a piece is high-stakes, so the choice is informed, not forced.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The content plan, calendar, and brief — owned by the [content-strategist](../content-strategist/). I write to the brief; I don't set it.
- Brand voice rules and messaging pillars — owned by [strategy](../../02-strategy/) and ratified via the brand veto.
- Per-platform formatting, posting, and community replies — owned by the [social-media-manager](../social-media-manager/).
- Visual design and art direction — outside the content department's text mandate.
- What ships when — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the one thing this reader must feel or do — and does the first line earn it?
- Whose voice is this, and would they actually say it out loud?
- What can I cut? (Usually a third of it.)
- Is this specific, or am I hiding behind adjectives?
- Am I serving the brief, or my own cleverness?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build a reusable bank of proven lines and patterns per client voice.
- Sharpen and feed back the voice-and-tone guide from real writing — flag where it fails in practice.
- Contribute language patterns that resonated to [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md).
- Raise the department's line-level craft over time through example and critique.
