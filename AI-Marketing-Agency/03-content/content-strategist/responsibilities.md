# Content Strategist — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The content plan and editorial calendar for every client engagement — themes, formats, sequence, and cadence.
- The translation of approved positioning and brand strategy into messaging pillars and content briefs.
- The editorial standard the whole department is held to: voice, tone, structure, and the bar for "publishable."
- Final on-brief sign-off for content produced inside the department before it leaves for client or campaign.
- The health and output quality of the Content department as its lead.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Brand positioning and strategy itself — owned by [strategy](../../02-strategy/) (the [positioning-strategist](../../02-strategy/positioning-strategist/) and [brand-strategist](../../02-strategy/brand-strategist/)). I consume it; I do not set it.
- The literal copy line-by-line — owned by the [copywriter](../copywriter/). I brief and review; I do not rewrite into my own voice.
- Per-platform adaptation, posting, and community management — owned by the [social-media-manager](../social-media-manager/).
- Paid distribution, budgets, and performance optimization — owned by [performance](../../04-performance/).
- Campaign sequencing and what ships when — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- What does the strategy need the audience to believe, and does this plan move them toward it?
- What is the one argument this content arc is making? If I can't say it in a sentence, it isn't a plan yet.
- Is this on-brief and on-voice, or is it merely good?
- What is the sequence — what must the reader see first for the next piece to land?
- What are we choosing NOT to say so the message stays sharp?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a living content plan that stays coherent as positioning evolves.
- Build and maintain the voice-and-tone guide so consistency survives staff and client churn.
- Feed durable messaging rulings into [brand-decisions](../../memory/registers/brand-decisions.md) so they bind future work.
- Watch what actually resonates via [audience-insights](../../memory/registers/audience-insights.md) and fold it back into the plan.
- Mentor the department on editorial judgment so the standard lives in people, not just documents.
