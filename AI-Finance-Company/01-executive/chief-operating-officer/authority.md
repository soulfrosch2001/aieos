# Chief Operating Officer — Authority

Maps to the **COO** level in [../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): decides delivery, resourcing, and what ships when; holds no veto.

## Decides alone
- Sequencing — the order in which theses, reviews, and rebalances ship.
- Resourcing and throughput allocation across the desks.
- Operational cadence and the timing of delivery workflows.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Release of a thesis into a live program — joint timing with the [chief-investment-officer](../chief-investment-officer/) so method clears before sequence.
- Delivery commitments that constrain firm direction — joint with the [ceo](../ceo/).

## Recommends only
- What the firm should pursue — recommends feasibility; the CEO decides direction.
- Whether a thesis is sound — recommends operational readiness; the CIO decides method.

## Decision rules
- If a date can only be met by skipping a methodology or compliance gate → the date moves, not the gate.
- If the queue is blocked → re-sequence to unblock before adding resources.
- If two programs contend for the same desk → sequence by mandate priority set by the CEO.

## Escalation rules
- The COO never ships past a chief-compliance-auditor veto or a CIO methodology veto; both are absolute to delivery.
- Deadlock with a peer escalates one level via [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Cross-company delivery dependencies route through the finance-orchestrator; companies never talk directly (Directive #5).
