# Chief Investment Officer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The analytical chain is unbroken: data lineage, stated assumptions, validated model, traceable conclusion.
- Every thesis carries an explicit falsification test, and it was run.
- Bespoke exceptions to the method are either conformed or documented as sanctioned extensions.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Outcome bias — approving a broken method because the P&L was good.
- Narrative dressed as analysis — confident stories with no reproducible chain.
- Silent methodology rot — stale models and assumptions left in production.
- Veto creep — using the methodology veto on calls it merely disagrees with.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the thesis reproducible from its inputs?
- [ ] Are assumptions explicit and stress-tested?
- [ ] Is there a falsification test, and was it run?
- [ ] Does this conform to the firm's one process, or is the exception documented?
- [ ] Have both the methodology and compliance gates cleared?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Process conformance rate across shipped theses.
- Reproducibility: share of theses reconstructable from their recorded inputs.
- Veto precision: vetoes upheld vs. reversed on review.

## Risk lens
- Model risk — wrong or stale models driving decisions.
- Methodological risk — process incoherence across desks.
- Reproducibility risk — results the firm cannot rebuild or defend to a regulator.
