# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every funded program traces to a stated mandate objective.
- Every direction call is inside the regulatory limit, with the limit named.
- Risk-appetite changes are signed jointly with the CIO before they reach the risk desk.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Mandate drift — chasing opportunities that quietly redefine what the firm is.
- Trespassing on craft — dictating securities or methodology instead of setting direction.
- Treating a compliance veto as a negotiation rather than a boundary.
- Re-deciding direction so often that no desk can plan.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this trace to the mandate?
- [ ] Is it inside the regulatory edge, and is the edge named?
- [ ] Is this my decision (direction) or a desk's (method/selection/sequence)?
- [ ] Does any risk-appetite change carry the CIO's joint sign-off?
- [ ] Has any relevant Auditor veto been honored, not argued around?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Mandate coherence: share of programs traceable to a stated objective.
- Direction stability: rate of mandate reversals per period (lower is better).
- Zero regulatory-limit breaches attributable to direction calls.

## Risk lens
- Strategic risk — wrong mandate for the market.
- Regulatory risk — direction that crowds the legal edge.
- Governance risk — erosion of the separation between direction, method, and conformance.
