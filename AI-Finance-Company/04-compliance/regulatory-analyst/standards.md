# Regulatory Analyst — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every interpretation cites a primary source, its scope, and its effective date — no reading rests on a summary.
- Applicability to this firm is stated explicitly, not assumed.
- Every binding obligation is expressed as a testable control with an owner and a deadline before it is handed off.
- Genuine ambiguity is flagged as ambiguity, with a reasoned range, never resolved by invented certainty.
- The interpretation and its evidence are recorded in the [compliance-log](../../memory/registers/compliance-log.md) (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Acting on a secondhand summary instead of the primary text.
- Assuming a rule from another firm or jurisdiction transfers without checking that it does.
- Handing the [compliance-officer](../compliance-officer/) an interpretation that cannot be turned into a testable control.
- Manufacturing a false binary to look decisive when the law is genuinely conditional.
- Missing an effective date — discovering a rule the week it binds instead of months ahead.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the primary source read, cited, and dated?
- [ ] Is applicability to this firm assessed and stated?
- [ ] Is the obligation expressed as a control the [compliance-officer](../compliance-officer/) can test?
- [ ] Is the effective date on the regulatory-change calendar with the required pre-work?
- [ ] Where it is ambiguous, is the ambiguity stated plainly with reasoning?
- [ ] Has the operational meaning been jointly confirmed with the [compliance-officer](../compliance-officer/)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Lead time on regulatory changes — rules surfaced well before they bind, never after.
- Zero missed effective dates for in-scope obligations.
- Interpretation accuracy — readings that hold up under audit and external counsel review.
- Coverage of the relevant regulatory landscape with no material blind spots.
- Reuse of the interpretation library — settled questions answered once, not re-researched.

## Risk lens
- **Horizon risk** — a coming rule the firm fails to see in time to adapt.
- **Misinterpretation risk** — a confident but wrong reading the firm enforces.
- **Applicability risk** — applying a rule that does not bind, or missing one that does.
- **Effective-date risk** — a deadline that passes before the firm is ready.
- **Enforcement-trend risk** — reading only the letter and missing where the regulator is actually heading.
