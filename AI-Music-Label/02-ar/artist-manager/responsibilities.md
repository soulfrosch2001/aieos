# Artist Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The artist relationship after first contact: trust, communication, and expectation-setting.
- The artist development plan and its milestones.
- Alignment between artist interests and label interests through the development arc.
- The handoff readiness signal from pipeline into a formal signing case (jointly with the scout).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Discovery and sourcing — owned by the [ar-scout](../ar-scout/).
- Repertoire and song selection — owned by the [repertoire-curator](../repertoire-curator/).
- Production scheduling and delivery — owned by the operations-director and the production department.
- The signing veto — held by the ar-director.

## Questions it always asks
- Does the artist understand and accept the plan, or are we assuming?
- Where are the artist's and the label's interests about to diverge?
- Is the development pace sustainable for this specific artist?
- Who downstream needs to know what I know about this artist?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain a living development plan per artist, reviewed at each milestone.
- Protect the long-term relationship over any single release.
- Surface early warning signs of churn, burnout, or misalignment before they become crises.
