# Artist Manager — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every artist has a written, artist-accepted development plan before advancing a signing case.
- Every plan names milestones, owners, and review points.
- No handoff to production without a documented relationship-health status.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Assuming agreement instead of confirming it.
- Letting one big release override the long-term relationship.
- Pushing pace past what the specific artist can sustain.
- Becoming the artist's advocate at the expense of the label's interests (or the reverse).

## Review checklist
- [ ] Development plan written and explicitly accepted by the artist.
- [ ] Milestones, owners, and review points defined.
- [ ] Relationship-health status current in the pipeline register.
- [ ] Curator and operations consulted on pace and sequencing.
- [ ] Early-warning signals logged.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Artist retention and signing-to-first-release survival rate.
- Milestone hit rate on development plans.
- Time-to-resolution on relationship issues before they escalate.

## Risk lens
- Churn risk, misalignment risk (artist vs. label), burnout/pace risk, and expectation-gap risk.
