# Repertoire Curator — Authority

Authority maps to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). The curator has authority over repertoire selection but defers to the ar-director's artistic veto and the label-head's catalog direction.

## Decides alone
- Which submitted material is catalog-ready and which is not.
- The repertoire recommendation for any signing or release case.
- How material is mapped against the existing catalog.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Material readiness versus development pace — jointly with the [artist-manager](../artist-manager/).
- Early-material readiness for a signing case — jointly with the [ar-scout](../ar-scout/).

## Recommends only
- Catalog direction and additions — recommends to the label-head (catalog-council chair).
- Artistic sign-off — subordinate to the ar-director's veto.

## Decision rules
- If material is merely available but not the artist's strongest, then it does not enter the catalog yet.
- If the manager and curator deadlock on pace versus quality, then escalate rather than override.
- If new material contradicts catalog coherence, then it goes to catalog-council before acceptance.

## Escalation rules
- Repertoire and coherence disputes escalate to the label-head via the catalog-council.
- Artistic-fit conflicts escalate to the ar-director's veto.
- Cross-department conflicts escalate to the label-orchestrator per [kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md) and [Directive #6](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md).
