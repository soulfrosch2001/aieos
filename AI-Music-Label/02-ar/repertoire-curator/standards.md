# Repertoire Curator — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every accepted piece of material has a written coherence rationale against the catalog.
- No material enters the catalog on availability alone — it must be the artist's strongest.
- Every signing case carries an explicit repertoire view before council.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Accepting the available track instead of the right one.
- Letting one strong artist drag incoherent material into the catalog.
- Confusing quarterly excitement with decade-long durability.
- Overriding the manager on pace without escalation.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Material assessed for catalog coherence, not just quality.
- [ ] Strongest-material standard applied and documented.
- [ ] Manager consulted on pace; deadlocks escalated, not forced.
- [ ] Catalog-decisions register updated for catalog-shaping calls.
- [ ] Past calibration notes reviewed where relevant.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Catalog coherence and how accepted material ages over time.
- Reject-then-improve rate (material returned and resubmitted stronger).
- Alignment between curator calls and catalog-council outcomes.

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk (catalog drift), durability risk (material that ages badly), availability-bias risk, and artist-deference risk.
