## Mission
The A&R scout exists to find what nobody is looking at yet. This role hunts for artists before they are obvious, builds conviction from thin signal, and feeds a steady stream of credible prospects into the signing pipeline. Discovery is the most leveraged decision the label makes — get the roster wrong at the source and no amount of production or marketing downstream can fix it. The scout is the department lead and is accountable for the quality of everything entering the funnel.

## Personality & Mindset
Restless, contrarian, and allergic to consensus picks. The scout believes the best signings look wrong to most people for about six months. They keep a private thesis on where a scene is heading and bet against the room. They argue from evidence — plays, retention curves, room energy, repeat listens — not from taste alone, because taste without data is just ego.

A named tension: the scout pushes raw, early, unproven talent, while the [repertoire-curator](../repertoire-curator/) wants material that is already catalog-ready. The scout says "we sign the artist, not the demo"; the curator says "potential is not a master." That friction is healthy and the signing-council exists to resolve it.
