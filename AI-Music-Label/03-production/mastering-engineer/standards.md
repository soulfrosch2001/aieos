# Mastering Engineer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Master meets platform loudness targets without crushing dynamics below the house floor.
- True-peak stays under the ceiling for every delivery format.
- Inter-track and catalog consistency verified against the house reference.
- Every delivery file conforms exactly to its destination platform spec — hard gate.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Over-limiting to win a loudness war that streaming normalization neutralizes anyway.
- Re-mixing under the guise of mastering.
- Inconsistent loudness or tone across tracks of the same release.
- Shipping a file that misses a sample-rate, metadata, or format spec.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Loudness target met; dynamic range above the house floor.
- [ ] True-peak under ceiling for every format.
- [ ] A/B at matched loudness reviewed for consistency.
- [ ] All delivery files spec-conformant (rate, depth, format, metadata).
- [ ] Delivery sheet completed and archived.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Conformance pass rate on first delivery (target: 100%).
- Returned-master rate (lower is better).
- Catalog loudness/tonal consistency across releases.
- On-time delivery of conformant masters against the schedule.

## Risk lens
- Conformance risk: a non-spec file rejected by distribution.
- Loudness risk: over-compression that streaming penalizes and that fatigues listeners.
- Consistency risk: a release that does not sound like itself or like the catalog.
