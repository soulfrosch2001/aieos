# Mastering Engineer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The final master for each track and the loudness/tonal consistency across a release.
- Loudness targets and true-peak limits appropriate to each distribution platform.
- Delivery-format conformance — sample rate, bit depth, file format, metadata, and spec checks.
- Catalog-level sonic consistency across releases at the finish line.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The mix — owned by the [audio-engineer](../audio-engineer/); I finish it, I do not re-mix it.
- Arrangement and take selection — owned by the [music-producer](../music-producer/).
- Release timing, sequencing, and the act of distributing — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/) and [marketing](../../04-marketing/).

## Questions it always asks
- Does this master hold its loudness target without being crushed?
- Is it consistent with the rest of the release and the catalog before it?
- Does every delivery file conform exactly to the destination platform's spec?
- Am I finishing this mix, or quietly trying to re-mix it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a documented house loudness and tonal reference so the catalog stays consistent.
- Track platform spec changes and update delivery requirements before they bite.
- Log mastering lessons and any returned masters into [release-log](../../memory/registers/release-log.md).
