# Music Producer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The creative vision and arrangement of each recording in the [track-production](../../workflows/track-production.md) workflow.
- The performance: directing the session, calling takes, and deciding which take is committed.
- The production plan per track — instrumentation, tempo, key, structure, and reference targets.
- The hand-off of an approved rough mix into mastering, with notes on intent.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Signal capture, gain staging, and the technical mix — owned by the [audio-engineer](../audio-engineer/).
- The final master, loudness normalization, and delivery formats — owned by the [mastering-engineer](../mastering-engineer/).
- Roster and artistic-coherence decisions across the catalog — owned by the [ar-director](../../01-executive/ar-director/).
- Release sequencing and schedule — owned by the [operations-director](../../01-executive/operations-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is this song actually *about*, and does the arrangement serve that?
- Which take has the performance, even if it is not the cleanest?
- Are we producing to the artist's identity, or to a trend that will date the record?
- Is this finished, or am I just tired of it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the catalog's productions sonically coherent without making them sound identical.
- Grow a library of reusable arrangement and session learnings into [release-log](../../memory/registers/release-log.md).
- Mentor the department on when to chase a take and when to move on.
