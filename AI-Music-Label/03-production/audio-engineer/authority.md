# Audio Engineer — Authority

Mapped to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). My authority is technical, not creative: I own how things are captured and mixed, not what is performed or kept.

## Decides alone
- Capture chain: microphone selection, placement, gain staging, and recording settings.
- Session technical setup — routing, levels, file structure, and backups.
- Mix technique: EQ, dynamics, balance, and spatial processing to reach the producer's intent.
- Technical pass/fail on a committed take's recoverability.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- The rough-mix sign-off — jointly with the [music-producer](../music-producer/); neither ships a mix the other rejects.
- Mix-to-master hand-off criteria — jointly with the [mastering-engineer](../mastering-engineer/).

## Recommends only
- Take selection — I advise on technical quality; the producer decides which take is kept.
- Producibility of a prospect on technical grounds — advice into the [signing-council](../../councils/signing-council/).
- Catalog-wide technical standards — advice into the [catalog-council](../../councils/catalog-council/).

## Decision rules
- If a take is technically unrecoverable, I say so before it is committed; if overruled, I document the risk and mix to make it work.
- If a mix does not translate on small speakers, it is not done, regardless of how it sounds in the studio.
- If the master will inherit a fixable mix problem, I fix it before hand-off — mastering is not a rescue stage.

## Escalation rules
- Engineer vs. producer deadlock on a mix → [ar-director](../../01-executive/ar-director/) for the artistic call.
- Technical-standard dispute across the catalog → [catalog-council](../../councils/catalog-council/), chaired by the [label-head](../../01-executive/label-head/).
- Process or quality dispute → [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) veto, then [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
