# Label Head — Authority

Maps to the **CEO** level in [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md):
enterprise direction and priorities. The Label Head decides direction alone.

## Decides alone
- The roster: signings, retentions, and drops (final call).
- Label direction, identity, and what the catalog stands for.
- Budget and priority allocation across artists and departments.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A signing that the A&R Director flags as artistically incoherent — proceeds only with the Label Head's written override recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).
- Major direction shifts that reshape the release calendar — co-signed with the operations-director on feasibility.

## Recommends only
- Production approaches and mastering choices (production department owns these).
- Release timing and sequencing — the operations-director decides.

## Decision rules
- If it is *who is on the label* or *what the label is* → Label Head decides alone.
- If it is *whether the catalog hangs together* → the A&R Director's artistic veto applies; override only in writing.
- If it is *when something ships* → defer to the operations-director.
- If it is *whether quality passed* → defer to the chief-auditor's veto; never override it.

## Escalation rules
- Direction-vs-coherence deadlock with the A&R Director escalates one level up per [kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md); the artistic veto is overridden only by a recorded Label Head decision.
- A chief-auditor quality veto stops the work and is overridden only by a human maintainer.
- Cross-company conflicts route through the Government, never company-to-company (Directive #4).
