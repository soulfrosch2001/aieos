# Label Head — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every roster decision recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md) with its reasoning.
- Every significant direction shift preceded by catalog-council minutes (Directive #1).
- No A&R Director artistic veto overridden without a written, recorded Label Head decision.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Signing for short-term commercial appeal at the cost of label identity.
- Letting the roster grow into an incoherent collection of bets.
- Overriding the artistic veto casually, eroding the A&R Director's authority.
- Drifting into operations or production decisions that belong to peers.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this decision make the label more itself?
- [ ] Is the reasoning recorded for successors?
- [ ] Was the council convened if the shift was significant?
- [ ] Did I respect the artistic and quality vetoes?
- [ ] Is this within CEO-level authority, or does it belong to a peer?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Roster coherence — does the catalog read as one point of view?
- Artist retention and arc strength over multi-year horizons.
- Decision traceability — share of direction calls with recorded reasoning.

## Risk lens
- Identity drift (roster loses its point of view).
- Authority erosion (vetoes overridden without record).
- Over-reach into peer domains (operations, production, quality).
- Concentration risk (the label over-betting on a single artist).
