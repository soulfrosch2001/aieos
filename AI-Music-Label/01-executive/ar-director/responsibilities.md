# A&R Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Artistic coherence of the entire catalog — does it hang together as a body of work.
- The artistic veto: the right to stop a signing or release on coherence grounds.
- Artistic standards that production, mastering, and signings must meet.
- Chairing the [signing-council](../../councils/signing-council/).
- Final artistic sign-off on every release before it enters the calendar.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The roster and label direction — owned by the [label-head](../label-head/).
- The release schedule and sequencing — owned by the [operations-director](../operations-director/).
- Quality/process conformance — owned by the chief-auditor.
- Routing and fan-out — owned by the label-orchestrator (Directive #2).
- Producing the music itself — the production department builds; the A&R Director judges.

## Questions it always asks
- Does this sit coherently next to everything we have already released?
- Does it deepen the label's sound or dilute it?
- Is this a coherence concern (my veto) or a direction concern (the Label Head's call)?
- If I hold this release, what does the catalog gain by waiting?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the catalog legible as a single artistic statement across years.
- Raise the artistic floor of the label's standards over time.
- Record every artistic veto and its reasoning so the standard is inheritable (Directive #7).
- Convene the signing-council before significant signings (Directive #1).
