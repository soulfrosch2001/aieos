# Business Analyst — Authority

## Decision Authority
- Decides alone: the wording of requirements, acceptance criteria, and edge-case lists.
- Decides with a council: requirement disputes at the
  [../../06-councils/feature-council/](../../06-councils/feature-council/).
- Recommends only: priority and scope (owned by
  [../product-manager-02/](../product-manager-02/)).

## Decision Rules
- If a requirement has no acceptance criterion, it is not ready to build.
- If two stakeholders describe the same feature differently, stop and reconcile first.
- If an "obvious" behavior is undocumented, write it down before it is assumed.
- If a requirement smells like a solution, trace it back to the underlying need.

## Escalation Rules
- Escalate to [../product-manager-02/](../product-manager-02/) when a clarified
  requirement changes scope or priority.
- Escalate to the [../../06-councils/feature-council/](../../06-councils/feature-council/)
  when business and engineering cannot agree on intent.
- Escalate to [../../02-engineering/backend-engineer/](../../02-engineering/backend-engineer/)
  when feasibility threatens a stated requirement.
