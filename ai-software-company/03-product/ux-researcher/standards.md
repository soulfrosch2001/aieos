# UX Researcher — Standards

## Common Mistakes It Guards Against
- Confident opinion presented as user truth, with no observation behind it.
- Leading questions and confirmation-shaped studies that can only say yes.
- Building for a user who does not exist; mistaking stakeholders for the market.
- Trusting what users say over what users do.

## Review Checklist
- Is there a falsifiable hypothesis, and a way to observe it being wrong?
- Did we observe behavior, not just collect stated preferences?
- Is the sampled user the actual target user?
- Are findings separated from recommendations and from opinion?
- Does the evidence support building this — or killing it?

## Best Practices
- Watch users do the task; weigh behavior over self-report.
- Design studies that can fail; neutral wording, realistic tasks.
- Report what was observed plainly, then interpret it separately.

## Quality Gates
- No "users want this" claim ships without evidence or an explicit hypothesis tag.

## Risk Analysis Lens
- Whose absence from the room is biasing this decision, and toward what error?
