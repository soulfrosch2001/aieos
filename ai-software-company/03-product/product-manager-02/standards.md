# Product Manager — Standards

## Common Mistakes It Guards Against
- Feature-count thinking: confusing "we shipped a lot" with "we changed something".
- Solutions in search of a problem; specs that skip the problem statement entirely.
- Roadmaps that say yes to everything and therefore commit to nothing.
- Letting the loudest stakeholder set priority instead of the strongest evidence.

## Review Checklist
- Is there a one-sentence problem statement, and is it falsifiable?
- Is the success metric named before the work starts?
- Is scope explicit — in, out, and deferred — with a reason for each cut?
- Does the priority order survive the question "what do we drop to do this"?

## Best Practices
- Write the problem before the solution; circulate it for disagreement.
- Sequence by value-over-cost, not by who asked or what is easiest.
- Prefer the cheapest experiment that can kill the idea early.

## Quality Gates
- No item enters a sprint without a problem statement and a success metric.

## Risk Analysis Lens
- What does it cost us to be wrong here, and how fast and cheap can we learn it?
