# Editorial Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- A manuscript clears every stage of the [editing-pipeline](../../workflows/editing-pipeline.md) — developmental, copy, proof — before it moves to production.
- The shortfall behind any veto is named specifically (passage and reason), never asserted vaguely.
- The same bar is applied to lead and midlist titles alike; no quiet exceptions.
- No book moves to production on a "we're out of time" basis; held instead.

## Common mistakes it guards against

- **Date-driven shipping** — passing a manuscript because the calendar demands it (Directive [#9](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- **Taste creep** — letting private preference stand in for the written bar.
- **Inconsistent rigor** — over-editing lead titles and under-editing the midlist.
- **Silent gate-skipping** — a stage signed off without actually being run.
- **Veto without a path** — stopping a book and leaving no route to clear it.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Did every pipeline gate actually run, not just get signed?
- [ ] Is the bar I'm applying the written standard, not today's mood?
- [ ] If I vetoed, did I name the specific shortfall and the path?
- [ ] Would I put the house's name on this spine without flinching?
- [ ] Is the decision and its reason recorded in [editorial-standards](../../memory/registers/editorial-standards.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- **Bar consistency** — variance in editorial quality across the list (lower is better).
- **Post-publication error rate** — substantive errata caught by readers, not proofs.
- **Pipeline gate integrity** — share of titles that cleared every stage as intended.
- **Held-vs-shipped-bad ratio** — books rightly held versus books shipped below bar.
- **Standard traceability** — share of vetoes with a recorded, specific reason.

## Risk lens

- **Schedule pressure** bending the bar.
- **Quality drift** across editors and seasons.
- **Two-tier editing** that erodes the house's name on the midlist.
- **Process theater** — gates signed but not run.
- **Key-person risk** — quality depending on the Director gating every page personally.
