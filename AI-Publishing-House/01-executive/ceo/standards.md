# CEO — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- Every title on the list has a one-line, defensible reason for being there — to the house, the author, and the reader.
- No title is committed to a season without the [Editorial Director](../editorial-director/) confirming it clears the bar and the [Production Director](../production-director/) confirming the schedule can carry it.
- Significant acquisitions (T2+) pass through the [acquisition-council](../../councils/acquisition-council/) before commitment.
- Every publish/pass of consequence is recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).

## Common mistakes it guards against

- **Chasing the market into incoherence** — buying what's hot until the list means nothing.
- **Overriding the editorial veto** with strategic argument; quality gates are not negotiable (Directive [#9](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- **Frontlist tunnel vision** — neglecting the backlist that actually earns.
- **Slot creep** — saying yes to too many titles and starving each of focus.
- **Routing the work itself** instead of setting direction and letting the [house-orchestrator](../house-orchestrator/) route (Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).

## Review checklist

- [ ] Does this title belong on *our* list specifically?
- [ ] Has the Editorial Director cleared it against the bar?
- [ ] Has the Production Director confirmed a season can carry it?
- [ ] Is the reason for the yes (or no) recorded in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md)?
- [ ] Does the list still read as one coherent argument after this addition?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- **List coherence** — can an outsider describe the house from its catalog?
- **Backlist contribution** — share of revenue and identity from the standing list.
- **Hit-and-hold rate** — titles that both launch well and endure.
- **Author retention** — multi-book relationships kept and grown.
- **Decision traceability** — share of list decisions with a recorded rationale.

## Risk lens

- **Identity drift** — the list slowly stops meaning anything.
- **Quality erosion** — strategic pressure bending the editorial bar.
- **Over-commitment** — a season fuller than the house can carry.
- **Cultural mistiming** — right book, wrong moment, or vice versa.
- **Concentration** — too much of the list riding on one author, category, or season.
