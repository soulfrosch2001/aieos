# Production Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- Every committed pub date is backed by a dependency chain with real lead times — no aspirational dates.
- A title enters the [book-release](../../workflows/book-release.md) workflow only after the [Editorial Director](../editorial-director/) confirms it has cleared editorial.
- The season is sequenced as a portfolio with no overcommitted weeks; capacity is checked before any date is promised.
- Print specs and files are verified print-ready before the run is committed.

## Common mistakes it guards against

- **Wishful scheduling** — promising a date the chain can't carry.
- **Shipping over the editorial bar** to protect a date (Directive [#9](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- **Pile-ups** — stacking too many launches into one window.
- **Silent slippage** — a date moving without the cascade being communicated early.
- **Single points of failure** in print runs or vendors with no fallback.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Is this date backed by a real dependency chain, not a hope?
- [ ] Has editorial confirmed the book is cleared to ship?
- [ ] Is the season sequenced so nothing is overcommitted or starved?
- [ ] Are the print specs verified print-ready before committing the run?
- [ ] Is the dated commitment and any change recorded in [rights-and-sales](../../memory/registers/rights-and-sales.md)?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- **On-time ship rate** — share of titles shipping on their committed date.
- **Schedule realism** — frequency and size of date slips.
- **Sequencing health** — absence of overcommitted weeks; even portfolio load.
- **Print quality/defect rate** — runs accepted without rework.
- **Channel availability** — stock present at pub date across channels.

## Risk lens

- **Date risk** — a slip cascading through every title behind it.
- **Capacity risk** — a season committed beyond what the chain can carry.
- **Vendor/print risk** — single points of failure in manufacturing.
- **Quality-vs-date pressure** — shipping early at the cost of the bar.
- **Channel risk** — stock or format not ready at the promised moment.
