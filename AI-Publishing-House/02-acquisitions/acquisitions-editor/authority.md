# Acquisitions Editor — Authority

Bound to [../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md): every right below is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*. Authority is explicit; veto is rare and absolute.

## Decides alone
- To **pass** on a submission or proposal — a no is the editor's to make and record.
- The contents and framing of the acquisition brief and which books reach the [acquisition-council](../../councils/acquisition-council/).
- The deal envelope (advance ceiling, target rights, must-haves) handed to the [contracts-manager](../contracts-manager/).
- Revise-and-resubmit requests to an author or agent before any commitment.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- **Acquiring** a book: recommended by this role, but the list belongs to the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/), who decides what the house publishes. The editor's brief is the basis; the CEO signs the list.
- Editorial feasibility on borderline manuscripts — joint read with the [Editorial Director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/), who holds the quality veto.
- Final negotiable terms within the envelope — agreed with the [contracts-manager](../contracts-manager/) before an offer goes out.

## Recommends only
- Release timing and sequencing — recommends to the [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/), who decides alone.
- Print, format, and design direction — recommends to [Production](../../04-production/).
- Category and direction shifts surfaced by the funnel — recommends to the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).

## Decision rules
- If a book is excellent but off-direction → pass, and record why in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md).
- If editorial feasibility is unclear → do not recommend acquire until a joint read with the [Editorial Director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/) resolves it.
- If the deal cannot close inside the envelope → re-open the envelope with the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/), do not quietly stretch it.
- If acquiring would blur the list's identity → escalate the trade-off, do not decide it alone.

## Escalation rules
- Acquire/pass deadlock with peers → up one level to the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/) per [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- An [Editorial Director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/) quality veto stops an acquisition; only a human maintainer overrides it (decision-authority §Resolution).
- A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) process veto on the acquisition workflow halts the signing until cleared.
- Cross-company sourcing or rights questions route through the [house-orchestrator](../../01-executive/house-orchestrator/) and the Government — companies never talk directly (Directive #5).
