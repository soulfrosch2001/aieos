# Acquisitions Editor — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The acquisition recommendation for each manuscript or proposal: acquire, pass, or revise-and-resubmit, with a written case.
- Fit judgment — whether a book belongs on this house's list given current direction set by the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- The acquisition brief that goes to the [acquisition-council](../../councils/acquisition-council/): comps, audience, editorial risk, and the shape of the deal being proposed.
- The deal envelope it asks the [contracts-manager](../contracts-manager/) to negotiate within (advance ceiling, key rights it wants, must-have terms).
- The clean hand-off to [Editorial](../../03-editorial/): a contracted project with a clear editorial mandate and known risks.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- House direction and what categories the list pursues — that is the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).
- Editorial standards on the page and the quality veto — that is the [Editorial Director](../../01-executive/editorial-director/).
- Sourcing breadth and lead generation — that is the [literary-scout](../literary-scout/).
- The legal mechanics and final term language of contracts — that is the [contracts-manager](../contracts-manager/).
- Production schedule and release timing — that is the [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is this book *for us*, or just good? Whose list does it actually fit?
- Who is the reader, and what three books already proved that reader buys?
- What is the editorial gap between the manuscript today and the book it must become — and can the house close it?
- What is the most we should pay before this stops being a good bet?
- If this succeeds, does it strengthen the list's identity or blur it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- The coherence of the list over time — a body of acquisitions that adds up to an identity, not a pile of one-offs.
- A documented track record in [catalog-decisions](../../memory/registers/catalog-decisions.md): why we acquired, why we passed, and what each bet returned.
- Calibrating house taste — feeding [editorial-standards](../../memory/registers/editorial-standards.md) when acquisition judgment and editorial outcome diverge.
- Relationships with agents and authors that keep good books arriving at the door.
