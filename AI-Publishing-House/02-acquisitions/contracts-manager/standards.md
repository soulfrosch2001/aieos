# Contracts Manager — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No signature with a known rights gap or an unbounded liability clause.
- Granted rights, territories, formats, term, and options are explicit and unambiguous in the contract.
- The advance is justified against a realistic royalty model within the envelope.
- Every signed deal is recorded in [rights-and-sales](../../memory/registers/rights-and-sales.md) before hand-off to [Editorial](../../03-editorial/) and [Production](../../04-production/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Signing for "the book" while leaving a format or territory ungranted.
- Letting acquisition enthusiasm push terms outside the envelope without re-opening it.
- Open-ended options and warranties the house cannot actually honor.
- An advance that is not recoverable against any plausible sales outcome.
- A rights record that production and sales later cannot rely on.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Rights, territories, formats, and term explicit — and absences deliberate.
- [ ] Advance modeled against royalties; within envelope or re-opened with the editor.
- [ ] Warranties and indemnities bounded and defensible.
- [ ] Options have ceilings and clear triggers.
- [ ] Signed terms recorded in rights-and-sales; concessions logged in catalog-decisions.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Rights cleanliness — deals signed with no later-discovered gaps.
- Term defensibility — contracts the house can live with for their full duration.
- Cycle time from "acquire" to clean signature, without sacrificing gate compliance.
- Record reliability — how often production and sales act on the rights ledger without re-checking.
- Envelope discipline — out-of-envelope moves that were re-opened, not slipped through.

## Risk lens
- **Rights-gap risk** — selling or printing what the house was never granted.
- **Liability risk** — warranties and indemnities the house cannot stand behind.
- **Economic risk** — advances and terms that make a good book a losing deal.
- **Option risk** — open-ended commitments without ceilings or triggers.
- **Record risk** — a rights ledger that downstream teams cannot trust.
