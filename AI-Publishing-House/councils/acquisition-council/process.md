# Acquisition Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel discussion norms (inherited via the stdlib
[feature-council](../../../councils/feature-council/)). Dissent is recorded, never
suppressed (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). The scout
and the editor speak first and unfiltered; rank does not settle a literary
judgment, argument does. "We already love it" is not an input — the case must be
made on the manuscript, the market, and the terms.

## Decision process
1. **Frame** — the [acquisitions-editor](../../02-acquisitions/acquisitions-editor/)
   states the title, the reader it is for, and why now.
2. **Read-out** — scout and editorial-feasibility reads are presented, including
   the cost to bring the book to house standard.
3. **Terms** — [contracts-manager](../../02-acquisitions/contracts-manager/) lays
   out advance, rights, and territories, with the walk-away number.
4. **Alternatives** — the council names what else the same capacity could buy, and
   the honest option to pass.
5. **Decide** — acquire / pass / acquire-with-conditions. The
   [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/) chairs and breaks deadlocks.
6. **Record** — minutes, the decision, and all dissent are filed (see
   [output.md](output.md)).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: [T2](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) standard
  acquisitions — council decision is sufficient.
- Must escalate: [T3](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) list-defining or
  high-advance acquisitions need council **plus** executive sign-off; see
  [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
Deadlock escalates one level up the chain per
[decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md) and
[escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). A
[chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto halts the
acquisition; only a human maintainer overrides it.
