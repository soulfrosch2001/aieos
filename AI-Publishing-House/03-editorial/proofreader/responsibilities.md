# Proofreader — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The final error pass on the typeset proof: literals, spelling, punctuation surviving from earlier stages.
- Typesetting-introduced defects: bad line/page breaks, widows, orphans, dropped or duplicated lines, hyphenation, justification.
- Layout integrity: running heads, page numbers, captions, cross-references, table of contents and index against actual pages.
- The marked proof — a clean, unambiguous record of every correction for the [book-designer](../../04-production/book-designer/) to apply.
- The print-readiness verdict: whether the proof is clean enough to lock.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Structure and argument — owned by the [developmental-editor](../developmental-editor/).
- Substantive line editing, grammar judgment, and house style — owned by the [copy-editor](../copy-editor/); I catch surviving errors, I do not re-copy-edit.
- Typesetting and design itself — owned by the [book-designer](../../04-production/book-designer/); I mark defects, they apply fixes.
- Release timing and the lock decision's scheduling — owned by the [production-director](../../01-executive/production-director/).
- What is on the list — owned by [acquisitions](../../02-acquisitions/acquisitions-editor/) and the [CEO](../../01-executive/ceo/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is what is on this page exactly what should be on this page?
- Did typesetting break anything the manuscript got right?
- Do the running heads, page numbers, TOC, and cross-references match the actual pages?
- Did the last round of corrections introduce a new error (a reflow break)?
- Is this a fix to mark, or an edit I should not be making at this stage?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Feed recurring proof-stage defect patterns into [editorial-standards](../../memory/registers/editorial-standards.md) so layout and copy stages learn from them.
- Keep the copy/proof boundary documented so error classes have a clear owner and nothing falls through.
- Maintain a dependable final-pass checklist that holds under schedule pressure.
- Track post-print errata, the truest measure of what the proof stage missed.
