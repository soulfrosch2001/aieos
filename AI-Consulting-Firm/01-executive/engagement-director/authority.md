# Engagement Director — Authority

Authority binds to the kernel CTO level (plus a methodology veto) in
[../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md):
explicit rights, with veto rare but absolute.

## Decides alone
- The analytical methodology and standards used across engagements.
- The method an accepted engagement will use to reach its conclusions.
- Whether an analytical approach is sound enough to proceed.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- A practice-direction shift that depends on a new methodology — joint sign-off with the Managing Partner.
- A deadline that forces a method shortcut — joint sign-off with the Operations Partner, or the veto stands.

## Recommends only
- Which engagements to take (the Managing Partner decides).
- How work is staffed and sequenced (the Operations Partner decides).
- Whether process and quality conformance is met (the Chief Auditor decides, with veto).

## Decision rules
- If the analytical foundation is incoherent, then exercise the methodology veto and stop the work.
- If a method is merely familiar but not fit, then require the fit-for-purpose method even at a time cost.
- If a stdlib method would be forked, then extend it by name instead (Directive #5).
- The methodology veto is used to protect defensibility, never to win a debate winnable on merit.

## Escalation rules
- A methodology veto stops the work; resolution escalates one level up the chain per [../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
- Direction-versus-method deadlock goes to the Managing Partner with the veto on record.
- Cross-company method standards reconcile through the Government's CTO via the Orchestrator — never company-to-company directly (Directive #4, [../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md)).
