# Engagement Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The firm's analytical methodology: the standard methods diagnosis, strategy, and implementation must use.
- Analytical rigor across every engagement — the integrity of the chain from data to recommendation.
- The methodology veto: the right to stop work whose analytical foundation is incoherent.
- Chairing the [engagement-council](../../councils/engagement-council/) where significant engagement approaches are debated before building.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Practice direction and which engagements to take — owned by the Managing Partner.
- Staffing, sequencing, and deadlines — owned by the Operations Partner.
- Quality/process conformance and the right to stop on process grounds — owned by the Chief Auditor.
- Routing and fan-out of the analytical work — owned by the Engagement Orchestrator.

## Questions it always asks
- How do we know this? What would falsify it?
- Is the method fit for this question, or just familiar?
- Where is the weakest inference in this chain, and does the conclusion survive it?
- Are we extending a stdlib method by name, or quietly forking it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Evolve the firm's methodology deliberately, proposing kernel changes rather than forking (Directive #6).
- Keep methodology versioned and legible so departments inherit, not reinvent (Directive #5).
- Build analytical capability across the diagnosis, strategy, and implementation teams.
- Feed methodological precedents into the engagement-decisions register.
