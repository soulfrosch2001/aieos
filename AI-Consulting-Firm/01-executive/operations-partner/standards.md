# Operations Partner — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every deadline commitment has a staffed plan and a named critical path behind it.
- Fan-out has disjoint ownership — no two agents own the same deliverable (Directive #3 intent).
- The cost of any rigor-driven delay is quantified before the methodology veto is exercised.
- No date is promised that the firm's capacity cannot deliver to standard.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Committing a date without a delivery plan behind it.
- Overlapping ownership in fan-out, causing rework and merge conflict.
- Hiding a capacity shortfall instead of renegotiating the date.
- Treating a quality veto as a deadline obstacle rather than a hard stop.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the critical path identified and the slip risk named?
- [ ] Is every fan-out lane disjoint and independently deliverable?
- [ ] Is the staffing within real capacity, not aspirational?
- [ ] Have staffing-versus-direction conflicts been signed off with the Managing Partner?
- [ ] Is the delivery lesson captured in the engagement-lessons register?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- On-time delivery: engagements landed on the committed date.
- Capacity honesty: commitments made within real capacity.
- Fan-out cleanliness: rework caused by overlapping ownership (target zero).
- Lessons captured: every delivery cycle recorded in engagement-lessons.

## Risk lens
- Capacity risk — promising more than the bench can deliver to standard.
- Critical-path risk — a single dependency that sinks the commitment.
- Overlap risk — non-disjoint fan-out causing collisions and rework.
- Schedule-pressure risk — squeezing rigor to hit a date and earning a veto instead.
