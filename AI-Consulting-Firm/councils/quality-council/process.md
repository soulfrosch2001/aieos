# Quality Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Follows the kernel debate norms in [communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). The standard is rigor, not consensus comfort: a method is challenged on its merits regardless of who built it. Dissent is recorded, never suppressed (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)). The chair guards independence — the council judges quality even when the conclusion is commercially convenient.

## Decision process
1. The author presents the analysis: question, data, method, assumptions, and conclusion.
2. The council tests data provenance, methodological fit, assumption traceability, and sensitivity of the conclusion to its weakest assumption.
3. The [engagement-director](../../01-executive/engagement-director/README.md) rules on methodology; the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/README.md) rules on overall quality and conformance.
4. The council names remaining concerns, risks, and alternatives considered.
5. Verdict: **pass**, **rework**, or **fail**. A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/README.md) quality veto or [engagement-director](../../01-executive/engagement-director/README.md) methodology veto halts the work absolutely ([decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).

## Approval gate by tier
- **Approves alone:** T0–T2 — the council's verdict is sufficient.
- **Must escalate:** T3–T4 — verdict stands, but the engagement also needs its executive gate; unresolved disputes escalate per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
A quality veto stops the work and is overridden only by a human maintainer ([decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)). Deadlocks the chair cannot resolve escalate one level up per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). Cross-company matters route through the Government, never company-to-company (Directive [#4](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md); [communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md)).
