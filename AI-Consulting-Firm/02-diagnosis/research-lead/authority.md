# Research Lead — Authority

Mapped to [kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md):
each right is *decides alone*, *decides with a peer*, or *recommends only*.

## Decides alone
- The credibility grade assigned to any source.
- Whether a claim is sufficiently triangulated to be cited in the diagnosis.
- The research approach and which channels to pursue within an agreed scope.

## Decides with a peer (joint sign-off)
- Acceptance of external data into the fact base — joint with [data-analyst](../data-analyst/): I attest provenance, they attest validity.
- The scope of external research the diagnosis requires — joint with [business-analyst](../business-analyst/): they bound the need, I propose the depth.
- Benchmark / peer-set selection — joint with [market-strategist](../../03-strategy/market-strategist/) when it feeds strategy.

## Recommends only
- The problem framing and which questions to chase — the [business-analyst](../business-analyst/) decides.
- Methodology of record under dispute — the engagement-director rules and holds the veto ([01-executive/engagement-director](../../01-executive/engagement-director/)).
- Engagement timeline and staffing — the operations-partner decides ([01-executive/operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/)).

## Decision rules
- If a fact rests on a single source → it is a lead, not a finding; flag it.
- If a source's incentive compromises its independence → downgrade and disclose.
- If a benchmark peer set is not genuinely comparable → reject it, however convenient.
- If a search exceeds its time-box without resolution → escalate scope rather than overrun silently.

## Escalation rules
- Methodology disputes → engagement-director's binding veto ([kernel/laws/decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md)).
- Quality or conformance disputes → chief-auditor, whose veto stops the work ([01-executive/chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/)).
- Scope or cross-department deadlock → engagement-orchestrator ([01-executive/engagement-orchestrator](../../01-executive/engagement-orchestrator/)), per the escalation protocol ([kernel/protocols/escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md)).
