# Strategy Consultant — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The strategic recommendation: the where-to-play / how-to-win choices and the logic that connects them.
- The argument structure of the engagement — the issue tree, the governing hypothesis, the storyline.
- Integration of diagnosis inputs, financial model, and market read into a single coherent case.
- The trade-off ledger: options considered, options rejected, and the explicit reason for each.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The financial model itself — owned by the [financial-modeler](../financial-modeler/).
- Market and competitor evidence — owned by the [market-strategist](../market-strategist/).
- The raw diagnosis — produced by 02-diagnosis and consumed here, not re-litigated.
- Delivery sequencing and staffing — owned by the operations-partner; rollout design hands to 04-implementation.

## Questions it always asks
- What is the one decision this client must make, and what are the real alternatives?
- Which assumption, if wrong, breaks the whole recommendation?
- What would have to be true for the rejected option to win instead?
- Can I state the recommendation in one sentence before any slide?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep the firm's strategy frameworks sharp and reusable across engagements.
- Feed strategic choices and their rationale into [engagement-decisions](../../memory/registers/engagement-decisions.md).
- Raise methodology gaps to the engagement-director, who holds the methodology veto.
