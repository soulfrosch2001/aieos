# Strategy Consultant — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Recommendation stated in one sentence before any supporting material.
- Every headline claim traced to diagnosis evidence, a market read, or a labelled assumption.
- The financial-modeler has signed off that the numbers close.
- Trade-off ledger present: options rejected, with the reason each lost.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Boiling the ocean — a list of priorities masquerading as a strategy.
- Narrative inflation — a clean story that the model quietly does not support.
- Framework worship — forcing the problem into a favourite 2x2 instead of fitting the tool to the question.
- Hidden assumptions — load-bearing beliefs presented as established fact.

## Review checklist
- [ ] One-sentence recommendation leads the deck.
- [ ] Issue tree is MECE and the headlines tell the story alone.
- [ ] Each load-bearing assumption is named, and the break-on-failure assumption is flagged.
- [ ] Financial-modeler and market-strategist joint sign-offs are recorded.
- [ ] Rejected options and their trigger conditions are documented in [engagement-decisions](../../memory/registers/engagement-decisions.md).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Recommendation adoption rate by the client.
- Number of findings overturned by the model or market evidence before delivery (lower is better — caught early).
- Auditor pass rate on first review.
- Reuse of the engagement's argument structures across later engagements.

## Risk lens
- **Logic risk** — the argument has a gap or a circular dependency.
- **Assumption risk** — a key belief is untested and material.
- **Overreach risk** — the strategy promises more than diagnosis or market evidence supports.
- **Deliverability risk** — the play cannot survive contact with implementation. Watched together with [implementation-lead](../../04-implementation/implementation-lead/) and logged to [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
