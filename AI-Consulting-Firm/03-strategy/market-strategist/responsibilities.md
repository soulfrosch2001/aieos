# Market Strategist — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The market read: sizing, segmentation, demand drivers, and trajectory.
- Competitive analysis: who the rivals are, their economics, and their likely reactions.
- Positioning: where the strategy can differentiate and defend.
- The customer view: who buys, why, and what would make them switch.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The strategic recommendation — owned by the [strategy-consultant](../strategy-consultant/).
- The financial model that turns demand into cash — owned by the [financial-modeler](../financial-modeler/).
- Primary internal diagnosis — produced by 02-diagnosis and consumed here.
- Go-to-market execution — handed to 04-implementation.

## Questions it always asks
- Is this market real and reachable, or a top-down number with no bottom-up basis?
- What does the strongest competitor do the day after we move?
- Which segment actually pays, and is it one segment or several?
- What outside-in evidence would falsify our positioning?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the firm's competitive and market-intelligence knowledge base.
- Feed positioning choices and competitive reads into [engagement-decisions](../../memory/registers/engagement-decisions.md).
- Flag market and competitor risks to [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
