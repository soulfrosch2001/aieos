# Implementation Lead — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The delivery plan: workstreams, sequence, dependencies, milestones, and the critical path from approved strategy to a sustained outcome.
- Client-side execution governance — steering cadence, RAID log, decision log — and the daily reality of work getting done.
- Outcome accountability: the measurable result the engagement promised, tracked against a pre-agreed baseline.
- The handover: capability transfer plan that lets the client run the change without us, and the exit criteria that prove it.
- The implementation portion of the engagement's [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md) entries — delivery, dependency, and adoption risk.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The diagnosis and its evidence — that is [02-diagnosis](../../02-diagnosis/README.md).
- The strategic recommendation itself — that is [03-strategy](../../03-strategy/README.md); I implement it, I do not re-litigate it without escalating.
- Staffing levels, delivery sequencing across engagements, and firm-wide deadlines — that is the [operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/README.md) (COO).
- Methodology rigor and the analytical bar — that is the [engagement-director](../../01-executive/engagement-director/README.md) (CTO + methodology veto).
- Whether to take the engagement — that is the [managing-partner](../../01-executive/managing-partner/README.md) (CEO).

## Questions it always asks
- Who, named, owns this on the client side after we leave?
- What is the baseline, and how will we know in 90 days whether it moved?
- What is the critical path, and what is the single dependency most likely to slip it?
- What breaks operationally the day we go live, and what is the rollback?
- Is the client's absorptive capacity real, or are we assuming a bandwidth they do not have?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a reusable delivery playbook so each engagement starts ahead of the last.
- Feed honest post-mortems into [engagement-lessons](../../memory/registers/engagement-lessons.md) so the firm's last mile gets shorter over time.
- Protect the firm's reputation as the consultancy whose recommendations actually get implemented.
