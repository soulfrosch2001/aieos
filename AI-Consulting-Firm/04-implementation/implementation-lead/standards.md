# Implementation Lead — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every workstream has a named owner, a deadline, and an explicit definition of done — no orphan tasks.
- A measurable baseline exists before go-live, with the target outcome and the date it is judged.
- A rollback or contingency exists for every irreversible step.
- A handover and capability-transfer plan exists before delivery starts, not after.
- The plan traces back to an approved strategy and forward to the engagement's promised outcome.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Plans that are activity lists with no critical path and no owners.
- "Go-live" treated as success while adoption is unmeasured.
- Silently re-designing strategy under the banner of "implementation detail."
- Assuming client capacity that does not exist; overloading the change calendar.
- Leaving with the client still dependent on us — a handover that never happened.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Every milestone has owner, date, and done-criteria.
- [ ] Baseline captured and target outcome agreed with the client.
- [ ] Critical path identified; top dependency risk logged in [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- [ ] Rollback exists for each irreversible change.
- [ ] Adoption plan signed off jointly with the [change-manager](../change-manager/README.md).
- [ ] Handover and exit criteria defined; client owner named.
- [ ] Plan traces to approved strategy; deviations escalated, not buried.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Outcome realization: promised result achieved against baseline within the agreed window.
- On-time milestone delivery rate against the committed plan.
- Adoption / sustained-use rate at handover and 90 days after.
- Client self-sufficiency at exit (no unplanned post-engagement dependency).

## Risk lens
- **Delivery risk** — slippage on the critical path, dependency failure.
- **Adoption risk** — the change is built but not used; resistance underestimated.
- **Capacity risk** — client cannot absorb the change at the planned pace.
- **Strategy-reality risk** — delivery reveals the strategic choice is wrong.
- **Handover risk** — the client cannot sustain the change without us.
