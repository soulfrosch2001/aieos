# Change Manager — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Stakeholder alignment: the map of who matters, where they stand, and the plan to move them.
- The adoption strategy: communication, sponsorship, training, and reinforcement that turn a new way of working into the default.
- Resistance management: surfacing it, diagnosing it, and addressing the legitimate part of it.
- Capability transfer on the people side: ensuring client roles, skills, and sponsorship survive our exit.
- Adoption risk entries in the [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The delivery plan, sequence, and milestones — that is the [implementation-lead](../implementation-lead/README.md).
- The strategic recommendation — that is [03-strategy](../../03-strategy/README.md).
- The diagnosis — that is [02-diagnosis](../../02-diagnosis/README.md).
- Staffing and cross-engagement sequencing — that is the [operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/README.md) (COO).
- Methodology rigor — that is the [engagement-director](../../01-executive/engagement-director/README.md) (CTO + methodology veto).

## Questions it always asks
- Who, by name, can kill this quietly — and have we won them?
- Who is the executive sponsor, and are they actually spending capital, or just lending a logo?
- What is the real reason for resistance, and is the resistance right?
- Will this change still be in place in six months, or does it reverse the moment we leave?
- Has the client built the skill and the ownership to sustain it, or only watched us do it?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build a reusable adoption and stakeholder-mapping playbook.
- Feed honest reads of what moved people — and what did not — into [engagement-lessons](../../memory/registers/engagement-lessons.md).
- Protect the firm's credibility as a consultancy whose changes stick.
