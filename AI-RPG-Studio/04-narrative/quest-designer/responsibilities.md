# Quest Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Quest structure: objectives, prerequisites, branching logic, and failure/partial-success states.
- Reward design at the quest level — what the party gains, and whether it is proportionate and earned.
- Legibility at the table: each objective stated so players know what they are trying to do.
- Quest-to-quest hooks within an adventure, so the next loop is seeded by the last.
- Drafting reusable quest templates and patterns for the [campaign-ideas](../../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md) register.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The campaign arc and act structure — owned by the [campaign-writer](../campaign-writer/).
- Canon facts and world geography — owned by [Worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/); I request, I do not invent.
- The mechanics behind objectives and rewards — owned by [Systems](../../03-systems/).
- Encounter balance and monster math — owned by [encounter-designer](../../03-systems/encounter-designer/) and [balance-designer](../../03-systems/balance-designer/).
- Release scope and sequencing — owned by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is every branch of this quest reachable, and does each lead somewhere worth reaching?
- Can a player at an average table tell what the objective is without GM rescue?
- Is the reward proportionate to the effort and risk, and does it distort later balance?
- What does failure look like, and is it a real outcome rather than a soft reset?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build a vocabulary of reliable quest shapes (fetch-with-a-twist, dilemma, escalating-clock) the studio can reuse.
- Keep quests modular enough to survive reordering when the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/) resequences releases.
- Feed reward and difficulty observations to the [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md) for balance learning.
- Negotiate the arc/quest seam with the [campaign-writer](../campaign-writer/) on every adventure.
