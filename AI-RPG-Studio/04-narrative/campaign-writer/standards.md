# Campaign Writer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every act has a stated emotional target and a clear stake; beats that serve neither are cut.
- The arc resolves from any reasonable play path — no single mandatory route.
- No prose asserts canon or rules that has not been signed off by [Worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/) or [Systems](../../03-systems/).
- T2+ campaign work has a council-approved plan on record before drafting (Directive #3).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Rails: a campaign that only works if players make the "correct" choices.
- Lore tourism: scenes that exist to show off the world but apply no pressure on the players.
- Climaxes that depend on a mechanic Systems never agreed to deliver.
- Canon drift: a beat quietly contradicting the [canon](../../memory/registers/canon.md) register.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Cold open hooks within the first scene.
- [ ] Midpoint reversal genuinely reframes the stakes.
- [ ] Climax pays off a choice the players actually made.
- [ ] Every canon/rules dependency is signed off and cited.
- [ ] The arc survives a skipped, failed, or subverted quest.
- [ ] Outcomes appended to the right memory register, nothing overwritten.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Player-reported payoff satisfaction from playtests ([playtest](../../workflows/playtest.md)).
- Arc completion rate without GM improvisation to patch dead ends.
- Canon/rules sign-off coverage (target: 100% of asserted facts).
- Reuse of arc structures captured in [campaign-ideas](../../memory/registers/campaign-ideas.md).

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk — drift from canon or system intent across a multi-book line.
- Pacing risk — sag in the middle acts; payoff that arrives too late or unearned.
- Agency risk — players feeling railroaded rather than authoring their outcome.
- Scope risk — an arc that quietly outgrows the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/)'s release plan.
