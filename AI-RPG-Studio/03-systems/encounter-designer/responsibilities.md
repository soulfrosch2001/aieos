# Encounter Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The design of individual encounters: composition, terrain, objectives, and the dramatic arc each is meant to produce.
- The encounter difficulty math layered on the core rules (threat budgets, scaling tables, action-economy pressure).
- The encounter entries inside books and modules — that each one is runnable as written by a GM.
- Contributions to [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md): what each playtested encounter taught.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The core resolution mechanic the encounters sit on — owned by the [rules-designer](../rules-designer/).
- The statistical balance curves and fairness thresholds — owned by the [balance-designer](../balance-designer/).
- The story and stakes the encounter serves — owned by [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).
- The world and creatures' fiction — owned by [02-worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/).
- Release sequencing — owned by the [line-producer](../../01-executive/line-producer/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the intended feeling at each beat of this encounter, and does the math produce it?
- Can I build this on the core rules without asking the [rules-designer](../rules-designer/) to bend them?
- Is the difficulty fair under the [balance-designer](../balance-designer/)'s thresholds, or just exciting on paper?
- Can a GM run this from the page without rebuilding it, and recover if a party breaks the script?
- Does the encounter serve the story beat the [campaign-writer](../../04-narrative/campaign-writer/) intended?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Build a reusable library of encounter shapes and difficulty templates that scale across lines.
- Track which encounter types repeatedly miss their intended tension and retire or rework them.
- Keep the encounter math honest against live playtest evidence in [encounter-log](../../memory/registers/encounter-log.md).
- Grow GM-facing runnability so encounters need less reading and less rebuilding at the table.
