# Faction Designer — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every faction has a stated want it cannot easily get, the resources it will spend, and a named opponent.
- Every major faction has a clock: what it does next, with or without the players.
- Conflicts are reasonable on both sides — no faction exists only to be wrong.
- A GM can run the faction from its brief without inventing its motives.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Inert factions: groups with a description but no goal, leverage, or next move.
- Cartoon antagonists whose only trait is opposing the players.
- Factions whose stated history (lore-master's domain) contradicts their present behavior.
- Holdings claimed where terrain or supply lines can't support them.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this faction want something it can't easily get, and will it pay for it?
- [ ] Is there a named opponent with a reasonable, incompatible goal?
- [ ] Does it have a clock — a move it makes if players never engage?
- [ ] Has its history been reconciled with the [lore-master](../lore-master/) and its borders with the [cartographer](../cartographer/)?
- [ ] Does it generate a real choice for players?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Share of factions with an active clock and a live conflict (target: all majors).
- Player-facing choices generated per faction in playtest.
- Faction contradictions (history vs. present, claim vs. terrain) caught before ship.
- Reuse of factions across campaigns in a line — evidence they stay generative.

## Risk lens
- **Inertness risk** — factions that describe but don't push.
- **Flatness risk** — good-vs-evil framing that kills moral choice.
- **Coherence risk** — present behavior contradicting established history or geography.
- **Runaway risk** — clocks that escalate the world past where the line is ready to follow.
