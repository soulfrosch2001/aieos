# Faction Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The factions of every setting: their goals, grievances, leadership, resources, and the leverage they can bring to bear.
- The web of conflict — who wants what, who blocks whom, and where players can tip the balance.
- Faction clocks and pressure: how groups act and react over a campaign even without player input.
- Each faction's present-day identity and strategy (its history is the lore-master's).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- A faction's founding and historical record — owned by the [lore-master](../lore-master/); the faction-designer owns the live present, not the dated past.
- Where territory physically sits and what terrain permits — owned by the [cartographer](../cartographer/); factions claim, terrain constrains.
- Mechanical stat blocks and combat rules for faction forces — owned by [03-systems](../../03-systems/).
- How a campaign sequences faction beats — owned by [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/); the designer supplies the pressure, narrative paces it.

## Questions it always asks
- What does this faction want that it cannot easily get, and what will it spend to get it?
- Who has the opposite goal, and is that conflict reasonable on both sides rather than good-vs-evil?
- What does this faction do next week if the players never show up?
- Where is its leverage, and where is it exposed — what could the party threaten?
- Does this faction create choices for players, or just exist?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep faction motives consistent as the setting evolves; a faction may change tactics, not silently change what it is.
- Maintain faction clocks that let the world advance between sessions and between books.
- Seed renewable conflict so a line stays generative across many campaigns.
- Hand live political hooks to narrative and flag where faction action would contradict canon or terrain.
