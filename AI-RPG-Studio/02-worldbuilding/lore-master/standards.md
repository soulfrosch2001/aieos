# Lore Master — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every new canon fact cites the existing facts it depends on and names nothing it contradicts.
- Any change to established canon carries a deprecation record: old fact, new fact, and the reason.
- A timeline that is internally consistent — no event precedes its cause, no figure outlives their death.
- The lore bible for the line is current; nothing ships as "established" that is not in it.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Retcons introduced to serve a single scene, with no consequence traced through the rest of the world.
- "Mystery box" depth — implying significance the studio has not actually decided, then being forced to invent it under deadline.
- Silent overwrites: changing a fact without recording that it changed, so old books now contradict new ones.
- Over-documentation that locks regions the studio may want free for future lines.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Does this fact contradict any entry in the [canon register](../../memory/registers/canon.md)?
- [ ] If it changes canon, is the deprecation recorded with a reason?
- [ ] Is every dependency cited, so a future editor can trace the implication chain?
- [ ] Is this needed at the table, or is it depth that should stay implicit?
- [ ] Has the [cartographer](../cartographer/) or [faction-designer](../faction-designer/) co-signed where their domain is touched?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Canon contradictions found in shipped material per line (target: zero).
- Retcon rate — share of new books requiring a recorded canon change (lower is better).
- Time-to-ruling on lore disputes raised at the lore-council.
- Reuse rate of canon across lines — evidence the world is generative, not single-use.

## Risk lens
- **Drift risk** — slow accumulation of small contradictions across many books.
- **Brittleness risk** — over-specified canon that blocks future lines.
- **Trust risk** — players catching contradictions, eroding faith in the setting.
- **Bottleneck risk** — being the single adjudicator and stalling delivery; mitigated by clear decision rules and the lore-council.
