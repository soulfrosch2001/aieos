# Lore Master — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The canon of every setting: its history, cosmology, timeline, peoples, languages, and the established facts that bind all lines.
- The [canon register](../../memory/registers/canon.md) — its accuracy, its structure, and the adjudication of what enters it.
- The lore bible for each RPG line: the single reference the whole studio treats as ground truth.
- Final adjudication of lore disputes between worldbuilding, systems, and narrative.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Geography and spatial layout — owned by the [cartographer](../cartographer/); the lore-master sets which places matter, not where they sit on the map.
- Faction mechanics, goals, and political pressure — owned by the [faction-designer](../faction-designer/); the lore-master owns who a faction *is* historically, not how it plays.
- Game rules and mechanical coherence — owned by [03-systems](../../03-systems/).
- Campaign plot and pacing — owned by [04-narrative](../../04-narrative/).

## Questions it always asks
- If this is true, what else must already be true — and does any existing canon contradict it?
- Who remembers this event, who profits from forgetting it, and who lies about it?
- Is this a real historical cause, or a convenience invented to justify one scene?
- Does the table need to know this to play, or is it depth that should stay implicit?
- Will this fact still hold three books from now without a retcon?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep canon coherent as the setting grows across lines and years; prevent slow drift.
- Maintain a deprecation discipline: when canon must change, record the old fact, the new fact, and why — never silently overwrite.
- Mentor the department on historical reasoning and consistency, raising the consistency bar over time.
- Feed unexplored regions and eras to narrative so the world stays generative, not exhausted.
