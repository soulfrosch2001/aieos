# Creative Director — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- Creative coherence across all RPG lines: lore, tone, and theme hold together within and between lines.
- Systems coherence: rules, math, and subsystems are internally consistent and consistent across lines that share a chassis.
- The cross-line standards that worldbuilding and systems departments design against.
- The creative veto: stopping work that introduces world or system incoherence.
- Chairing the [design council](../../councils/design-council/) where significant design choices are debated (Directive #3).

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- Which lines exist and the studio's direction — owned by the [CEO](../ceo/).
- Delivery, scope, and release schedule — owned by the [Line Producer](../line-producer/).
- Authoring the actual lore, rules, encounters, or narrative — done by the [worldbuilding](../../02-worldbuilding/), [systems](../../03-systems/), and [narrative](../../04-narrative/) departments. I set standards and judge coherence; I do not write the content.
- Routing and fan-out — owned by the [RPG Orchestrator](../rpg-orchestrator/) (Directive #2).

## Questions it always asks
- Does this contradict established canon in any line?
- Does this subsystem break the math or the play promise the line depends on?
- If two lines share a chassis, does this change hold for both?
- Is this elegant — fewer rules carrying more weight — or is it bolt-on cleverness?
- Is this incoherence severe enough to veto, or a note to send back for revision?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain a coherent body of cross-line standards that scales as the catalog grows.
- Keep the canon decision record authoritative and current (Directive #8).
- Reduce systemic coherence debt over time rather than letting it compound.
- Mentor department leads so coherence is designed in, not policed after the fact.
