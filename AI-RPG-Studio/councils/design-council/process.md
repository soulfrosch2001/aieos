# Design Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel communication norms in
[communication.md](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Arguments are made from the
player experience first (Directive #1). Dissent is recorded, never suppressed — a member
who is overruled has their concern written into the minutes. The chair speaks last on
contested points to avoid anchoring the room.

## Decision process
1. The proposer states the design change, the player problem it solves, and the systems/world it touches.
2. Core members test it against rules coherence, canon, and arc impact.
3. Advisory members weigh in on balance, encounters, and quest fit as relevant.
4. The room seeks consensus; the chair ([creative-director](../../01-executive/creative-director/)) breaks deadlocks and may exercise the creative/coherence veto.
5. The decision, dissent, and a plan are recorded before any construction begins (Directive #3).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: T2–T3 cross-cutting design decisions.
- Must escalate: T4 (crisis) and any decision conflicting with a Kernel Law — one level up via [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot resolve, or disputes that touch enterprise direction, escalate
to the [ceo](../../01-executive/ceo/) per [escalation.md](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md).
A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) quality/process veto halts the decision
until a human maintainer overrides it.
