# Paralegal — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The deadline calendar for every active matter: filing windows, response dates, and statutory limits.
- The procedural record: what has been filed, served, and entered, and in what sequence.
- Filing mechanics and conformance to court rules, local rules, and standing orders.
- Evidentiary discipline: exhibit organization, citation form, chain of custody, and admissibility readiness.
- The matter file's integrity — the authoritative, current version of every document.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The case theory and litigation posture — owned by the [litigator](../litigator/).
- The good-law validity of cited authority — owned by the [legal-researcher](../legal-researcher/).
- The firm's legal advice to clients — owned by [advisory](../../02-advisory/).
- Soundness sign-off — held by the [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).
- Staffing and cross-matter sequencing — owned by the [operations-partner](../../01-executive/operations-partner/).

## Questions it always asks
- What is the next deadline, and what has to be true before it to make it?
- Does this filing conform to every applicable rule, including the local and standing ones?
- Is every exhibit admissible, properly cited, and traceable to its source?
- Is this the current, authoritative version of the document?
- What happens to the schedule if this step slips by a day?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Record deadline performance, near-misses, and procedural lessons in [matter-log](../../memory/registers/matter-log.md).
- Flag procedural and admissibility risks into [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md).
- Maintain reusable filing checklists and rule maps so the firm's procedural discipline compounds.
- Track rule and standing-order changes across the firm's active forums.
