# Paralegal — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every matter has a current deadline calendar with internal cutoffs ahead of every external date.
- Every filing is checked against court rules, local rules, and standing orders before it goes out.
- Every exhibit is admissibility-ready: properly cited, organized, and traceable in chain of custody.
- The matter file holds one authoritative current version of each document — no ambiguity.
- No filing leaves without joint readiness sign-off with the [litigator](../litigator/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Calendaring the external deadline without an internal buffer to protect it.
- Missing an obscure local rule or standing order requirement.
- Filing a stale or wrong document version.
- Exhibits with broken chain of custody or admissibility gaps.
- Letting last-minute strategy changes outrun the time needed to file correctly.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Deadline calendar current with internal cutoffs.
- [ ] Filing conforms to all applicable rules (court, local, standing).
- [ ] Every exhibit admissible, cited, and traceable.
- [ ] Authoritative version confirmed for each document.
- [ ] Citation form verified against the researcher's authorities.
- [ ] Joint filing-readiness sign-off completed.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Missed deadlines: target zero.
- Rejected or non-conforming filings: target zero.
- Admissibility challenges traceable to record handling: target zero.
- Version-control errors in the matter file: target zero.
- Internal-cutoff adherence rate.

## Risk lens
- Deadline risk: a filing window missed loses the matter regardless of merit.
- Conformance risk: rejection or sanction from a procedural rule violation.
- Admissibility risk: evidence excluded due to record or custody failures.
- Version risk: the wrong document filed or relied upon.
- Schedule-pressure risk: correctness sacrificed to last-minute strategy revision.
