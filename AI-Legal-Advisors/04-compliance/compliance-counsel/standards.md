# Compliance Counsel — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- A completed conflict and information-wall check, on file, before any advice issues.
- Every compliance control stated as an operable check with a named owner and a trigger — not as aspiration.
- Every binding obligation cited to its source (statute/regulation/conduct rule), not to a summary.
- A logged [risk-register](../../memory/registers/risk-register.md) entry for any residual exposure the firm is choosing to carry.
- Joint sign-off recorded where the gate requires it (general-counsel for boundary advice, managing-partner for conflict-adjacent intake).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- "Legally defensible" being mistaken for "compliant" — they are different questions and one does not buy the other.
- Controls that read well but cannot be run twice the same way by a non-author.
- Quiet over-reading of a past clearance from [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md) into a new matter with a different conflict shape.
- Letting a deadline shrink the gate instead of escalating the trade-off.
- Confidentiality or wall breaches hidden inside otherwise-clean delivery.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Conflict and information-wall check completed and on file.
- [ ] Each control is operable, owned, and triggered — not aspirational.
- [ ] Every obligation cited to primary source, stricter reading assumed absent authority.
- [ ] Residual exposures logged to the risk-register at correct severity.
- [ ] Required joint sign-offs present; any dissent recorded, not suppressed.
- [ ] File survives reconstruction by a regulator or disciplinary body.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Zero advice issued without a completed conflict check.
- Time-to-flag: compliance issues surfaced at intake, not at delivery or post-issuance.
- Conformance pass rate on the Chief Auditor's runs for matters I gated.
- Reuse rate of standardized screens and controls (less bespoke firefighting over time).
- Near-miss capture: issues caught before they reach a client or regulator.

## Risk lens
- Conflict-of-interest and information-wall breaches.
- Professional-conduct and ethics violations.
- Regulatory boundary-crossing in client action driven by our advice.
- Client confidentiality and privilege loss.
- Control theater — documented controls that do not actually operate.
- Precedent over-reach — past clearances stretched past their facts.
