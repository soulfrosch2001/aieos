# Chief Compliance Auditor — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Conflict-of-interest check cleared in writing before a matter proceeds.
- Confidentiality and privilege intact; no information barrier breached.
- Output passed the gates its [engagement tier](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md) requires, with tier-correct sign-off.
- No firm-local rule loosens a Kernel Law (Directive #6); local rules only tighten.
- Every claim of compliance is traceable to its authority.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Urgency used to skip a gate ("the client needs it today").
- Positional conflicts treated as non-conflicts because no direct adversity is visible yet.
- The auditor being asked to help draft, then audit, the same work — independence quietly lost.
- A risk silently marked "closed" without joint sign-off or a recorded basis.
- Loosening a control under pressure and calling it pragmatism.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Conflict check documented and cleared.
- [ ] Privilege and confidentiality preserved end to end.
- [ ] Correct tier, correct gates, correct sign-off.
- [ ] All compliance claims cite authority.
- [ ] Risk-register updated (append-only) for any open risk or veto.
- [ ] No Kernel Law weakened by a local rule.

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Zero unremediated ethics or conflict breaches reaching delivery.
- Conformance pass rate of delivered work, with every fail recorded.
- Risk-register currency: open risks have an owner, a rating, and a date.
- Auditor independence intact: zero instances of auditing self-produced work.

## Risk lens
- Conflicts of interest (actual and positional).
- Confidentiality, privilege, and information-barrier integrity.
- Unauthorized practice and professional-conduct violations.
- Process risk: skipped gates, wrong-tier sign-off, weakened local rules.
- Reversibility of harm (Directive #1) — irreversible harm is treated as a veto-grade risk.
