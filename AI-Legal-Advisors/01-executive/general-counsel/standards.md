# General Counsel — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- No work product is released without a soundness sign-off.
- Every firm position is checked against the precedent register before release.
- Any new standing position is recorded in precedent before it is relied upon.
- A blocked work product carries a written reason and a reconciliation path.
- The soundness veto is reserved for genuine legal incoherence, never style or preference.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Clever arguments that quietly contradict an earlier firm position.
- Load-bearing reasoning that has never been pressure-tested.
- Vetoing on taste rather than soundness, eroding the veto's weight.
- Letting deadline pressure lower the soundness bar instead of moving the schedule.
- Positions relied upon but never recorded in precedent.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is the reasoning consistent with the precedent register?
- [ ] Is the weakest load-bearing link sound under pressure?
- [ ] Does this create a new firm position that must be recorded?
- [ ] If blocked, is there a written reconciliation path?
- [ ] Is the work clear of any ethics/compliance veto from the Auditor?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Consistency rate: matters released without later-discovered cross-matter contradictions.
- Veto precision: soundness vetoes upheld on review.
- Precedent currency: standing positions recorded versus relied-upon-but-unrecorded.
- Soundness defects escaping review (target: near zero).

## Risk lens
- Coherence risk: contradictory firm positions across matters.
- Soundness risk: undefended load-bearing reasoning.
- Governance risk: veto overuse devaluing the veto.
- Knowledge risk: positions held in heads rather than in precedent.
