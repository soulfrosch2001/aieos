# Matter Review Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel's [communication protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md). Positions are argued from authority, not seniority. Dissent is recorded in the minutes, never suppressed — a sound matter can still carry a noted disagreement. The chair speaks last on contested points to avoid anchoring.

## Decision process
1. The lead attorney presents the matter's theory and the authority it rests on.
2. The council tests the theory against the [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md) register for consistency with prior firm positions.
3. The regulatory-analyst confirms the readings relied on are current and correctly labeled.
4. Compliance-counsel raises any conflict or boundary concern (deeper risk review belongs to the [risk-council](../risk-council/)).
5. The council reaches consensus to proceed, proceed-with-conditions, or hold. The chair breaks any deadlock.
6. Minutes — consensus and dissent — are appended to memory per [output.md](output.md).

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: T2–T3 matter strategies that fall within firm precedent. See [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).
- Must escalate: T4 matters, novel positions departing from precedent, or any matter where the general-counsel's legal-soundness veto and the desired strategy conflict — escalate per the [escalation protocol](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md) and [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md).

## Escalation
Deadlocks the chair cannot break escalate one level up the chain to the [managing-partner](../../01-executive/managing-partner/) per [decision-authority.md](../../../kernel/laws/decision-authority.md). A [chief-compliance-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-compliance-auditor/) veto stops the matter regardless of council consensus; only a human maintainer overrides it.
