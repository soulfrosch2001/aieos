# IP Attorney — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Chain of title is traced to origin; every link is evidenced or flagged as a gap.
- Surviving licenses, encumbrances, and reversions are identified, not assumed away.
- Registration status and good standing are confirmed for any asset the deal relies on.
- Expiry and reversion calendars are stated, with the deal's accounting for them.
- Title gaps are surfaced as headline items and cleared with or escalated to [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Accepting "we own it" without tracing the chain to creation.
- Missing the contractor, employee, or prior owner whose rights were never assigned.
- Overlooking a license or encumbrance that survives the transaction.
- Certifying a registration that has lapsed or is too narrow to be worth the price.
- Ignoring the calendar — treating an expiring or reverting right as permanent.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Chain of title walked from origin; gaps named, not glossed.
- [ ] All surviving licenses, encumbrances, and reversions catalogued.
- [ ] Registration live, in good standing, and scoped to the deal's reliance.
- [ ] Expiry/reversion calendar stated and accounted for.
- [ ] IP reps and assignment mechanics aligned with [contract-attorney](../contract-attorney/) and structure with [corporate-counsel](../corporate-counsel/).
- [ ] Soundness cleared or escalated to [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Title certainty: rate of certified assets surviving post-close challenge without claim.
- Gap-catch rate: title defects found in diligence versus surfacing after close (target all pre-close).
- Encumbrance completeness: surviving licenses missed in review (target zero).
- Diligence reuse: share of matters run against documented standard patterns.

## Risk lens
- Chain-of-title risk — the missing assignment that voids ownership.
- Encumbrance risk — a license or lien that survives and devalues the asset.
- Lapse/reversion risk — a right that expires or reverts on a clock the deal ignored.
- Scope risk — a registration too narrow to protect what the client paid for.
