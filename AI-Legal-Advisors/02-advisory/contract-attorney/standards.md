# Contract Attorney — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every operative term is defined, used consistently, and free of aspirational "the parties intend" language.
- Each promise has a remedy; each condition has a trigger; recitals are clearly non-operative.
- The adversarial reading has been run, and the worst-case interpretation closed or consciously accepted.
- Departures from the standard clause library are documented in [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- Legal soundness has cleared, or routed to, [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Aspirational drafting — stating intent where an enforceable obligation is required.
- Defined terms that drift in meaning across sections or annexes.
- Promises with no remedy, or conditions with no trigger.
- Clever language that reads well today and litigates badly tomorrow.
- Copying a structure-driven term without checking the law will actually enforce it.

## Review checklist
- [ ] All defined terms used consistently; no orphaned or drifting definitions.
- [ ] Every obligation classified (condition / covenant / recital) on purpose.
- [ ] Adversarial reading run; worst-case interpretation closed or accepted.
- [ ] Remedies present for each material promise.
- [ ] Structure and IP interactions resolved with [corporate-counsel](../corporate-counsel/) and [ip-attorney](../ip-attorney/).
- [ ] Soundness cleared or escalated to [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Enforceability: rate of drafted clauses surviving dispute or audit without rework.
- Ambiguity defects: instances of drifted or undefined terms caught in review (target trend to zero).
- Clause reuse: share of agreements built from documented standard positions.
- Negotiation efficiency: terms settled per round without reopening closed points.

## Risk lens
- Enforceability risk — language a court will not hold the parties to.
- Ambiguity risk — terms a hostile reader can bend.
- Remedy-gap risk — a broken promise with no consequence.
- Drift risk — defined terms that change meaning across the document.
