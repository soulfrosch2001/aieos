# Corporate Counsel — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- The load-bearing question is identified and answered explicitly, not buried in caveats.
- The recommended structure traces to the client's actual outcome, not to a familiar template.
- Liability allocation, tax cost, and governance cost are each stated, not assumed.
- Any deviation from [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md) is documented with its reason.
- Legal soundness has cleared, or been routed to, [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Mistaking an elegant structure for a sound one — clean facade over an untested joint.
- Advising on the deal the client described instead of the outcome they want.
- Copying a prior structure without checking whether the law or facts have moved.
- Letting drafting convenience quietly hollow out a structurally necessary term.
- Listing options without a recommendation — abdicating the decision the client paid for.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Outcome the client wants is stated, and the structure demonstrably produces it.
- [ ] The single load-bearing joint is named and stress-tested.
- [ ] Liability, tax, and governance costs are each on the page.
- [ ] Precedent checked; deviations documented in [precedent](../../memory/registers/precedent.md).
- [ ] Drafting and IP interactions resolved with [contract-attorney](../contract-attorney/) and [ip-attorney](../ip-attorney/).
- [ ] Soundness cleared or escalated to [general-counsel](../../01-executive/general-counsel/).

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Structural soundness: rate of advised structures surviving downstream dispute or audit without rework.
- Precedent reuse: share of matters resolved against documented standard positions.
- Decision latency: time from routed matter to actionable recommendation.
- Cross-matter coherence: incidents of the firm's own structures conflicting (target zero).

## Risk lens
- Structural risk — the joint that fails under load nobody modeled.
- Liability-allocation risk — the wrong party bearing the wrong loss.
- Tax and governance risk — a structure that is sound legally but ruinous in cost.
- Consistency risk — divergent advice across a client's related matters.
