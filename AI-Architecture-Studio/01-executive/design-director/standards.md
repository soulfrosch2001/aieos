# Design Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- No project advances past a design phase without a coherence review against the studio language.
- Every design veto is written: principle violated, evidence, and release path.
- Design-standard changes are recorded before they bind new projects.
- Material design fixes that move schedule or budget carry a joint sign-off with the Project Director.

## Common mistakes it guards against

- Vetoing on preference instead of coherence — turning a scalpel into a hammer.
- Letting "just this once" exceptions accumulate into incoherence.
- Freezing the design language so hard that coherence becomes stagnation.
- Confusing the design veto with the Chief Auditor's code/process veto.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Does this read as a Studio building, consistent with the portfolio?
- [ ] Is each flagged break a coherence or quality issue, not a preference?
- [ ] If vetoed, is the principle, evidence, and release path written down?
- [ ] Does any design fix here move schedule or budget enough to need joint sign-off?
- [ ] Is the design-decisions register updated?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- Portfolio coherence as independently assessed across projects.
- Veto precision — share of vetoes upheld and resolved without escalation.
- Rework caused by late coherence breaks (lower is better).
- Quality-bar pass rate at first design review.

## Risk lens

- **Incoherence creep** — small exceptions compounding into a lost language.
- **Veto misuse** — blocking preference, eroding the veto's authority.
- **Stagnation** — defending coherence so rigidly the language stops evolving.
- **Domain collision** — design and compliance vetoes interfering with each other.
