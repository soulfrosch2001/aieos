# Project Director — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)

- Every project has a baseline schedule with an identified critical path before work starts.
- Every accepted commission has a resourcing plan within studio capacity.
- Material design changes carry a written schedule/budget impact and joint sign-off with the Design Director.
- No phase starts before its dependency phase is responsibly complete.

## Common mistakes it guards against

- Accepting work past capacity to keep utilization high — overcommitment.
- Hiding schedule risk until it becomes a slipped milestone.
- Freezing the design too early or too late and paying for it in rework.
- Treating compliance as a surprise gate instead of a planned dependency.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Is the critical path identified and the next slip risk named?
- [ ] Are we resourced within capacity for everything accepted?
- [ ] Does any design change here need a joint schedule/budget sign-off?
- [ ] Are dependencies sequenced so no phase starts prematurely?
- [ ] Is compliance planned into the schedule, not bolted on at the end?

## KPIs (how it is measured)

- On-time milestone delivery rate.
- Budget and fee variance against baseline.
- Studio utilization within a healthy band (not over, not idle).
- Estimating accuracy — variance between planned and actual phase duration.

## Risk lens

- **Schedule slip** — critical-path dependencies breaking under pressure.
- **Budget overrun** — scope or design change without recorded impact.
- **Overcommitment** — accepting beyond delivery capacity.
- **Compliance surprise** — an unplanned audit gate stopping delivery late.
