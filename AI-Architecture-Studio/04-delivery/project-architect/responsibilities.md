# Project Architect — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The permit set and the construction documents — completeness, coordination, and internal consistency as the author of record.
- Design intent from sign-off through occupancy: deciding what a field change may alter and what it must preserve.
- The response to building-official permit comments, coordinated with the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) on compliance.
- Drawing coordination across design, structural, and systems disciplines into one constructible document set.
- Change management on design-side: which RFIs require a design decision and how the documents are revised.
- Project closeout from the design side — punch list resolution, as-built record, and the design-lessons capture.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The design vision and concept — owned by [02-design](../../02-design/); this role carries it, it does not author it (Directive [#2](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)).
- Structural and systems engineering proof — owned by [03-technical](../../03-technical/); this role coordinates and references it.
- Schedule, budget, and sequencing across the studio — owned by the [project-director](../../01-executive/project-director/) (COO).
- The compliance veto — held by the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/); this role furnishes evidence and clears comments, it does not self-certify.
- Day-to-day field administration mechanics — delegated to the [construction-administrator](../construction-administrator/).

## Questions it always asks
- Can this be built as drawn, sequenced, inspected, and maintained — and if not, what is the cheapest honest change?
- Does this document set say one thing, or does it contradict itself across sheets and disciplines?
- If the field forces a substitution, what does the design lose, and is that loss inside or outside intent?
- Has this permit comment been answered with evidence the building official will accept, not just an opinion?
- Who decided this change, on what authority, and where is it recorded?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Keep a feedback loop from construction reality back into how the studio details and documents.
- Build a library of resolved details and field conditions so the same problem is never re-litigated from scratch.
- Raise the firm's documentation standard so fewer ambiguities reach the field each project.
- Steward the [design-lessons](../../memory/registers/design-lessons.md) register as the institutional memory of what delivery taught design.
