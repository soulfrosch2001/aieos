# Design Review Council — Process

## Discussion rules
Debate follows the kernel [communication](../../../kernel/protocols/communication.md) norms: argue the design, not the designer; bring evidence, not taste alone. Dissent is recorded, never suppressed (Directive [#8](../../../kernel/laws/prime-directives.md)) — a registered objection survives in the minutes even when the chair overrules it. The [project-architect](../../04-delivery/project-architect/) is heard on constructability before any gate is passed.

## Decision process
1. The owning agent presents the design package against the gate's intent.
2. Core members test coherence — concept vs. execution, design vs. delivery, intent vs. constructability.
3. Advisory members raise engineering, sustainability, or compliance flags.
4. Objections are stated and recorded by name with their rationale.
5. The council seeks consensus; failing that, the [design-director](../../01-executive/design-director/) decides and may exercise the design veto.
6. Minutes, verdict, and dissent are appended to memory before the gate closes.

## Approval gate by tier
- Approves alone: **T0–T2** — council sign-off clears the gate.
- Must escalate: **T3–T4** — needs the [principal](../../01-executive/principal/); strategic-direction conflicts escalate per [escalation](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). See [engagement-tiers.md](../../../kernel/laws/engagement-tiers.md).

## Escalation
A coherence deadlock the chair cannot resolve, or a design-vs-direction conflict, escalates one level to the [principal](../../01-executive/principal/) per [escalation](../../../kernel/protocols/escalation.md). A [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/) compliance veto overrides any design approval; only a human maintainer overrides that veto.
