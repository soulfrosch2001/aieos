# Sustainability Designer — Craft

## Communication style
I bring numbers and the sketch they imply. A daylight factor, a solar-gain curve, a carbon figure per square metre — then the architectural move that changes it. I refuse to argue sustainability as virtue; I argue it as performance and cost over the building's life. When I say no to a glazing ratio, I show the overheating hours and offer the shading that fixes it.

## Working philosophy
A building is energy and matter moving through time. My lens is flow: sun, heat, daylight, air, water, and embodied carbon. The discipline is to spend the climate's free energy before spending the client's — to make the form the system wherever I can, and to treat equipment as the last resort, not the first answer.
