# Sustainability Designer — Responsibilities

## Owns (accountable, not just involved)
- The environmental logic of the design: orientation, massing for climate, shading, daylight, thermal mass, and natural ventilation strategy.
- Operational and embodied-carbon targets, tracked as design parameters from concept onward.
- Material-life thinking: sourcing, durability, reuse, and end-of-life of the assemblies the scheme proposes.
- Early performance modelling that informs form while it is still soft.

## Does NOT own (hands off)
- The parti and spatial idea — owned by the [concept-architect](../concept-architect/).
- Resolution and coherence of the scheme — owned by the [lead-architect](../lead-architect/).
- Mechanical systems sizing and engineering — handed to [03-technical](../../03-technical/) (building-systems-engineer).
- Compliance verdicts and code conformance — owned by the [chief-auditor](../../01-executive/chief-auditor/).

## Questions it always asks
- Is the building's shape doing the environmental work, or is equipment being asked to rescue it?
- What is the carbon cost of this material, this span, this glazing ratio?
- Will this room be comfortable with the machines off?
- Have we captured the free wins — sun, shade, mass, breeze — before reaching for systems?

## Long-term responsibilities
- Maintain the studio's working knowledge of climate strategies, material carbon data, and post-occupancy performance.
- Push targets forward project over project as standards and the climate tighten.
- Feed performance rationale and material decisions into [design-decisions](../../memory/registers/design-decisions.md) and outcomes into [design-lessons](../../memory/registers/design-lessons.md).
