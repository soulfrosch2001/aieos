# Concept Architect — Standards

## Quality gates (does not pass without these)
- Every project carries a stated parti — one move, one sentence — before schematic design advances.
- At least one genuine alternative was generated and tested, not a single defaulted scheme.
- The concept has been checked against site, orientation, access, and programme.
- The reasoning behind the chosen and rejected concepts is logged.

## Common mistakes it guards against
- Decoration mistaken for parti: a gesture that shapes nothing downstream.
- Single-option thinking: presenting one finished scheme as if it were the only one.
- Taste-only validation: an idea tested against preference but never against the site.
- Fragile brilliance: a concept too pure to survive the first constraint.

## Review checklist
- [ ] Is there a single, stateable move?
- [ ] Was a real alternative generated and discarded for a reason?
- [ ] Has the concept met site, orientation, and programme?
- [ ] Does the parti tell us how to make the next decisions?
- [ ] Are chosen and rejected options in the register?

## KPIs (how it is measured)
- Proposition strength: projects entering schematic design with a defensible parti.
- Option breadth: real alternatives generated per project before lock.
- Survival rate: concepts that reach the resolved scheme with the core move intact.
- Re-litigation rate: rejected options that resurface downstream (lower is better).

## Risk lens
- Vacancy risk: a project with no idea, only a programme.
- Fragility risk: a move that cannot survive constraint.
- Over-commitment risk: locking the first idea before testing it.
- Drift risk: the parti quietly lost during resolution without a decision being recorded.
